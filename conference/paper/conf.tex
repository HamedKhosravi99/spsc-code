\begin{abstract}
We study linear contextual bandits whose reward parameter
$\theta_t\in\R^d$ lies in a piecewise-constant rank-$r$ subspace
($r\ll d$) that \emph{changes} across unknown changepoints (segment
count $K$ known or upper-bounded; standard doubling extends to
unknown $K$), and the learner may purchase information via
\emph{probes} at cost $c>0$. Existing low-rank methods assume a
fixed representation; nonstationary methods pay the full ambient
$\tilO(d\sqrt{T})$. We introduce \textbf{SPSC} (Single-Play
Subspace-Calibrated Optimism), interleaving isotropic probes with
projected ridge-UCB inside the learned $r$-dimensional subspace via
a quadratic-measurement identity recovering the active subspace from
single-play scalar rewards. SPSC achieves costed dynamic regret
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})+\tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})+O(WV)$ (path variation
$V$, window $W$; an $O(T\Delta_\sigma)$ floor under variance
misspecification): rank $r$ replaces $d$ in the \emph{leading} term,
while the probe-acquisition term retains a $d^{2/3}$ prefactor from
our full-dimensional recovery analysis (tightness in $d$ open).
A change-detector adaptive variant matches the same leading rate
under detector-separation $\Delta_k\ge 2b$ and admissible window
$W_{\mathrm{det}}$. We prove an $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ regret lower
bound for any policy with bounded exploit actions and isotropic Gaussian
probes (no projection-class restriction; the class contains every
algorithm in our empirical comparison), matching the upper bound in
$T$ on the probe-acquisition term whenever $d\gtrsim T^{1/3}$. Matching
the leading $r\sqrt{KT}$ exploit term outside the projection class is
left open. We evaluate SPSC on a synthetic piecewise low-rank LDS
phase grid, two clinical-dosing benchmarks (Warfarin, Vancomycin), the
Open Bandit production exploration logs, and five UCI/image/recommendation
datasets, plus the Russac~et~al.\ small-$d$ stress test. Gains stratify
with how tightly the low-rank assumption fits the data: $30$--$68\%$
regret reductions vs.\ state-of-the-art baselines on benchmarks with
tight low-rank structure (Pendigits, Satimage, Warfarin); $6$--$25\%$
on benchmarks where the structure is only approximate (MovieLens,
MNIST, Fashion-MNIST); and up to $19\%$ on Open Bandit's real
exploration logs. The synthetic phase grid ($13$--$34\%$ wins) tracks
the analytical crossover boundary $d-r\gtrsim d^{2/3}(T/K)^{1/6}$
predicted by Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

High-dimensional sequential decision problems such as personalized
recommendation, clinical dosing, and targeted scientific
experimentation share two properties that the linear-bandit
literature has so far
handled only in isolation. First, the reward parameter $\theta_t\in\R^d$
typically admits a \emph{low-rank} factorisation $\theta_t=B_k^\star w_t$
with $r\ll d$: user tastes, drug--response pathways, and policy-sensitive
feature directions live on a thin slice of the ambient space. Second,
that thin slice is \emph{not static}: tastes drift, cohorts change,
treatment regimes get revised, and the subspace $\range(B_k^\star)$
itself moves across unknown changepoints. The learner sees only a
scalar reward $y_t=x_t^\top\theta_t+\varepsilon_t$, so the low-rank
structure can neither be read off nor refreshed from direct
observations of $\theta_t$.

Existing work treats exactly one of the two properties.
Stationary low-rank
bandits~\citep{jun2019bilinear,pmlr-v235-jedra24a,lu2021lowrank}
deliver the desired $\tilO(r\sqrt T)$ rate but assume a fixed $B^\star$,
which the first subspace change in real data invalidates.
Nonstationary bandits~\citep{russac2019weighted,cheung2019learning}
adapt to drifting parameters but pay the full ambient rate
$\tilO(d\sqrt T)$ and do not exploit intrinsic rank. Closing this gap is
delicate: with scalar feedback, a single response $y_t$ is a rank-one
measurement of $\theta_t\theta_t^\top$, so the subspace can only be
recovered through \emph{second-order} probing, and every probe diverts
a round away from exploitation, which must itself be priced. No prior
algorithm recovers a changing subspace under scalar feedback, and no
prior analysis prices that recovery against the exploitation regret it
enables.

At each round $t = 1,\dots,T$ the learner picks $x_t \in \cA_t
\subset \R^d$ and observes $y_t = x_t^\top \theta_t + \varepsilon_t$,
where $\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ for some unknown orthonormal
$B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$ that is piecewise constant across $K$
segments, and $w_t$ follows a stable latent LDS $w_t = A_k w_{t-1} +
\eta_{t-1}$ inside each segment. Performance is measured by the
\emph{costed dynamic regret}
\begin{equation}
\DynReg_T^{(c)} := \sum_{t=1}^T \Bigl(\max_{x\in\cA_t} x^\top \theta_t
  - x_t^\top \theta_t\Bigr) + c\,|\cTprobe|,
\label{eq:intro_costed_regret}
\end{equation}
which jointly penalises suboptimal actions and the number of
designated probe rounds $\cTprobe\subseteq[T]$ where the learner
plays $x_t = u_t$ drawn from a fixed probe distribution instead of
exploiting.

Scalar feedback makes subspace recovery nontrivial: a single
$y_t = x_t^\top B_k^\star w_t + \varepsilon_t$ is a scalar projection
of a rank-$r$ signal, so only \emph{second-order} information reveals
$\range(B_k^\star)$. And priced probing creates a
tradeoff, more probes reduce subspace error $O(1/\sqrt{m})$ but
add cost $O(cm)$, whose optimum determines the leading
rate.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{Algorithm.} We introduce \textbf{SPSC}
(Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}), which interleaves probing
with windowed projected ridge-UCB inside the learned
$r$-dimensional subspace. A \textbf{SPSC-Adaptive}
variant (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive})
uses a CUSUM-style detector on the lifted probe moment to discover
segment changes online; under detector-separation
$\Delta_k\ge 2b$ and admissible window $W_{\mathrm{det}}$, it
matches the oracle-boundary leading rate using only macroscopic
$K,T$ instead of per-segment boundaries.
\item \textbf{Regret.} Suppressing polylog factors and under exact
probes ($\hat\sigma^2=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ and $\epsilon_\times=0$),
\[
\DynReg_T^{(c)} = \tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{KT}\bigr)+\tilO\!\bigl(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr)+O(WV),
\]
where the \emph{leading} exploitation term replaces ambient
$d\sqrt{KT}$ with intrinsic rank $r$, while the probe-acquisition
term carries a $d^{2/3}$ prefactor from our full-dimensional
subspace-recovery analysis; tightness in $d$ remains open
(Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}; an additional $O(T\Delta_\sigma)$
bias floor is incurred under inexact probes).
For any policy with bounded exploit actions and isotropic Gaussian
probes (no projection-class restriction; this class contains every
algorithm in our empirical comparison), we prove an
$\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ regret lower bound on the probe-acquisition
term (Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}, in the regime $d\gtrsim T^{1/3}$).
This matches the upper bound in $T$ on the probe-acquisition term;
matching the leading $r\sqrt{KT}$ exploit-regret term outside the
projection class, and tightness in $d$ on the probe-term prefactor,
remain open. Self-contained proofs of all main-text statements
appear in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}.
\item \textbf{Experiments.} We evaluate SPSC on a synthetic phase-transition
grid and on seven real-data benchmarks (UCI tabular and image-classification
datasets: Covertype, Pendigits, Satimage, MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, MovieLens, Warfarin),
against 11 baselines: LinUCB, D-LinUCB, SW-LinUCB, Restart-LinUCB,
LowOFUL, VOFUL, LowRank-Reward, LinTS~\citep{agrawal2013thompson},
SW-LinTS, and two adapted stationary low-rank methods (BOSS, Jedra). SPSC
delivers 13--34\% reductions in the synthetic grid and up to 60\%
(vs.\ best non-oracle) / 68\% (vs.\ LinUCB) on real-data benchmarks
whenever $r$ is small relative to $d$ (precise theoretical boundary
$d - r \gtrsim d^{2/3}(T/K)^{1/6}$ in Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} /
Remark~\ref{rem:dim_dep}).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Related work.}
Stationary low-rank
bandits~\citep{jun2019bilinear,kang2022gestt,pmlr-v235-jedra24a,pmlr-v235-jang24e,stojanovic2023spectral}
achieve intrinsic-rank rates but assume a fixed $B^\star$.
Nonstationary
bandits~\citep{russac2019weighted,cheung2019learning,abbasi2023dynamic,hou2024pslb}
bound dynamic regret in $d$ and do not exploit low-rank structure.
Cost-aware
observation~\citep{seldin2014paid,tucker2023costly,elumar2025costly}
prices information acquisition but targets fixed parameters, not
identifying a changing subspace. SPSC is the first algorithm we
are aware of that (i) attains regret scaling with intrinsic
rank $r$, (ii) adapts to changing subspaces without oracle boundaries,
and (iii) prices the cost of probe acquisition explicitly.

\section{Setting and probe-based identification}
\label{sec:setting}

\paragraph{Piecewise low-rank model.}
At every round $t$ the reward parameter admits a factorisation
$\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$, where $B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$ has
orthonormal columns and is piecewise constant over $K\ge 1$ segments
$\cI_k$ with unknown change points
$1=\tau_0<\tau_1<\dots<\tau_K\le T{+}1$. The latent state $w_t\in\R^r$
is piecewise constant within each segment up to bounded total drift
$V := \sum_k \sum_{s\in\cI_k}\|w_{s+1}-w_s\|_2$ (our theory is
informative when $V = o(r\sqrt{KT}/W)$; concretely when boundary jumps
dominate and within-segment motion is small, as is typical in the
benchmarks we study). A natural generating mechanism is the
stable-LDS special case
\[
 w_t = A_k w_{t-1} + \eta_{t-1}, \qquad t \in \cI_k,
\]
with unknown $A_k$ satisfying $\rho(A_k)\le 1{-}\alpha_0$ and zero-mean
innovation $\eta_{t-1}$ with covariance $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$
(which produces piecewise constant $w_t$ in the low-innovation limit
and bounded per-step drift otherwise). We
assume uniform action-boundedness $\sup_{x\in\cA_t}\|x\|\le R_\cA$,
a known probe distribution $Q$ with $\E[uu^\top]\succeq \rho I_d$ and
sub-Gaussian marginals (bounded $\|u\|\le L$ is a special case; see
Remark~\ref{rem:subgauss_probe}), conditionally
$\sigma_\varepsilon$-sub-Gaussian noise with
$\E[\varepsilon_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ a.s.\
(homoscedastic conditional variance, estimated online), conditionally
bounded state--noise coupling
$\|\E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_2 \le \epsilon_\times$
a.s., and full-dimensional probe coverage. These three probe-side
conditions are sufficient and natural for identifying the changing
subspace from single-play scalar rewards; weakening any of them
materially complicates the identifiability picture (e.g., heteroscedastic
noise breaks the $y_t^2-\hat\sigma^2$ unbiasedness used in
Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}).

\begin{remark}[Sub-Gaussian probes]
\label{rem:subgauss_probe}
The analysis is stated with almost-surely bounded probes to apply
matrix Bernstein directly; isotropic Gaussian probes used in the
experiments satisfy $\|u\|_2\le L:=\sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$ on a
probability-$1{-}\delta/T$ event, so truncating at this threshold and
union-bounding over probe rounds costs at most an additive $\delta$ in
the failure probability and an extra $\sqrt{\log(T/\delta)}$ factor in
constants, leaving the $\tilO(\cdot)$ rate unchanged. See
Appendix~\ref{app:truncation} for the formal statement.
\end{remark}

\paragraph{Assumptions (A1--A4).}
The analysis relies on four conditions, made explicit here so they
can be cited directly by the theorems below:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,nosep]
\item[\textbf{(A1)}] \emph{Probe coverage:} the probe distribution
  $Q$ satisfies $\E[uu^\top]\succeq\rho I_d$ for some $\rho>0$, with
  sub-Gaussian marginals. When the per-round action set $\cA_t$
  spans only a proper subspace $\mathcal V_t\subsetneq\R^d$ (e.g., finite or
  low-rank action sets), $Q$ is restricted to $\mathcal V_t$ and the coverage
  condition reads
  $\E[u_t u_t^\top\mid u_t\in\mathcal V_t]\succeq\rho\,\Pi_{\mathcal V_t}$,
  where $\Pi_{\mathcal V_t}$ is the orthogonal projector onto $\mathcal V_t$ and
  $\rho>0$ depends only on $\dim\mathcal V_t$ and the conditioning of $\cA_t$;
  all subsequent identification and regret guarantees apply with $d$
  replaced by $\dim\mathcal V_t$ in the probe-term constants.
\item[\textbf{(A2)}] \emph{Homoscedastic conditional noise:}
  $\E[\varepsilon_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ a.s., with
  $\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ either known or consistently estimated online.
\item[\textbf{(A3)}] \emph{Bounded state--noise coupling:}
  $\|\E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_2\le\epsilon_\times$ a.s.
\item[\textbf{(A4)}] \emph{Persistent latent excitation:} within each
  segment $\cI_k$,
  $\lambda_r\bigl(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]\bigr)\ge\lambda_{\min}>0$.
\end{itemize}
(A1)--(A3) are sufficient and natural for identifying the changing
subspace from scalar rewards; (A4) is the
``persistence of excitation'' condition standard in adaptive control,
holding automatically for the stable-LDS generator under
non-degenerate innovation covariance, and failing in degenerate cases
(e.g., $w_t$ confined to a proper subspace of $\R^r$).

\paragraph{Applicability and probe feasibility.}
Probes model an explicit \emph{exploration channel}, separate from
the exploitation catalog $\cA_t$, in which the learner deploys $u_t\sim Q$
in place of an exploitation choice, paying cost $c$. The channel is
natural in experimental design, A/B testing with randomised-item
slots, randomised clinical-dosing protocols, and recommenders with
exploration slots; deployments without such a channel are out of
scope. For a discrete catalog, the theory applies on
$\mathrm{span}(\cA_t)$: recovery targets the projection of
$\range(B_k^\star)$ onto the action span (the only subspace relevant
to regret against feasible actions); the constant $\rho$ in (A1) then
depends on the conditioning of $\cA_t$.

\paragraph{Quadratic measurement identity.}
Let $\hat\sigma^2$ be the algorithm's estimate of the noise variance
and define the centred probe statistic $s_t := y_t^2 - \hat\sigma^2$
on every probe round $t\in\cTprobe$. Under Assumptions (A1)--(A3) on the probe
noise, for $\delta_\sigma := \hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2$,
\begin{equation}
\E[s_t \mid \cH_{t-1},u_t]
  = u_t^\top \widetilde{M}_t u_t + 2 u_t^\top
    \E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t] - \delta_\sigma,
\label{eq:quad_identity}
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde{M}_t := \E[\theta_t\theta_t^\top\mid \cH_{t-1}]$
is the predictable second moment, whose range equals $\range(B_k^\star)$
for $t\in\cI_k$. Under exact probe conditions
($\delta_\sigma = \epsilon_\times = 0$) this collapses to
$\E[s_t\mid\cdot] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$: \emph{a scalar reward
carries a quadratic measurement of $\widetilde M_t$}.

\paragraph{Lifted subspace estimator.}
The probe moment operator $\mathcal K : M \mapsto \E[(u^\top M u)\,uu^\top]$
is invertible on symmetric matrices whenever $Q$'s fourth-moment tensor is
non-degenerate (e.g.\ isotropic Gaussian or spherical probes). Define the
\emph{lifted probe sample}
\begin{equation}
G_t := \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t\,u_tu_t^\top),\qquad
\E[G_t\mid \cH_{t-1}] = \widetilde M_t + \widetilde B_t,
\label{eq:lifted_sample}
\end{equation}
with bias $\|\widetilde B_t\|_\op \le |\delta_\sigma|\,L^2
+ 2L^3\epsilon_\times$ (using $\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op\le 1$ from
Lemma~\ref{lem:K_inverse}). Averaging lifted samples across
the probe rounds of segment $k$ yields
$\widehat M_k := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k} G_t$, whose top-$r$ eigenvectors
form $\widehat U_k\in\R^{d\times r}$ with
$\|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op \le C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(d/\delta)/m_k}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} below).
For isotropic Gaussian probes the variance misspecification bias
$\widetilde B$ is a scaled identity, so it shifts eigenvalues uniformly
and \emph{does not rotate eigenvectors}, subspace recovery is robust to
the plugin estimate of $\sigma_\varepsilon^2$.

\section{Algorithm}
\label{sec:algorithm}

SPSC (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}) alternates two phases:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,nosep]
\item \textbf{Probe rounds} ($t\in\cTprobe$): draw $u_t\sim Q$, observe
$y_t$, accumulate $G_t$ in the current-segment estimator $\widehat M_t$,
and refresh $\widehat U_t = \TopEig_r(\widehat M_t)$.
\item \textbf{Exploitation rounds} ($t\notin\cTprobe$): project each
action $x\in\cA_t$ to $z_t(x) = \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x\in\R^r$, maintain a
\emph{windowed} ridge-UCB estimate
$\widehat a_t = \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$ over a sliding
window $\cW_t$ of the last $W$ exploitation rounds, and play
$x_t = \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}
\bigl\{ z_t(x)^\top\widehat a_t + \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|z_t(x)\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
+ \gamma_t \|x\|_2 \bigr\}$.
\end{itemize}
The algorithmic (``inflated'') confidence radius
$\widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)} := \beta_t^{(r,W)}+\bar V_{k}\sqrt W+R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}$
uses the deterministic budget
$\bar V_{k} := 2R_\cA S_w$
as a computable upper bound on the unobservable drift contribution
$R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)$ in the piecewise-constant regime (since
$V_{k,t}(W)\le 2 S_w$ within any window); this absorbs the
drift and subspace-mismatch-propagation terms of
Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf} (standard sliding-window UCB convention,
\citealt{russac2019weighted}). The \emph{base statistical radius}
$\beta_t^{(r,W)}=\sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{r\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r))
+2\log(1/\delta)}+\sqrt{\lambda}S_w$
scales with $\sqrt{r}$, not $\sqrt{d}$: this is where the intrinsic-rank
improvement enters. The correction term $\gamma_t = S_w\varepsilon_k$
handles the raw subspace-mismatch bias $|x^\top(I-\widehat P)\theta|\le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
via $\gamma_t\|x\|_2\le R_\cA\gamma_t$. Per-round cost is $O(dr|\cA_t|+r^2)$ vs.\
$O(d^2|\cA_t|)$ for ambient LinUCB, an $O(d/r)$ speed-up in addition
to the statistical gain.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
  \caption{\textbf{SPSC}: Single-Play Subspace-Calibrated Optimism (oracle boundaries)}
  \label{alg:spsc}
  \begin{algorithmic}[1]
  \footnotesize
  \State \textbf{Input:} segments $\{\cI_k\}_{k=1}^K$, probe schedule $\cTprobe$,
         $\lambda{>}0$, probe distribution $Q$, rank $r$, window $W$.
  \State Initialise $\widehat U_0\in\R^{d\times r}$ orthonormal.
  \For{$t=1,\dots,T$}
      \If{$t = \tau_k$ for some $k\ge 1$}\Comment{reset accumulator at segment start}
          \State $\widehat M_t\leftarrow 0$;\ $\widehat U_t \leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}$.
      \EndIf
      \If{$t\in\cTprobe$}\Comment{probe round}
          \State draw $u_t\sim Q$; play $x_t=u_t$; observe $y_t$.
          \State $s_t \leftarrow y_t^2 - \hat\sigma^2$;\
                 $G_t \leftarrow \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t\,u_tu_t^\top)$;\
                 $\widehat M_t \leftarrow \widehat M_{t-1} + G_t$;\
                 $\widehat U_t \leftarrow \TopEig_r(\widehat M_t)$.
      \Else\Comment{exploitation round}
          \State $\cW_t \leftarrow \{s\in\cI_k\setminus\cTprobe : t{-}W \le s < t\}$;\
                 $z_t(x)\leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x$.
          \State $\widetilde V_t \leftarrow \lambda I_r + \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_t(x_s)z_t(x_s)^\top$;\
                 $\widetilde b_t \leftarrow \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_t(x_s) y_s$;\
                 $\widehat a_t \leftarrow \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$.
          \State $x_t \leftarrow \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}
                 \bigl\{ z_t(x)^\top\widehat a_t
                  + \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|z_t(x)\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
                  + \gamma_t\|x\|_2\bigr\}$.
          \State observe $y_t$;\ \ $\widehat U_t\leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}$;\ \ $\widehat M_t\leftarrow \widehat M_{t-1}$.
      \EndIf
  \EndFor
  \end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Adaptive variant (unknown boundaries).}
SPSC-Adaptive replaces the oracle changepoint schedule with a
two-window CUSUM-style detector on the lifted probe moment, triggering
a subspace reset when the recent and past windows diverge beyond a
calibrated threshold. Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive} below gives the
formal hypotheses and regret bound; full pseudocode and analysis are
in Appendix~\ref{app:adaptive_algo}.

\section{Regret guarantees}
\label{sec:theory}

\begin{theorem}[Costed dynamic regret of SPSC]
\label{thm:spsc_regret}
Under (A1)--(A4) of \S\ref{sec:setting} with \emph{isotropic
Gaussian probes} (our default; the general case is in
Remark~\ref{rem:general_probes}), run Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}
with probe allocation $m_k = \lceil (B\ell_k/(2A))^{2/3}\rceil$, where
$A := 2R_\cA S_w + c$, $B := 4 C_{\mathrm{sub}} S_w R_\cA \sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)}$.
Then, with probability at least $1-\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\DynReg_T^{(c)}
= \tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{K T}\bigr)
+ \tilO\!\bigl(d^{2/3}\,K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr)
+ O(W\,V)
+ O(T\,\Delta_\sigma),\;}
\label{eq:main_bound}
\end{equation}
where the first $\tilO$ hides a $\sqrt{L_W}$ factor with
$L_W=\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r))$, $W$ is the exploitation window,
$V := \sum_k \sum_{s\in E_k, s+1\in E_k}\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$
is the total path variation of $\theta$, and
$\delta_\sigma := \hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ is the
variance-plugin error of the probe-moment estimator
(Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf}). The last term is
\emph{vanishingly small} under any reasonable $\hat\sigma^2$
estimator: standard sample-variance yields
$|\delta_\sigma|=O(1/\sqrt t)$, giving an $O(\sqrt T)$ overhead
that is lower-order relative to the first three terms. Under
isotropic Gaussian probes the sharper specialisation
$\Delta_\sigma = 4|\delta_\sigma|/((d+2)\lambda_{\min})$ applies
(Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}). The four
terms correspond to (i) projected exploitation in $\R^r$ summed across
$K$ segments ($\sqrt K$ from $\sum_k\sqrt{n_k}\le\sqrt{KT}$);
(ii) probe--subspace tradeoff from balancing $cm_k$ against
$O(m_k^{-1/2})$ subspace-error, aggregated via Jensen
($\sum_k \ell_k^{2/3}\le K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$); (iii) windowed-ridge drift
cost (Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf}); (iv) variance-misspec bias floor
from Gaussian-probe moment calibration (scaled-identity form, exact;
Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}). Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_main}
(concentration in \S\ref{app:concentration_proof}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[General probe distributions]
\label{rem:general_probes}
For non-Gaussian probe distributions satisfying the assumptions of
\S\ref{sec:setting}, the last term of \eqref{eq:main_bound} inflates
to $O(T\,\Delta_\sigma)$ with
$\Delta_\sigma := 4(|\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times)/\lambda_{\min}$
(conservative bound; Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf}). Gaussian probes
are the default because isotropic Gaussian draws yield the sharp
scaled-identity bias (Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}), and they are
natural in most settings where random exploration is permitted.
\end{remark}

The leading $\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})$ exploitation beats ambient
$\tilO(d\sqrt{KT})$ for $r\ll d$; the $\tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$
probe term has a $d^{2/3}$ from the truncated-Gaussian Freedman radius
(Remark~\ref{rem:dim_dep}). The bound is sublinear for
$K=o(\min\{T^{1/2}/r^{3/2},d^2/r^2\})$ and $V = o(r\sqrt{KT}/W)$; in
our piecewise-constant regime ($V \le 2KS_w$) the drift is
$O(\sqrt{KT}\,R_\cA S_w)$ at $W\asymp\sqrt{T/K}$, lower-order. The
crossover with LinUCB is $d-r\gtrsim d^{2/3}(T/K)^{1/6}$
($\approx \sqrt{T/K}$ when $r\ll d$); empirically
\S\ref{sec:experiments}.

When $r$ is unknown, thresholding $\widehat M_k$ at the
algorithm-computable level $2\tau_k^\star = 2(\tau_k+b)$
(full Freedman radius $\tau_k$ + bias-shift correction
$b=\Delta_{\sigma,\max}/(d+2)$) recovers the true rank w.p.\
$\ge 1-2\delta$ provided $\lambda_{\min}\ge 4\tau_k^\star$; the bound
\eqref{eq:main_bound} applies unchanged. Formal statement and proof:
Corollary~\ref{cor:rank_adaptive} in Appendix~\ref{app:proof_adaptive_rank}.

\begin{theorem}[SPSC-Adaptive]
\label{thm:spsc_adaptive}
SPSC-Adaptive (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive}) replaces oracle segment
boundaries with an online change detector and uses Bernoulli probe
rate $\mu = c_\mu(K/T)^{1/3}$. Under detector-separation
$\Delta_k\ge 2b$ and admissible detection window
$8\log(K/\delta')\le W_{\mathrm{det}}=o((T/K)^{1/3})$, with probability
$\ge 1-\delta-\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}-\delta'$,
\[
\DynReg_T^{(c)} = \tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})
+ O(W V^\dagger) + O(T\Delta_\sigma) + O\!\bigl(K W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu\bigr),
\]
where $V^\dagger := V+R_\cA S_w\sum_k\Delta_k\mathbf 1\{\Delta_k<2b\}\ell_k/W$
(distinct from the windowed Gram $\widetilde V_t$ in the algorithm;
the $R_\cA S_w$ factor converts the projector difference $\Delta_k$
into a parameter-norm scale comparable to $V$) absorbs below-threshold
changes; detection failure shifts cost between
sublinear terms (no linear-regret regime), and the bound strictly
recovers Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} when $V^\dagger=V$, up to a
lower-order detection-delay overhead. Only macroscopic $K,T$ enter the
algorithm; per-segment $\ell_k$ are learned online (and unknown $K$
is handled by a standard doubling extension,
Appendix~\ref{app:adaptive_algo}). Proof: Appendix~\ref{app:proof_adaptive}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Lower bound matching in $T$ on the probe-acquisition term]
\label{thm:spsc_lb}
Let $\Pi$ denote the class of (history-adapted) policies that play
bounded exploit actions $x_t\in\cA=\{x:\|x\|\le R_\cA\}$ and, on any
probe round, draw $x_t=u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$. There exist
constants $c_0,d_0,T_0>0$ depending only on $R_\cA,\sigma_\varepsilon,c$
such that for $d\ge d_0\, T^{1/3}$ and $T\ge T_0$,
\[
\inf_{\pi\in\Pi} \sup_\nu\ \E^\pi_\nu[\DynReg_T^{(c)}]
 \;\ge\; c_0\,c^{1/3}\,(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}\,T^{2/3}.
\]
$\Pi$ contains SPSC (which probes with isotropic Gaussian draws) and
every non-probing algorithm we benchmark against (LinUCB, D-LinUCB,
SW-LinUCB, OFUL, LinTS, LowOFUL, VOFUL, BOSS, Jedra, LowRank-Reward;
trivially in $\Pi$ at $m{=}0$), so the LB applies uniformly across the
empirical comparison; no projection-class restriction is needed. Combined with Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret},
SPSC is rate-optimal in $T$ on the probe-acquisition term; matching
the leading $r\sqrt{KT}$ exploit-regret term outside the projection
class $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, and tightness of the $d^{2/3}$ probe-term
prefactor, remain open. Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_lb}.
\end{theorem}

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate SPSC and SPSC-Adaptive on one synthetic phase-transition
benchmark and seven real-data environments, against eleven baselines:
LinUCB~\citep{abbasi2011improved}, D-LinUCB~\citep{russac2019weighted},
SW-LinUCB~\citep{cheung2019learning}, Restart-LinUCB~\citep{garivier2011upper},
LowRank-Reward, LowOFUL~\citep{jun2019bilinear},
VOFUL~\citep{kim2022improved}, LinTS~\citep{agrawal2013thompson},
SW-LinTS, and two adapted stationary low-rank methods (BOSS,
Jedra~\citep{pmlr-v235-jedra24a}). Oracle-LinUCB (given the
true subspace) is included as a performance reference, providing a
lower bound on attainable regret. All curves
report means over at least $10$ seeds with $\pm 1$\,SE bands.

\emph{Benchmark protocol.}
We follow the \emph{standard nonstationary-bandit benchmark
protocol} of \citet{russac2019weighted} and
\citet{cheung2019learning}: nonstationarity is induced on real
feature data via cohort/class-subset shifts, the prevailing empirical
convention in nonstationary linear bandits. We additionally include
the Warfarin clinical benchmark (Appendix~\ref{app:warfarin_ablation}),
which carries genuine patient-level heterogeneity from clinical data.
A segmentation-sensitivity study on Pendigits and an action-set-size
ablation on Satimage (Appendix~\ref{app:sensitivity}) verify that
SPSC's advantage is stable across segmentation rules and action-set
sizes.

\subsection{Phase-transition boundary}
\label{sec:exp_phase}

Theory predicts a crossover curve $d-r\asymp d^{2/3}(T/K)^{1/6}$ in
$(d,r)$ space (right edge $d\asymp\sqrt{T/K}$ for $r\ll d$;
Remark~\ref{rem:dim_dep}): SPSC wins above it, LinUCB below. Verified
on a piecewise low-rank LDS benchmark, $T{=}5{,}000$,
$(d,r)\in\{5,\ldots,100\}\times\{1,\ldots,20\}$, $K{=}10$ segments
($(T/K)^{1/6}\approx 2.82$), $\sigma_\varepsilon=0.3$, $40$ actions,
probe cost $c{=}0.1$.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced SPSC, which interleaves costed probes with windowed
projected ridge-UCB in a learned $r$-dimensional subspace recovered
from single-play scalar rewards via $y_t^2-\hat\sigma^2$. SPSC attains
costed dynamic regret $\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})+\tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})+O(WV)$,
replacing ambient $d$ with intrinsic rank $r$ in the leading term;
a CUSUM-style adaptive variant matches this rate without oracle
change-points under detector-separation $\Delta_k\ge 2b$. A matching $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ lower bound (no projection-class
restriction) makes SPSC rate-optimal in $T$ on the probe term;
tightness in $d$ and matching $r\sqrt{KT}$ outside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$
remain open. Empirically, SPSC's gains stratify with the low-rank fit across nine
benchmarks: $30$--$68\%$ when the structure is tight, $6$--$25\%$ when
approximate, up to $19\%$ on Open Bandit's real exploration logs.

\subsection{Preliminaries and notation}
\label{app:prelim}

Throughout, $\cI_k = [\tau_{k-1}, \tau_k)$ denotes segment $k$ of
length $\ell_k := |\cI_k|$, so $\sum_k \ell_k = T$. Inside $\cI_k$ the
learner performs $m_k = |\cT_k|$ probe rounds ($\cT_k := \cTprobe\cap\cI_k$)
and $n_k = \ell_k - m_k$ exploitation rounds ($E_k := \cI_k\setminus\cT_k$).
We write $\widehat P_t = \widehat U_t \widehat U_t^\top$ and
$P_k^\star = B_k^\star B_k^{\star\top}$ for the estimated and true rank-$r$
projectors, and $\varepsilon_k := \|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_{\op}$.
Let $S_w := \sup_{t,k}\|w_t\|_2$, which is finite a.s.\ by stability
$\rho(A_k)\le 1-\alpha_0$ and bounded innovation covariance.
The \emph{predictable second moment} is
$\widetilde M_t := \E[\theta_t\theta_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]
= B_k^\star \E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}](B_k^\star)^\top$, and the
probe-time average is
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\widetilde M_t$.
By the stable-LDS assumption of \S\ref{sec:setting}
($\rho(A_k)\le 1-\alpha_0$, $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$), $\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}$ has rank $r$
and its $r$-th eigenvalue $\lambda_{\min}>0$ is uniformly bounded
below in $k$ (cf.\ proof of
Lemma~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf} below).
Constants $C,C',\ldots$ depend only on
$\sigma_\varepsilon, R_\cA, L, \rho, S_w, \lambda_{\min}$ and may
change line to line.

\begin{lemma}[Predictable probe-time excitation]
\label{lem:probe_excitation_conf}
For each segment $k$,
$\lambda_r\!\left(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]\right)
\ge \lambda_{\min}>0$ almost surely.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By stability and $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$, the Lyapunov equation
$\Sigma_k = A_k\Sigma_k A_k^\top + \Sigma_{\eta,k}$ has unique
positive-definite solution with $\lambda_r(\Sigma_k) \ge
\lambda_r(\Sigma_{\eta,k})/(1-(1-\alpha_0)^2) =: \underline\lambda$.
Since $\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]$ converges exponentially fast to
$\Sigma_k$ inside $\cI_k$, the probe-time average over any subset
$\cT_k\subseteq\cI_k$ with $|\cT_k|=m_k$ satisfies
$\lambda_r(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}])
\ge \underline\lambda/2 =: \lambda_{\min}$ for $m_k$ large enough
(i.e.\ past an $O(1)$ warm-up that we absorb into constants).
\end{proof}

\noindent\textbf{F2 (Windowed self-normalised bound).}\ On projected
covariates $z_t = \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\in\R^r$, the windowed ridge
estimator $\widehat a_t = \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$ satisfies,
with probability $\ge 1-\delta$ and simultaneously for all $t$,
$\|\widehat a_t - a_t^\star\|_{\widetilde V_t}\le\beta^{(r,W)}_t$
with
$\beta^{(r,W)}_t = \sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{r\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r)) + 2\log(1/\delta)} + \sqrt\lambda S_w$.
This is the $r$-dimensional projected version of
\citet[Theorem~2]{abbasi2011improved} with the sliding-window correction
of \citet{russac2019weighted}; the proof is an immediate adaptation
of the self-normalised martingale bound of
\citet[Theorem~1]{abbasi2011improved} with window reset, applied to the
projected regressor $z_t$ (dimension $r$) rather than $x_t$.

\noindent\textbf{F3 (Elliptical potential).}\ For any adapted sequence
$z_1,\dots,z_T\in\R^r$ with $\|z_t\|\le R_\cA$ and
$\widetilde V_t = \lambda I + \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s z_s^\top$,
$\sum_{t=1}^T \|z_t\|^2_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le 2r\log(1 + W R_\cA^2/(\lambda r)) =: 2r L_W$
\citep[Lemma~11]{abbasi2011improved}.

\subsection{Concentration of the lifted probe moment (load-bearing step)}
\label{app:concentration_proof}

This is the non-standard piece of the analysis; we derive it in full.
For isotropic Gaussian probes $u\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ (truncated at
$\|u\|\le L := \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$ on a $1-\delta/T$ event;
see \S\ref{app:truncation}), the probe moment operator
$\mathcal K: M\mapsto\E[(u^\top Mu)uu^\top]$ is a linear bijection on
symmetric matrices.

\begin{lemma}[Closed-form inverse of $\mathcal K$]
\label{lem:K_inverse}
For isotropic Gaussian probes,
$\mathcal K(M) = 2M + \tr(M)\,I_d$ and
$\mathcal K^{-1}(N) = \tfrac12 N - \tfrac{\tr(N)}{2(d+2)}\,I_d$, so
$\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op \le 1$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Expand $u^\top M u = \sum_{ij} M_{ij} u_i u_j$ and use the Isserlis
identity $\E[u_i u_j u_k u_l] = \delta_{ij}\delta_{kl} +
\delta_{ik}\delta_{jl} + \delta_{il}\delta_{jk}$ to compute
$\E[(u^\top M u)u_k u_l] = M_{kl} + M_{lk} + \tr(M)\delta_{kl} = 2M_{kl}
+ \tr(M)\delta_{kl}$ (symmetric $M$). Inverting
$N = 2M + \tr(M)I_d$: taking traces gives $\tr(N) = (d+2)\tr(M)$, hence
$M = \tfrac12 N - \tfrac{\tr(N)}{2(d+2)}I_d$. The operator norm
follows because $\tr(N)/(d+2)\le\|N\|_\op$ for symmetric $N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Quadratic measurement identity]
\label{lem:quad_conf}
On every probe round $t\in\cTprobe$, with
$\delta_\sigma := \hat\sigma^2 - \sigma_\varepsilon^2$,
\[
\E[s_t\mid\sigma(\cH_{t-1},u_t)]
= u_t^\top \widetilde M_t u_t + 2 u_t^\top m_t
- \delta_\sigma,\qquad \|m_t\|\le\epsilon_\times.
\]
Under exact probe conditions ($\delta_\sigma=\epsilon_\times=0$) this
collapses to $\E[s_t\mid\cdot] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Expand $y_t^2 = (u_t^\top\theta_t)^2 + 2\varepsilon_t u_t^\top\theta_t
+ \varepsilon_t^2$ and condition on $\sigma(\cH_{t-1},u_t)$; the middle
term contributes $2u_t^\top m_t$ with
$m_t := \E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t]$ bounded by
$\epsilon_\times$ by Assumption; the last term contributes
$\sigma_\varepsilon^2$, and subtracting $\hat\sigma^2$ leaves
$-\delta_\sigma$. Independence of $u_t$ from $(\theta_t,\cH_{t-1})$
gives $\E[(u_t^\top\theta_t)^2\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Lifted probe sample is near-unbiased]
\label{lem:G_unbiased_conf}
Let $G_t := \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t u_tu_t^\top)$. Under $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$
(untruncated; truncation handled in \S\ref{app:truncation}),
$\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}] = \widetilde M_t + \widetilde B_t$ where
the bias satisfies
\[
\widetilde B_t \;=\; -\frac{\delta_\sigma}{d+2}\,I_d
\qquad (\text{scaled identity, exact}),
\]
so $\|\widetilde B_t\|_\op = |\delta_\sigma|/(d+2) \le |\delta_\sigma|$.
In particular, $\widetilde B_t$ is a uniform eigenvalue shift that
does not rotate eigenvectors of $\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Linearity of $\mathcal K^{-1}$ and Lemma~\ref{lem:quad_conf} give
\[
\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]
= \mathcal K^{-1}(\mathcal K(\widetilde M_t))
+ 2\,\mathcal K^{-1}(\E[(u_t^\top m_t)u_tu_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}])
- \delta_\sigma\,\mathcal K^{-1}(\E[u_tu_t^\top]).
\]
The first term equals $\widetilde M_t$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:K_inverse}).
The middle (cross) term vanishes: for any $\cH_{t-1}$-measurable
vector $m_t$, $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ has all third moments zero
by symmetry, so
$\E[(u_t^\top m_t)u_{t,k}u_{t,l}]
= \sum_i m_{t,i}\,\E[u_{t,i}u_{t,k}u_{t,l}] = 0$
for all $k,l$. Hence
$\E[(u_t^\top m_t)u_tu_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}] = 0$ identically, and
the $\epsilon_\times$-dependent term contributes zero to $\widetilde B_t$.
The third term: $\E[u_tu_t^\top]=I_d$, so by Lemma~\ref{lem:K_inverse},
$-\delta_\sigma\mathcal K^{-1}(I_d) = -\delta_\sigma[I_d/2 - d/(2(d+2))\,I_d]
= -\delta_\sigma/(d+2)\cdot I_d$.
Combining, $\widetilde B_t = -\delta_\sigma/(d+2)\,I_d$, a scaled
identity.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Observable a.s.\ bound on $G_t$]
\label{lem:G_bound_conf}
Define the \emph{observable} truncation event
$\cA_t := \{\|u_t\|_2\le L\}\cap\{|y_t|\le L_y\}$ with
$L_y := L\,S_w + L_\varepsilon$ and $L_\varepsilon := \sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{2\log(T/\delta)}$.
Both indicators $\mathbf 1\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$ and $\mathbf 1\{|y_t|\le L_y\}$
are $\sigma(u_t,y_t)$-measurable, hence observable at round $t$. On
the event $\cA_t$,
\[
  |s_t|\le R_s := L_y^2 + \hat\sigma^2, \qquad
  \|G_t\|_\op \le R_X := L^2 R_s + S_w^2.
\]
Choosing $L^2=2d\log(4T/\delta)$ and
$L_\varepsilon=\sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{2\log(4T/\delta)}$ gives
$\Pr(\cA_t^c\mid\cH_{t-1})\le\delta/T$, hence
$\Pr(\bigcap_{t\in\cTprobe}\cA_t)\ge 1-\delta$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Since $\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ with $B_k^\star$ orthonormal and
$\|w_t\|\le S_w$, $\|\theta_t\|_2\le S_w$. On $\cA_t$,
$|y_t|\le L_y$ so $|s_t|=|y_t^2-\hat\sigma^2|\le y_t^2+\hat\sigma^2\le L_y^2+\hat\sigma^2=R_s$;
using $\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op\le 1$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:K_inverse}) and
$\|u_tu_t^\top\|_\op = \|u_t\|^2\le L^2$, we get
$\|G_t\|_\op\le|s_t|\,L^2 + S_w^2 = R_s L^2 + S_w^2 = R_X$ (the $S_w^2$
absorbs the centering term $\|\widetilde M_t\|_\op\le S_w^2$).

For the probability bound, by the $\chi^2_d$ tail
\citep[Lemma~1]{laurent2000adaptive} with $L^2=2d\log(4T/\delta)$,
$\Pr(\|u_t\|>L)\le\delta/(4T)$. On $\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$, the triangle
inequality gives $|y_t|\le L S_w+|\varepsilon_t|$, so
$\{|y_t|>L_y\}\cap\{\|u_t\|\le L\}\subseteq\{|\varepsilon_t|>L_\varepsilon\}$,
and the sub-Gaussian tail with
$L_\varepsilon^2/(2\sigma_\varepsilon^2)=\log(4T/\delta)$ gives
$\Pr(|\varepsilon_t|>L_\varepsilon)\le 2\exp(-\log(4T/\delta))=\delta/(2T)$.
Combining via the union bound
$\cA_t^c = \{\|u_t\|>L\}\cup\{|y_t|>L_y\}\subseteq\{\|u_t\|>L\}\cup\{|\varepsilon_t|>L_\varepsilon\}$
(where the second inclusion uses the triangle-inequality subset relation
on $\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$), $\Pr(\cA_t^c)\le\delta/(4T)+\delta/(2T)\le\delta/T$.
Union over $|\cTprobe|\le T$ rounds gives $\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Matrix Freedman for $\widehat M_k$ via predictable truncation]
\label{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}
Define the predictably-truncated lifted sample
\[
  \tilde G_t \;:=\; G_t\cdot\mathbf 1\{\cA_t\} \qquad(t\in\cTprobe),
\]
where $\cA_t$ is the observable event of Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf},
and let $\tilde X_t := \tilde G_t - \mathbb E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$.
Then $\{\tilde X_t\}_{t\in\cT_k}$ is a matrix martingale-difference
sequence satisfying $\|\tilde X_t\|_\op\le 2R_X$ a.s.\ and
$\|\E[\tilde X_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op\le R_X^2$ a.s. By
\citet{tropp2011freedman}, with probability at least $1-\delta$,
\begin{equation}
  \Bigl\|\tfrac{1}{m_k}\textstyle\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\tilde X_t\Bigr\|_\op
  \;\le\; 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k} + \tfrac{2R_X\log(2d/\delta)}{3m_k}.
\end{equation}
Transferring this to the algorithm's estimator
$\widehat M_k = m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k} G_t$: on the high-probability
event $\cE := \bigcap_{t\in\cT_k}\cA_t$ (which has mass $\ge 1-\delta$
by Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf}) we have $\tilde G_t = G_t$ for every
$t\in\cT_k$, so the Freedman bound applies to $\widehat M_k$ up to a
deterministic bias offset of norm $\|\Theta_k\|_\op$ where
$\Theta_k := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\cA_t^c\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]$.
That offset is polynomially small in $T$ (see \S\ref{app:truncation}).
With probability at least $1-2\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} - \widetilde B\|_\op
\le 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k} + \frac{2R_X\log(2d/\delta)}{3m_k}
+ \|\Theta_k\|_\op.
\label{eq:matrix_bernstein}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
\emph{MDS property.} $\mathbf 1\{\cA_t\}$ is $\sigma(u_t,y_t)$-measurable
and hence $\cH_t$-measurable, so $\tilde G_t$ is $\cH_t$-measurable and
$\E[\tilde X_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]=0$ by construction.

\emph{Deterministic a.s.\ bound.} On $\cA_t$, $\|\tilde G_t\|_\op=\|G_t\|_\op\le R_X$
by Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf}. On $\cA_t^c$, $\tilde G_t=0$, so
$\|\tilde G_t\|_\op\le R_X$ unconditionally. The centered version
satisfies $\|\tilde X_t\|_\op\le\|\tilde G_t\|_\op+\|\E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op\le 2R_X$
a.s. The predictable-variance bound
$\|\E[\tilde X_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op\le R_X^2$ follows from
$\E[\tilde X_t^2]\preceq\E[\tilde G_t^2]\preceq R_X^2 I$ (using the a.s.\ bound).

\emph{Freedman.} Apply the matrix Freedman inequality to the MDS
$\{\tilde X_t\}$ in $\R^{d\times d}_{\mathrm{sym}}$ with the a.s.\ and
variance bounds above; this gives the first displayed inequality on
an event of probability $\ge 1-\delta$.

\emph{Transfer to $\widehat M_k$.} Let $\bar{\tilde G}_k:=m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\tilde G_t$
and $\bar\mu_k:=m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$,
so $m_k^{-1}\sum\tilde X_t = \bar{\tilde G}_k-\bar\mu_k$. On $\cE$,
$\bar{\tilde G}_k=\widehat M_k$, and
$\bar\mu_k=m_k^{-1}\sum\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]-\Theta_k
=\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}+\widetilde B-\Theta_k$
by Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}. Rearranging,
$\widehat M_k-\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}-\widetilde B = (m_k^{-1}\sum\tilde X_t) - \Theta_k$
on $\cE$, and the triangle inequality with the Freedman bound yields
\eqref{eq:matrix_bernstein} on $\cE\cap\{\text{Freedman event}\}$,
which has mass $\ge 1-2\delta$ (accounting for $\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$
and the Freedman $\delta$).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Projector concentration]
\label{cor:projector_conf}
Let $b_\sigma := |\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times$ denote the
(deterministic) conservative upper bound on $\|\widetilde B\|_\op$ valid
for any probe distribution (Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}).
Assume the following three conditions:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=1pt,topsep=1pt]
\item \emph{(Freedman domination)}\ \ $m_k \ge \log(2d/\delta)$, so the
  linear Freedman term of Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf} is at
  most the square-root term;
\item \emph{(Large-sample)}\ \ $m_k \ge (16 R_X/\lambda_{\min})^2\log(2d/\delta)$,
  so the Freedman square-root bound is at most $\lambda_{\min}/4$
  (w.p.\ $\ge 1-2\delta$, accounting for the Freedman $\delta$ and the
  truncation-event $\delta$ from Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf});
\item \emph{(Probe-bias smallness)}\ \ $b_\sigma+\|\Theta_k\|_\op \le \lambda_{\min}/4$,
  so that $\|\widetilde B\|_\op+\|\Theta_k\|_\op \le \lambda_{\min}/4$ and,
  combined with the previous bound and \eqref{eq:matrix_bernstein},
  yields $\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op \le \lambda_{\min}/2$
  at the same confidence.
\end{itemize}
Then with probability at least $1-2\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon_k := \|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op
\le C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}
+ \Delta_\sigma + \frac{4\|\Theta_k\|_\op}{\lambda_{\min}},\quad
C_{\mathrm{sub}} := \frac{8 R_X}{\lambda_{\min}},\
\Delta_\sigma := \frac{4(|\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times)}{\lambda_{\min}}.
\label{eq:proj_bound_conf}
\end{equation}
The truncation-bias contribution $4\|\Theta_k\|_\op/\lambda_{\min}$ is
$O(d\sqrt{\delta/T}/\lambda_{\min}) = o(1)$ for $\delta$ polynomially
small in $T$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:theta_bound}) and is absorbed into the
leading $\tilO(\cdot)$ constants of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}; we
retain it explicitly here so downstream corollaries (e.g.,
Corollary~\ref{cor:rank_adaptive}) can reference it.
For Gaussian probes, the tighter form
$\Delta_\sigma = 4|\delta_\sigma|/((d+2)\lambda_{\min})$ from
Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf} applies in place of the conservative
form above (the state-noise coupling $\epsilon_\times$ contributes
zero bias by Gaussian odd-moment vanishing).
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}, $\E[\widehat M_k\mid\cH]
= \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} + \widetilde B$ with
$\|\widetilde B\|_\op \le |\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times =: b_\sigma$
(conservative bound valid for any probe distribution).
Combining with Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}, with probability
$\ge 1-2\delta$,
\[
\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op
\le 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}
+ \frac{2R_X\log(2d/\delta)}{3m_k}
+ \|\Theta_k\|_\op
+ b_\sigma.
\]
Under the assumption $m_k \ge \log(2d/\delta)$ the linear Freedman term
$2R_X\log(2d/\delta)/(3m_k)$ is at most the square-root term; and the
truncation bias $\|\Theta_k\|_\op = O(d\sqrt{\delta/T})$ is
polynomially small (Proposition~\ref{prop:theta_bound} in Appendix~\ref{app:truncation}),
so both are absorbed into a constant inflation of $C_{\mathrm{sub}}$.
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}$ has rank exactly $r$ with $r$-th
eigenvalue $\ge\lambda_{\min}$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf}
via Proposition~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf} below); Davis--Kahan
\citep[Theorem~VII.3.1]{bhatia1997matrix} gives
$\|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op\le 4\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op / \lambda_{\min}$,
yielding \eqref{eq:proj_bound_conf}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Segment factorisation]
\label{prop:segment_factorization_conf}
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} = B_k^\star \bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}} (B_k^\star)^\top$ where
$\bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}} := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]$.
In particular, $\range(\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}) = \range(B_k^\star)$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$\widetilde M_t = B_k^\star \E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}](B_k^\star)^\top$
for $t\in\cI_k$; average over $\cT_k$ and pull $B_k^\star$ out.
Range equality uses
$\lambda_r(\bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}})\ge\lambda_{\min}>0$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf}).
\end{proof}

This chain, Lemmas \ref{lem:K_inverse}--\ref{lem:G_bound_conf},
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}, Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf},
Proposition~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf}, gives a fully
self-contained upper bound on subspace recovery at rate
$O(1/\sqrt{m_k})$. A variance-adaptive refinement (replacing the
uniform a.s.\ bound $R_X$ by a local effective scale) sharpens
constants but does not change the leading order.

\begin{remark}[Sharpness of the probe-term constant]
\label{rem:dim_dep}
Writing the probe-term $\tilO$ of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} in
full constants: Gaussian probes truncated at $L=O(\sqrt{d\log(T/\delta)})$
produce Freedman radius $R_s=\Theta(L^2)$, so
$C_{\mathrm{sub}}=8R_X/\lambda_{\min}=\Theta(d\log(T/\delta))$ and
$B=\Theta(d\,\sqrt{\log(d/\delta)})$. The probe contribution then
reads
$\tilO(d^{2/3}\,K^{1/3}\,T^{2/3})$, the $\tilO$ hiding polylog factors
only. This $d^{2/3}$ scaling reflects our full-dimensional
subspace-recovery analysis (truncated Gaussian/sphere probes give
Freedman radius $L^2=\Theta(d)$, propagating to
$C_{\mathrm{sub}}=\Theta(d)$). Whether a tighter analysis could remove
this factor is open; the $d^{2/3}$ does not contradict our
policy-class-agnostic lower bound $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$, which we
only claim is tight \emph{in $T$} (Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}).

\emph{Conjecture (why the $d^{2/3}$ is loose).} The empirical
$d$-scaling fit of \S\ref{app:probe_rank_ablation} gives a log-log
slope of $-0.16$, far below the predicted $+0.67$. The worst-case
Freedman envelope $L^2=\Theta(d\log T)$ is enforced uniformly, but
$\|u_t\|^2$ for $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ concentrates at $d$ with
$\sqrt d$ fluctuations — typical probes contribute variance $\Theta(d)$,
not $\Theta(d\log T)$. A self-normalised tail bound replacing the
worst-case $L^2$ with the second moment would give
$C_{\mathrm{sub}}=\Theta(\sqrt d)$ and a probe term of
$\tilO(d^{1/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$, still above the empirical near-constant
scaling. Closing the remaining gap likely requires an instance-specific
analysis tracking $\|\widehat M_k-\bar M_k\|_\op$ on the $r$-dim
subspace rather than the ambient ball.
The $r$-saving lives in the leading exploitation term
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})$, which has no ambient $d$-dependence, so the
``intrinsic rank replaces ambient dimension'' message refers to the
dominant term. Balancing
$r\sqrt{KT}+d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$ against $d\sqrt{KT}$ yields the
crossover curve in $(d,r)$ space,
\[
d - r \;\gtrsim\; d^{2/3}\,(T/K)^{1/6},
\]
with the simpler scale-form $d\gtrsim\sqrt{T/K}$ recovering the
$r\to 0$ corner. The synthetic phase-transition of
\S\ref{sec:exp_phase} verifies the curve directly: at $T=5000$, $K=10$
($(T/K)^{1/6}\approx 2.82$), the predicted gap $d^{2/3}\cdot 2.82$
gives e.g.\ $r<11.7$ at $d=50$ and $r<39.3$ at $d=100$
(Figure~\ref{fig:synthetic-phase}). The empirical boundary tracks the
theoretical curve, a positive check on both the analysis and the
probe-cost calibration.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}}
\label{app:proof_main}

Fix a segment $\cI_k$ with probe budget $m_k$ and let
$\Delta_t := \max_{x\in\cA_t} x^\top\theta_t - x_t^\top\theta_t$ denote
the instantaneous exploitation regret at $t$. We decompose the costed
regret into three pieces bounded separately.

\emph{(i) Projected exploitation regret.}
On exploitation rounds the learner plays
$x_t = \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}\{(\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x)^\top \widehat a_t
+ \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
+ \gamma_t\|x\|\}$. Write $a_t^\star := \widehat U_{t-1}^\top \theta_t$
(the projection of the truth onto the current estimate). Let
$\mathrm{UCB}(x) := (\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x)^\top\widehat a_t
+ \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
+ \gamma_t\|x\|_2$. For $x_t^\star\in\argmax_x x^\top\theta_t$,
optimism (when the UCB event holds) gives $x_t^{\star\top}\theta_t
\le \mathrm{UCB}(x_t^\star) \le \mathrm{UCB}(x_t)$ (the second by
$x_t = \argmax \mathrm{UCB}$), hence
\begin{align}
\Delta_t
&\le \mathrm{UCB}(x_t) - x_t^\top\theta_t \notag\\
&= \underbrace{x_t^\top(\widehat U_{t-1}\widehat a_t - \theta_t)}_{\text{in-subspace estimation}\,+\,\text{subspace mismatch}}
 + \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
 + \gamma_t\|x_t\|_2 \notag \\
&\le \underbrace{\bigl|x_t^\top \widehat U_{t-1}(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)\bigr|}_{\text{in-subspace estimation, step (i.b)}}
 + \underbrace{\bigl|x_t^\top(I - \widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t\bigr|}_{\text{subspace mismatch, step (i.a)}}
 + \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
 + \gamma_t\|x_t\|_2.
\label{eq:exploit_decomp}
\end{align}
The second line uses $\widehat U_{t-1}\widehat a_t = \widehat P_{t-1}\widehat U_{t-1}\widehat a_t$
and $\widehat P_{t-1}\theta_t = \widehat U_{t-1}a_t^\star$, giving
$\widehat U_{t-1}\widehat a_t - \theta_t = \widehat U_{t-1}(\widehat a_t-a_t^\star) - (I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t$;
the third line applies the triangle inequality to split it into two
non-negative pieces, bounded separately below.
We set $\gamma_t := S_w\varepsilon_{k,t}$, which produces the
\emph{pointwise} domination
$|x^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta| \le \|x\|_2\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta\|_2 \le \|x\|_2 S_w\varepsilon_k = \gamma_t\|x\|_2$
(Cauchy--Schwarz + equal-rank projector identity), so the $\gamma_t\|x\|_2$
slack absorbs the subspace-mismatch bias at every $x$, ensuring optimism
holds without assuming $R_\cA\ge 1$.
\emph{(i.a) Subspace-mismatch term.}
$|x_t^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t| \le R_\cA\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t\|_2
\le R_\cA\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})B_k^\star\|_\op\cdot\|w_t\|_2 \le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
(using the equal-rank-projector identity
$\|(I-\widehat P)B^\star\|_\op\le\|P^\star-\widehat P\|_\op=\varepsilon_k$
and $\|w_t\|\le S_w$). Summed over $E_k$: $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$.

\emph{(i.b) In-subspace estimation error.} The ridge regression is
\emph{misspecified}: the residual is
$y_s - z_s^\top a_s^\star = \xi_s + \varepsilon_s$ with
$\xi_s = x_s^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_s$ bounded by $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$ (using the reference projector of Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf}; on $E_k$ we have $\widehat P_{t-1}\equiv\widehat P_k$ constant)
(same bound as step (i.a), via the equal-rank identity). We therefore
apply Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf} \emph{in place of} the standard
well-specified self-normalized bound, taking $\bar\xi_k:=R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$:
for $z=z_t(x_t)$ with $\|z\|_2\le R_\cA$,
\[
  |(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z|
  \;\le\; \bigl(\beta_t^{(r,W)}
  + R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)\sqrt{W}
  + R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}\bigr)\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}.
\]
Summing over $E_k$: the noise and mismatch-propagation pieces are
handled by F3 ($\sum_{t\in E_k}\|z_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\le\sqrt{2rn_kL_W}$);
the drift piece is handled by Cauchy--Schwarz in $t$ together with F3
and $\sum_t V_{k,t}(W)^2\le(\max_t V_{k,t}(W))\sum_t V_{k,t}(W)\le V_k\cdot W V_k=WV_k^2$
(each drift edge counted in $\le W$ windows):
\begin{equation}
\sum_{t\in E_k}|(\widehat a_t-a_t^\star)^\top z_t|
\le \beta_k^{(r,W)}\sqrt{2 r n_k L_W}
+ R_\cA W V_k\sqrt{2rL_W}
+ 2 r L_W R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{W n_k}.
\label{eq:in_subspace_sum}
\end{equation}
The third term (subspace-mismatch propagation through the ridge) is
\emph{lower-order} than the step-(i.a) term $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$
whenever $n_k\ge W(2rL_W)^2$, which holds in the natural regime; it
is absorbed into the $\tilO(\cdot)$ constants below.

\emph{(i.c) Total exploit regret.}
For optimism at $x_t^\star$ we need the UCB radius to dominate the full
Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf} bound. We therefore distinguish the
\emph{base statistical radius} $\beta_t^{(r,W)}$ (appearing in F2) from
the \emph{algorithmic inflated radius}
\[
  \widetilde\beta_t^{(r,W)} \;:=\; \beta_t^{(r,W)}
  + \bar V_k\sqrt{W}
  + R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{2WrL_W},
\]
where $\bar V_k := 2R_\cA S_w$ is a deterministic upper bound on the
unobservable per-window drift contribution $R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)$ in the
piecewise-constant regime (a single boundary edge contributes
$\le R_\cA\cdot 2 S_w$ to $V_{k,t}(W)$ within any window). Since
$\bar V_k\sqrt W \ge R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)\sqrt W$ termwise, the algorithm's
UCB radius dominates the analytical drift bound of
Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf}; optimism therefore holds, and the regret
analysis below proceeds with $V_{k,t}(W)$ in the inequality
(yielding the tighter $O(WV)$ regret bound) while the algorithm uses
the computable $\bar V_k$. This is the standard sliding-window UCB
convention of \citet{russac2019weighted}; the $\gamma_t\|x\|$ slack with
$\gamma_t := S_w\varepsilon_{k,t}$ produces the pointwise domination
$|x^\top(I-\widehat P)\theta|\le\|x\|\|(I-\widehat P)\theta\|\le\|x\|S_w\varepsilon_k=\gamma_t\|x\|$
(Cauchy--Schwarz + equal-rank projector identity), covering the
subspace-mismatch bias at every $x$ with no $R_\cA\ge 1$ assumption. Adding step-(i.a), step-(i.b),
and the $\gamma_t\|x_t\|$ accounting,
\begin{equation}
\sum_{t\in E_k}\Delta_t
\le 2\beta_k^{(r,W)}\sqrt{2 r n_k L_W}
+ 2R_\cA S_w \varepsilon_k n_k
+ 2 R_\cA W V_k\sqrt{2rL_W}
+ \text{(lower-order)}.
\label{eq:exploit_sum}
\end{equation}
(The factor of 2 in front of each radius comes from optimism; the
factor 2 in front of step-(i.a) comes from adding
$\sum_t\gamma_t\|x_t\|\le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$ to the raw step-(i.a)
$R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$ mismatch regret.) Summing over segments via
$\sum_k\sqrt{n_k}\le\sqrt{KT}$ (Cauchy--Schwarz) and $\sum_k V_k=V$ gives
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \sum_k 2R_\cA S_w \varepsilon_k n_k + O(R_\cA W V)$.

\emph{(ii) Probe--subspace tradeoff.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf} (matrix Bernstein +
Davis--Kahan, proved in \S\ref{app:concentration_proof}), applied
at confidence level $\delta/(2K)$, gives, on an
event of probability $\ge 1-\delta/(2K)$,
\[
\varepsilon_k \;\le\; C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(4Kd/\delta)/m_k} \;+\; \Delta_\sigma,\qquad
\Delta_\sigma := 4\bigl(|\hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times\bigr)/\lambda_{\min}.
\]
The bias floor $\Delta_\sigma$ does \emph{not} vanish with $m_k$: it
is a constant (in $T$) determined by plugin variance misspecification
and state--noise coupling. Substituting into the (factor-2) step-(i.c)
subspace-mismatch regret $2R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$ yields two
contributions per segment:
\[
  2R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k \;\le\; \underbrace{B\,n_k/\sqrt{m_k}}_{\text{statistical}}
  \;+\; \underbrace{2R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma n_k}_{\text{bias floor}},
\qquad B := 4 C_{\mathrm{sub}} S_w R_\cA\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)}.
\]
(The $B$-constant absorbs the factor of 2 from step (i.c) so that the
subsequent minimisation expression has a clean form.) Probe rounds
contribute $A m_k$ with $A := 2 R_\cA S_w + c$ (foregone action plus
designated cost). Minimising $A m_k + B\ell_k/\sqrt{m_k}$ in
$m_k\in[1,\ell_k]$ (the statistical part) by setting the derivative to
zero gives $m_k^\star = \lceil (B\ell_k/(2A))^{2/3}\rceil$ and
\begin{equation}
A m_k^\star + \frac{B\ell_k}{\sqrt{m_k^\star}}
= \tfrac{3}{2^{2/3}}\, A^{1/3} B^{2/3}\, \ell_k^{2/3}\,(1+o(1)).
\label{eq:opt_mk}
\end{equation}
Summing over segments, the statistical contribution uses Jensen,
$\sum_k \ell_k^{2/3}\le K^{1/3}(\sum_k\ell_k)^{2/3} = K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$,
so the probe--subspace-statistical cost is $\tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$.
The bias-floor contribution sums to $R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma\sum_k n_k
\le R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma T = O(T\Delta_\sigma)$, the last term in
the main bound. Under exact probes ($\hat\sigma^2=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$
and $\epsilon_\times=0$), $\Delta_\sigma=0$ and this term vanishes.

\emph{(iii) Within-segment drift.}
Let
$V_k := \sum_{s\in E_k,s+1\in E_k}\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$
and $V := \sum_k V_k$ denote the per-segment and total path variation
of $\theta$.

\begin{lemma}[Windowed ridge prediction bound]
\label{lem:drift_conf}
Assume the projected-regression observation model on $s\in\cW_t$ is
\begin{equation}
y_s = z_s^\top a_s^\star + \xi_s + \varepsilon_s,\qquad
|\xi_s|\le\bar\xi_k,\qquad
\varepsilon_s\;\text{is F2-sub-Gaussian},
\label{eq:ridge_residual_decomp}
\end{equation}
where the projected covariate $z_s := \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_s$ and the
target $a_s^\star := \widehat U_{t-1}^\top\theta_s$ both use the
\emph{single reference projector} $\widehat U_{t-1}$ across the window
$\cW_t$ (i.e., the ridge inside a window is solved with respect to a
fixed basis, the one available at the end of the window). This is
the standard sliding-window convention of \citet{russac2019weighted}.
$\xi_s$ is a \emph{deterministic residual} (subspace-mismatch bias at
round $s$), and $\varepsilon_s$ is the sub-Gaussian reward noise.
On the F2 concentration event, the windowed ridge estimator
$\widehat a_t=\widetilde V_t^{-1}\sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s y_s$ satisfies,
for every $\|z\|_2\le R_\cA$,
\begin{equation}
\bigl|(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z\bigr|
\;\le\; \bigl(\underbrace{\beta_t^{(r,W)}}_{\text{noise}}
 \;+\; \underbrace{R_\cA\,V_{k,t}(W)\sqrt{W}}_{\text{drift}}
 \;+\; \underbrace{\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2W r L_W}}_{\text{mismatch prop.}}\bigr)\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}},
\label{eq:drift_lemma_bound}
\end{equation}
where
$V_{k,t}(W) := \sum_{s\in\cW_t,s+1\in\cW_t}\|a_{s+1}^\star-a_s^\star\|_2$
is the in-subspace path variation inside the window.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Write $a_t^\star=\widehat U_{t-1}^\top\theta_t$ and
$\eta_s := a_s^\star - a_t^\star$. Using
\eqref{eq:ridge_residual_decomp}, the ridge normal equations give
\[
\widetilde V_t(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)
= \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s\bigl(z_s^\top\eta_s + \xi_s + \varepsilon_s\bigr) - \lambda a_t^\star
= \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s z_s^\top \eta_s}_{B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}}
\;+\; \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s \xi_s}_{B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}}
\;+\; \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s\varepsilon_s - \lambda a_t^\star}_{N_t}.
\]
Then
$(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z
= z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}} + z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{sub}} + z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}N_t$.

\emph{Noise term.} By Cauchy--Schwarz in the $\widetilde V_t^{-1}$-inner
product, $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}N_t|\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\cdot\|N_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\,\beta_t^{(r,W)}$, where F2 supplies the
second inequality.

\emph{Drift term.} By Cauchy--Schwarz in the $\widetilde V_t^{-1}$-inner
product, $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}|
\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\cdot\|B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}$.
To bound $\|B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}$, set
$c_s := z_s^\top\eta_s$ and note
$|c_s|\le\|z_s\|\,\|\eta_s\|\le R_\cA\,V_{k,t}(W)$ uniformly (since
$\|\eta_s\|=\|a_s^\star-a_t^\star\|\le V_{k,t}(W)$ by telescoping).
Writing $B_t^{\mathrm{drift}} = \sum_{s\in\cW_t} c_s z_s$ and using the
matrix-inner-product expansion,
\[
\|B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}^2
\;=\; \sum_{s,s'\in\cW_t} c_s c_{s'}\,z_s^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1} z_{s'}
\;=\; c^\top M c,
\]
where $M_{ss'}:=z_s^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}z_{s'}$. The matrix $M$ is the
Gram matrix of the vectors $\{\widetilde V_t^{-1/2}z_s\}_{s\in\cW_t}$, so
$M\preceq I_{|\cW_t|}$ iff $\sum_s z_sz_s^\top\preceq\widetilde V_t$,
which holds because $\widetilde V_t=\lambda I+\sum_s z_sz_s^\top$.
(Concretely, if $Z$ stacks the $z_s$ as rows, $M = Z\widetilde V_t^{-1}Z^\top$
and $\|M\|_\op=\|Z\widetilde V_t^{-1}Z^\top\|_\op\le 1$.)
Hence $c^\top M c\le\|c\|_2^2\le|\cW_t|\,R_\cA^2 V_{k,t}(W)^2\le W R_\cA^2 V_{k,t}(W)^2$,
giving $\|B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\le R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)\sqrt{W}$
and $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}|\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\,R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)\sqrt{W}$.

\emph{Subspace-mismatch propagation.} By Cauchy--Schwarz,
$\|B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
=\bigl\|\sum_s z_s\xi_s\bigr\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\sum_s|\xi_s|\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\bar\xi_k\sum_s\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}$.
Applying the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality to the last sum and the
elliptical-potential lemma F3,
$\sum_s\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\sqrt{W}\sqrt{\sum_{s\in\cW_t}\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}^2}
\le\sqrt{W\cdot 2rL_W}$,
giving $\|B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\le\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}$
and $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}|\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\cdot\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}$.

Combining the three terms yields \eqref{eq:drift_lemma_bound}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Specialization to the SPSC projected ridge]
\label{rem:drift_lemma_spec}
When applied to SPSC's projected ridge, the residual is
$\xi_s := x_s^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_s$, using the \emph{same
reference projector} $\widehat P_{t-1}$ that Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf}
uses to define $z_s$. (Inside a single segment's exploitation phase,
$\widehat P_{t-1}=\widehat P_{s-1}=\widehat P_k$ is constant across the
window, so the two conventions coincide on $E_k$.) The residual is
bounded by
$|\xi_s|\le R_\cA\cdot\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})B_k^\star\|_\op\cdot S_w\le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
via the equal-rank projector identity
$\|(I-\widehat P)B^\star\|_\op\le\|P^\star-\widehat P\|_\op$. Hence in
\eqref{eq:drift_lemma_bound} we may take $\bar\xi_k=R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$.
\end{remark}

\emph{Quantifying the subspace-mismatch propagation.} Substituting
$m_k^\star\asymp T^{2/3}K^{-2/3}$, $\varepsilon_k\asymp m_k^{-1/2}\asymp K^{1/3}T^{-1/3}$,
$n_k\asymp T/K$ into the lower-order term of \eqref{eq:in_subspace_sum},
$2rL_W R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{Wn_k}
\asymp \tilO(W^{1/2}K^{-1/6}T^{1/6})$
per segment, giving $\tilO(K^{5/6}W^{1/2}T^{1/6})$ summed over $K$
segments, which is $o(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ whenever $WK=o(T)$
(the natural window-scaling regime).\footnote{The $\gamma_t\|x\|_2$
correction in Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} supplements the UCB radius with a
per-round local-variation estimate and ensures the chosen $x_t$ is
optimistic against both the drift and the subspace-mismatch bias; it
does not change the $O(WV)$ rate.}

\emph{Drift summation.} Each drift edge
$\|a_{s+1}^\star-a_s^\star\|_2\le\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$ appears
in at most $W$ windows, so $\sum_{k,t\in E_k}V_{k,t}(W)\le W V$, and
the total drift contribution is $O(R_\cA W V)$.

\emph{Regime of interest.} The bound is informative whenever
$V = o(r\sqrt{KT}/W)$. For \emph{piecewise-constant} subspaces the
path variation is supported on the $O(K)$ boundary edges, so
$V\le 2(K-1)\,S_w\le 2K S_w$ deterministically (each
$\|\theta_{\tau_k+1}-\theta_{\tau_k}\|\le 2\|\theta\|\le 2S_w$ by the
triangle inequality and $\|\theta_t\|=\|B_k^\star w_t\|\le S_w$). The
drift contribution is then $R_\cA W V\le 2K W R_\cA S_w$; for the
natural window choice $W\asymp\sqrt{T/K}$ this is
$O(\sqrt{KT}\,R_\cA S_w)$, lower-order than both leading terms. For a
\emph{stationary stable LDS}, by contrast, $\mathbb E[V_k]=\Theta(\ell_k)$
(the path variation grows linearly in segment length because
$\mathbb E\|\theta_{t+1}-\theta_t\|$ is $\Theta(1)$ per step for any
stationary non-degenerate process), so $V=\Theta(T)$ and the $O(WV)$
term dominates unless $W$ is chosen small enough; hence the
piecewise-constant simplification is the natural regime in which the
main bound is interesting.

Taking a union bound over the $K$ segment-level concentration events
(each at confidence $\delta/(2K)$, total mass $\delta/2$) and the
windowed self-normalised event (at confidence $\delta/2$), we obtain,
with probability $\ge 1-\delta$,
\begin{align*}
\DynReg_T^{(c)}
&\le C_1\, r\sqrt{KT\, L_W \log(2T/\delta)}
+ C_2\, (A^2 B)^{1/3} K^{1/3} T^{2/3} \\
&\quad + C_3\, R_\cA\, W\, V
+ C_4\, R_\cA S_w T\Delta_\sigma \\
&= \tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})
+ O(WV) + O(T\Delta_\sigma),
\end{align*}
matching the statement of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} (the
$d^{2/3}$ in the probe term comes from
$B = \Theta(C_{\mathrm{sub}}) = \Theta(d)$ via the
truncated-Gaussian Freedman radius $L^2 = \Theta(d\log T)$;
cf.\ Remark~\ref{rem:dim_dep}). The
$\sqrt{K}$ factor in the exploitation term is tight for any algorithm
that restarts its estimator at every segment boundary (standard
lower bound for piecewise-stationary linear bandits); under exact
probes the $T\Delta_\sigma$ term vanishes.\qed

\subsection{Rank-adaptive corollary and proof}
\label{app:proof_adaptive_rank}

\begin{corollary}[Rank-adaptive guarantee, stated]
\label{cor:rank_adaptive}
When $r$ is unknown, thresholding $\widehat M_k$ at the
algorithm-computable level $2\tau_k^\star = 2(\tau_k+b)$, where
$\tau_k$ is the full Freedman radius of
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf} and
$b=\Delta_{\sigma,\max}/(d+2)$ is the bias-shift correction
(scaled-identity, Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}), recovers the true
rank with probability $\ge 1-2\delta$ provided
$\lambda_{\min}\ge 4\tau_k^\star$; the bound \eqref{eq:main_bound}
applies unchanged. The proof is a direct Weyl-inequality argument on
the spectrum of $\widehat M_k$: the $r$ ``true'' eigenvalues lie above
$3\tau_k^\star$ and the $d-r$ ``zero'' eigenvalues lie below
$\tau_k^\star$, so thresholding at $2\tau_k^\star$ separates them.
We omit the routine details for space.
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}}
\label{app:proof_adaptive}

\emph{False-alarm bound ($H_0$).}
The detector $S_t := \|\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}} - \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}\|_\op$
compares the lifted moment over the last $W_{\mathrm{det}}$ probes
against the segment-accumulated moment. Under $H_0$ (no change),
triangle inequality plus matrix Freedman (Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf})
applied to each half gives
$\Pr(S_t > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}) \le \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}/T$ with
$\eta_{\mathrm{det}} = C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{2\log(dT/\delta_{\mathrm{FA}})/W_{\mathrm{det}}}$
(the factor $\sqrt 2$ accounts for each half-window having
$W_{\mathrm{det}}/2$ probes). Union bound over $T$ rounds gives
false-alarm probability $\le \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$.

\emph{Detection delay ($H_1$).}
Under $H_1$ at time $\tau_k$, the separation $\Delta_k \ge 2b$ with
$b > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}$ forces the detector to trigger once the
\emph{recent} window accumulates enough post-change probes to shift
the moment by $\ge 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}$. Since probes arrive as
Bernoulli$(\mu)$, the number of post-change rounds $N$ needed to
collect $W_{\mathrm{det}}$ fresh probes is a negative-binomial random
variable with mean $W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu$. By a Chernoff bound for
sums of Bernoulli$(\mu)$ random variables,
$\Pr(N > 2W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu) \le \exp(-W_{\mathrm{det}}/8)$. Taking
$W_{\mathrm{det}} \ge 8\log(K/\delta')$ for a \emph{total} detection-delay
failure budget $\delta'$ (equivalently, per-change budget $\delta'/K$),
the detection delay satisfies $D_k \le 2W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu$ with
probability $\ge 1-\delta'/K$; union-bounding over the $K$ changes
gives total delay probability $\ge 1-\delta'$. The regret incurred during delays is
$2R_\cA S_w\sum_k D_k = O(KW_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu)$ on this event.

\emph{Fixed-rate probe allocation and its consequences.}
With $m_k \sim \mu\ell_k$ probes per segment, the subspace error is
$\varepsilon_k = O\bigl(1/\sqrt{\mu\ell_k}\bigr) + \Delta_\sigma$.
Per-segment subspace-mismatch regret is
$R_\cA S_w \varepsilon_k n_k = O(R_\cA S_w \sqrt{\ell_k/\mu}) + R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma n_k$.
Summing over segments via Cauchy--Schwarz ($\sum_k\sqrt{\ell_k}\le\sqrt{KT}$):
\[
\underbrace{c\mu T}_{\text{probe cost}} \;+\; \underbrace{O(R_\cA S_w \sqrt{KT/\mu})}_{\text{estimation regret}}
\;+\; O(T\Delta_\sigma).
\]
Minimising the sum $c\mu T + C\sqrt{KT/\mu}$ in $\mu$ gives
$\mu^\star = \Theta\bigl((C/c)^{2/3}(K/T)^{1/3}\bigr)$ and optimal
value $\Theta(c^{1/3}C^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$. With the calibration
$c_\mu := (C/c)^{2/3}$ stated in the theorem, $\mu^\star = c_\mu(K/T)^{1/3}$
and the statistical+probe cost is $\Theta(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$, matching
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}'s oracle rate.

\emph{Detection-delay overhead under $\mu = \Theta((K/T)^{1/3})$.}
$KW_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu = KW_{\mathrm{det}}\cdot(T/K)^{1/3}/c_\mu
= (c_\mu^{-1})K^{2/3}T^{1/3}W_{\mathrm{det}}$, which is
$o(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ iff $W_{\mathrm{det}} = o((T/K)^{1/3})$, the
upper half of the theorem's two-sided $W_{\mathrm{det}}$ condition.
The compatibility $\log(K/\delta')=o((T/K)^{1/3})$ (theorem hypothesis)
is equivalent to $K\log^3(K/\delta')=o(T)$; for constant $\delta'$,
the sharp applicability boundary is $K=o(T/\log^3 T)$. Polynomial
sparsity $K=T^\beta$ ($\beta<1$) is safely inside; $K=T/(\log T)^c$
is inside iff $c>3$ (so $K=T/(\log T)^2$ is \emph{outside}, the
lower bound $8\log(K/\delta')\asymp\log T$ exceeds the upper bound
$(T/K)^{1/3}=(\log T)^{2/3}$, no admissible $W_{\mathrm{det}}$ exists,
and the theorem does not apply).

\emph{Exploitation regret and drift.}
The projected exploitation analysis of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}
(Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf}) carries through verbatim with estimated
boundaries $\widehat\tau_k$; the noise contribution is
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})$, and the drift contribution is $O(WV)$.

\emph{Final bound.} Combining,
\[
\DynReg_T^{(c)} \le \tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})
+ O(WV) + O(T\Delta_\sigma) + O(KW_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu),
\]
with the last term lower-order under the stated $W_{\mathrm{det}}$
scaling. Total failure probability via union bound: $\delta$ (Freedman
over the segment-level concentration events) $+\ \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$
(false alarms) $+\ \delta'$ (detection-delay excursion, from R3.1's
Chernoff argument above) $=\delta+\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}+\delta'$,
matching the theorem statement.\qed

\begin{remark}[Graceful degradation for small changes]
\label{rem:small_change}
If a true change at $\tau_k$ has $\Delta_k < 2b$ (subspace separation
below the detector threshold), the detector may miss it, and the
algorithm's projector $\widehat U$ persists across the boundary. The
resulting per-round subspace-mismatch regret is
$R_\cA\|(I-\widehat P)\theta_t\|\le R_\cA S_w\Delta_k$, which is a
\emph{persistent linear-in-horizon} term, not a drift term. Two
accounting options: (i) if the detector threshold $b$ is taken
large enough that all practically relevant changes have $\Delta_k\ge 2b$
(e.g., $b$ chosen to match domain knowledge of the minimum
actionable subspace change), missed changes by definition contribute
negligibly, $R_\cA S_w\cdot 2b\cdot T = O(bT)$; or (ii) if missed
changes must be absorbed analytically, restate the drift term as
$O(WV + R_\cA S_w\sum_k \Delta_k\mathbf 1\{\Delta_k<2b\}\cdot\ell_k)$,
replacing $V$ with $V^\dagger := V + R_\cA S_w\sum_k\Delta_k\mathbf 1\{\Delta_k<2b\}\cdot\ell_k/W$
inside the $O(WV^\dagger)$ drift term (the $R_\cA S_w$ factor converts
the projector difference $\Delta_k$ into a $\theta$-norm scale). In the piecewise-constant
regime this degradation is sharp: the algorithm cannot recover from a
missed subspace jump without a windowed \emph{subspace} estimator,
which SPSC-Adaptive does not deploy.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}}
\label{app:proof_lb}

We refine the lower-bound argument via the trace identity, which
removes the hard-projection restriction $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ that an
earlier sketch imposed and yields a policy-class-agnostic bound.

\begin{lemma}[Trace KL bound, policy-agnostic]
\label{lem:trace_kl_conf}
Fix $T\ge 1$ and $0\le m\le T$, and let $\pi$ be any (possibly
randomized) history-adapted policy taking actions $x_t\in\R^d$ with
$\|x_t\|_2\le R_\cA$ on exploit rounds, and $x_t = u_t$ with
$u_t \sim \mathcal N(0,I_d)$ on the $m$ designated probe rounds (with
$m$ fixed in advance; the bound applies to any deterministic probe
schedule). Let $\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}\subseteq[T]$ denote the exploit
rounds (so $|\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}|=T-m$). Under $\nu_0$, for any random
unit vector $v\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\mathbb S^{d-1})$ chosen
\emph{after} the policy's distribution under $\nu_0$ is fixed,
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E_v\!\left[\,\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t=1}^T(x_t^\top v)^2\Bigr]\right]
\;\le\; m \;+\; \frac{R_\cA^{\,2}\,(T-m)}{d}.
\label{eq:trace_kl_conf}
\end{equation}
Restricting the inner sum to exploit rounds gives the sharper
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E_v\!\left[\,\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}(x_t^\top v)^2\Bigr]\right]
\;\le\; \frac{R_\cA^{\,2}\,(T-m)}{d}.
\label{eq:trace_kl_ex_conf}
\end{equation}
Consequently there exists $v^\star\in\mathbb S^{d-1}$ for which the
$\mathbb P_0$-expectations in \eqref{eq:trace_kl_conf} and
\eqref{eq:trace_kl_ex_conf} hold with $v$ replaced by $v^\star$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By the trace identity
$\mathbb E_{v\sim\mathrm{Unif}(\mathbb S^{d-1})}[v v^\top] = I_d/d$,
$\mathbb E_v[(x^\top v)^2] = x^\top \mathbb E_v[vv^\top] x = \|x\|^2/d$
for any $x\in\R^d$.

\emph{Probe rounds.} For $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$,
$\mathbb E_{u_t}[\|u_t\|^2]=d$, so
$\mathbb E_v\mathbb E_{u_t}[(u_t^\top v)^2]=\mathbb E_{u_t}[\|u_t\|^2/d]=1$,
contributing $m$ over the $m$ probe rounds.

\emph{Exploit rounds.} On exploit rounds $\|x_t\|\le R_\cA$ a.s., and
the trajectory law under $\mathbb P_0$ is independent of $v$ (rewards
under $\nu_0$ are $\mathcal N(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2)$ regardless of $v$), so
we may swap the order of expectations by Fubini:
\[
\mathbb E_v\,\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\bigl[(x_t^\top v)^2\bigr]
\;=\; \mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\bigl[\mathbb E_v[(x_t^\top v)^2\mid x_t]\bigr]
\;=\; \mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\bigl[\|x_t\|^2/d\bigr] \;\le\; R_\cA^2/d,
\]
contributing $R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$ over the $T-m$ exploit rounds.

Adding the two contributions yields \eqref{eq:trace_kl_conf};
restricting to exploit rounds yields \eqref{eq:trace_kl_ex_conf}.

\emph{A single $v^\star$ achieves both bounds.} Define
$g_{\mathrm{full}}(v) := \mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}[\sum_{t=1}^T(x_t^\top v)^2]$
and
$g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v) := \mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}(x_t^\top v)^2]$.
The difference is the contribution from probe rounds:
$g_{\mathrm{full}}(v) - g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v)
= \mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{probe}}}(u_t^\top v)^2]$.
For every unit $v\in\mathbb S^{d-1}$, $u_t^\top v\sim \mathcal N(0,1)$ (since
$u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ is rotationally invariant), so each probe round
contributes exactly $1$ to this expectation; with $m$ probe rounds,
$g_{\mathrm{full}}(v) - g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v) = m$ for every $v$. Hence
any minimizer $v^\star\in\mathbb S^{d-1}$ of $g_{\mathrm{ex}}$ that
witnesses $g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v^\star)\le \mathbb E_v[g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v)]
\le R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$ \emph{simultaneously} witnesses
$g_{\mathrm{full}}(v^\star) = m + g_{\mathrm{ex}}(v^\star)\le m + R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$,
proving both bounds with the same $v^\star$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the Cauchy--Schwarz argument]
\label{rem:trace_vs_cs_conf}
A naive proof would bound exploit-round contributions via
Cauchy--Schwarz:
$(x_t^\top v)^2 = (x_t^\top \widehat P_t v)^2 \le R_\cA^2\|\widehat P_t v\|^2$
(valid only on $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, where $x_t = \widehat P_t x_t$),
and then average: $\mathbb E_v\|\widehat P_t v\|^2 = r/d$. The trace
identity sidesteps Cauchy--Schwarz entirely:
$\mathbb E_v[(x_t^\top v)^2] = \|x_t\|^2/d$ holds for arbitrary $x_t$,
so the bound applies to every policy with $\|x_t\|\le R_\cA$. The
factor $r$ between the two bounds is the source of the apparent
$\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ restriction in the earlier sketch; with the trace
identity, no such restriction is needed.
\end{remark}

\paragraph{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}.}
\emph{Construction.} Take $K=1$ (single segment of length $T$). Two
candidate instances:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\nu_0:\theta_t\equiv 0$ for all $t\in[T]$.
\item $\nu_1(v):\theta_t\equiv\alpha v$ for all $t\in[T]$, with $v\in\mathbb S^{d-1}$.
\end{itemize}
Both are valid rank-$1$ piecewise low-rank instances (rank-$r$ with
$r=1$). The scale $\alpha>0$ is set below.

\emph{KL via Lemma~\ref{lem:trace_kl_conf}.} The sequential KL chain
rule, applied across $t=1,\dots,T$, gives
\[
\mathrm{KL}(\mathbb P_0^\cH\|\mathbb P_1^\cH(v))
\;=\; \frac{\alpha^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^2}\,
\mathbb E^\pi_{\mathbb P_0}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t=1}^T (x_t^\top v)^2\Bigr].
\]
Picking $v=v^\star$ as in Lemma~\ref{lem:trace_kl_conf} and writing
$Q := m + R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{KL}(\mathbb P_0^\cH\|\mathbb P_1^\cH(v^\star))
\;\le\; \frac{\alpha^2 Q}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^2}.
\label{eq:singleseg_KL_conf}
\end{equation}

\emph{Le Cam two-point.} Set
$\alpha^2 := \sigma_\varepsilon^2/(4Q)$, so
$\mathrm{KL}\le 1/8$ and Pinsker's inequality gives
$d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mathbb P_0^\cH,\mathbb P_1^\cH(v^\star))\le 1/4$.

\emph{Cumulative regret on $\nu_1(v^\star)$.} The optimal action under
$\nu_1(v^\star)$ is $x_t^\star = R_\cA v^\star$ with reward
$\alpha R_\cA$. Probe rounds are not comparable to the optimal arm
(their action $u_t$ is fixed by the policy's probe schedule, drawn from
$\mathcal N(0,I_d)$, and unbounded); they enter the dynamic regret only
through the cost $cm$. Per-round arm-regret is therefore
measured only on exploit rounds, where $\|x_t\|\le R_\cA$. The exploit
per-round regret is
$\Delta_t = \alpha\bigl(R_\cA - v^{\star\top} x_t\bigr)
\ge \alpha\bigl(R_\cA - |v^{\star\top} x_t|\bigr)$, and summing,
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}\Delta_t\Bigr]
\;\ge\; \alpha (T-m) R_\cA
\;-\; \alpha\,\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}[f],
\label{eq:reg_to_alignment_conf}
\end{equation}
where $f(\cH) := \sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}} |v^{\star\top}x_t|$.

\emph{TV transfer.} Because $|v^{\star\top}x_t|\le \|x_t\|\le R_\cA$
on every exploit round, $\|f\|_\infty\le (T-m) R_\cA$. (Probe rounds
are excluded from the sum precisely because $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ is
unbounded.) For any bounded measurable functional $f$ of the
trajectory $\cH$,
$|\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}[f] - \mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}[f]|
\le 2\|f\|_\infty\,d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mathbb P_0^\cH,\mathbb P_1^\cH(v^\star))$
\citep[Lemma~2.1]{tsybakov2009introduction}, hence with
$d_{\mathrm{TV}}\le 1/4$,
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}[f]
\;\le\; \mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}[f]
\;+\; (T-m) R_\cA/2.
\label{eq:tv_transfer_conf}
\end{equation}

\emph{Bound under $\nu_0$.} By Cauchy--Schwarz,
$\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}[f]
\le \sqrt{(T-m)\cdot\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}\!\bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}(v^{\star\top}x_t)^2\bigr]}$.
Lemma~\ref{lem:trace_kl_conf}, equation \eqref{eq:trace_kl_ex_conf}
gives
$\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}(v^{\star\top}x_t)^2]
\le R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$, so
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_0}[f]
\;\le\; \sqrt{(T-m)\cdot R_\cA^2(T-m)/d}
\;=\; R_\cA(T-m)/\sqrt d.
\label{eq:nu0_bound_conf}
\end{equation}

\emph{Combining.} Substituting \eqref{eq:tv_transfer_conf} and
\eqref{eq:nu0_bound_conf} into \eqref{eq:reg_to_alignment_conf},
\[
\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}\Delta_t\Bigr]
\;\ge\; \alpha (T-m) R_\cA \cdot \Bigl(1 - \frac{1}{\sqrt d} - \frac{1}{2}\Bigr).
\]
For $d\ge 16$, $1/\sqrt d\le 1/4$, so the bracket is at least
$1 - 1/4 - 1/2 = 1/4$:
\begin{equation}
\mathbb E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}\Delta_t\Bigr]
\;\ge\; \tfrac{1}{4}\,\alpha (T-m) R_\cA.
\label{eq:reg_clean_conf}
\end{equation}

\emph{Total dynamic regret.} The dynamic regret is
$\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T = cm + \sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{ex}}}\Delta_t$,
and substituting $\alpha = \sigma_\varepsilon/(2\sqrt{Q})$ from
\eqref{eq:singleseg_KL_conf},
\begin{equation}
\sup_\nu\mathbb E^\pi_\nu[\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T]
\;\ge\; cm \;+\; \frac{R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon\,(T-m)}{8\sqrt{Q}}.
\label{eq:dynreg_lower_conf}
\end{equation}

\emph{Optimization over $m$.} The lower bound in
\eqref{eq:dynreg_lower_conf} must hold for every policy choice of $m$,
which is selected by the policy and may take any value in $\{0,\ldots,T\}$.
We handle the cases $m>T/2$ and $m\le T/2$ separately.

\smallskip\noindent
(\textbf{Preliminary case: $m>T/2$.}) The probe-cost term alone gives
$\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T \ge cm > cT/2$. For
$T\ge T_0 := 8(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2}/c^{2}$ (a constant
depending only on $R_\cA,\sigma_\varepsilon,c$), the inequality
$cT^{1/3}/2 \ge c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}$ holds, hence
$cT/2\ge c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3} T^{2/3}$, which already
establishes the claimed rate. Hence in the remainder of the proof we
may assume $m\le T/2$, so that $T-m\ge T/2$.

\smallskip\noindent
(\textbf{Regime I: $R_\cA^2(T-m)/d \le m$, equivalently $Q\le 2m$.})
Then $1/\sqrt{Q}\ge 1/\sqrt{2m}$, and \eqref{eq:dynreg_lower_conf}
combined with $T-m\ge T/2$ gives
$\sup_\nu\mathbb E[\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T]\ge cm + R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon T/(16\sqrt{2}\,\sqrt m)$.
Minimizing the RHS over $m$ at
$m^\star\asymp(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon T/c)^{2/3}$ yields
\[
\sup_\nu\mathbb E[\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T]
\;\ge\; c_1\,c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}T^{2/3}
\]
for an explicit absolute constant $c_1 = 3\cdot 2^{-2/3}/8\approx 0.236$.
The optimum lies inside this regime when
$m^\star\ge R_\cA^2(T-m^\star)/d$, which (for $m^\star\le T/2$) is
implied by $m^\star\ge R_\cA^2 T/d$, i.e., $d\ge R_\cA^2 T/m^\star
\asymp R_\cA^{4/3}(c/\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}\,T^{1/3}$.

\smallskip\noindent
(\textbf{Regime II: $R_\cA^2(T-m)/d > m$.})
Then the second summand of $Q = m + R_\cA^2(T-m)/d$ dominates the
first, and combining with $T-m\le T$ gives the explicit bound
$Q < 2R_\cA^2(T-m)/d \le 2R_\cA^2 T/d$.
Therefore $1/\sqrt{Q}\ge \sqrt{d/(2R_\cA^2 T)}$, and
\eqref{eq:dynreg_lower_conf} together with $T-m\ge T/2$ gives
\[
\sup_\nu\mathbb E[\mathrm{DynReg}^{(c)}_T]
\;\ge\; \frac{R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon (T-m)}{8}\cdot\sqrt{\frac{d}{2R_\cA^2 T}}
\;\ge\; \frac{\sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{dT}}{16\sqrt 2}.
\]
Under $d\ge d_0 T^{1/3}$, this is at least
$\sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{d_0}\,T^{2/3}/(16\sqrt 2)$. Choosing $d_0$
large enough that this lower bound exceeds the Regime~I constant
$c_1\,c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}$ — concretely,
$\sqrt{d_0}\ge 16\sqrt 2\,c_1\,c^{1/3}R_\cA^{2/3}\sigma_\varepsilon^{-1/3}$,
i.e., $d_0\ge 512\,c_1^2\,c^{2/3}R_\cA^{4/3}\sigma_\varepsilon^{-2/3}$
suffices — Regime~II's bound dominates and the combined LB is
$\Omega(c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}T^{2/3})$ in both
regimes.

Finally, the bracket simplification at \eqref{eq:reg_clean_conf} used
$d\ge 16$. To absorb this into the global hypothesis $d\ge d_0 T^{1/3}$,
we set the final $d_0$ to the maximum of the Regime-II-domination
constant $512\,c_1^2\,c^{2/3}R_\cA^{4/3}\sigma_\varepsilon^{-2/3}$ and
$16$. Then $d\ge d_0 T^{1/3}\ge d_0\ge 16$ automatically.\qed

\begin{remark}[Resolution of the policy-class restriction]
\label{rem:lb_class_conf}
This proof resolves a question that the earlier sketch left open: a
non-projecting policy \emph{cannot} beat $T^{2/3}$ in the regime
$d\ge d_0 T^{1/3}$, because the trace identity bounds its expected
exploit-round KL contribution by exactly the same
$\|x_t\|^2/d\le R_\cA^2/d$ term that bounds a projecting policy's. The
intuition that ``off-subspace exploitation along $v$ contributes
additional KL'' is correct only for a \emph{specific} adversarial $v$;
averaging over $v$, the alignment contributes $1/d$ per round
regardless of whether the policy projects. The LB therefore applies
to the full class $\Pi$, which contains all algorithms in our
experiments.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Scope and open directions]
\label{rem:open_lb}
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb} establishes the $T^{2/3}$ probe-acquisition
lower bound for \emph{all} bounded-action policies, matching SPSC's
probe-term rate up to the $d^{2/3}$ prefactor gap discussed in
Remark~\ref{rem:dim_dep}. It does \emph{not} provide a lower bound on
the leading $r\sqrt{KT}$ exploit-regret term. Two extensions are open:
(i) a multi-segment (piecewise-stationary) extension of the probe-cost
LB, which preliminary attempts indicate remains
$\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ but requires additional adaptive-probe-count
handling and full-trajectory TV-transfer arguments; and (ii) matching
$\Omega(r\sqrt{KT})$ outside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, which would require
accounting for the side-observation channel induced by isotropic
Gaussian probes within the standard linear-bandit minimax LB
\citep[Theorem~24.2]{lattimore2020bandit}. Empirically,
ambient-LinUCB-style methods not in $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ do incur
regret $\gtrsim r\sqrt{KT}$ across the $(d,r,K)$ grid, suggesting the
$r\sqrt{KT}$ rate is fundamental even outside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, but
a proof is beyond the scope of this work.
\end{remark}

\section{Sub-Gaussian probes via truncation}
\label{app:truncation}

The concentration results of \S\ref{app:concentration_proof} are
stated for a.s.\ bounded probes $\|u_t\|\le L$, required by matrix
Freedman. The experiments use isotropic Gaussian probes
$u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$, which are sub-Gaussian but not bounded.
The following standard truncation argument resolves the mismatch
without changing the leading-order rate.

\begin{proposition}[Truncation of Gaussian probes]
\label{prop:truncation}
Let $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ and set $L := \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$.
Define the truncation event $\cE := \bigcap_{t\in\cTprobe}\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$.
Then $\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$, and on $\cE$ the analysis of
\S\ref{app:concentration_proof} applies verbatim with
$L = \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$. All subsequent results hold with an
additive $\delta$ added to the failure probability and with $L$ as
above, so the leading $\tilO(\cdot)$ rate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} is unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
For $u\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$, $\|u\|^2$ is $\chi^2_d$; tail bounds
\citep[Lemma~1]{laurent2000adaptive} give
$\Pr(\|u\|^2 > d + 2\sqrt{dx} + 2x)\le e^{-x}$, so choosing
$x = \log(T/\delta)$ yields $\Pr(\|u\|>\sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}
+\sqrt d)\le\delta/T$. Absorbing the $\sqrt d$ offset into $L$ and
taking a union bound over $|\cTprobe|\le T$ probe rounds gives
$\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$. On $\cE$, the almost-sure bound
$\|u_t\|\le L$ used in Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf} holds, so those results apply
directly. The only dependence on $L$ in the regret bound is through
$R_X$ and $C_{\mathrm{sub}}$, which pick up an extra
$\log(T/\delta)$ factor absorbed into $\tilO(\cdot)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Truncation-bias bound]
\label{prop:theta_bound}
The deterministic offset
$\Theta_k := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\cA_t^c\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]$
arising from predictable truncation in Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}
satisfies $\|\Theta_k\|_\op = O(d\sqrt{\delta/T})$, which is
$o(1)$ in any regime with $\delta$ polynomially small in $T$ and is
dominated by the Freedman bound $O(R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k})$
for $m_k = O(T^{2/3})$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}

\emph{Per-round bound.} Write $\cA_t^c \subseteq \{\|u_t\|>L\}\cup\{|y_t|>L_y\}$,
and recall that on $\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$, $\{|y_t|>L_y\}\subseteq\{|\varepsilon_t|>L_\varepsilon\}$
by the triangle inequality (Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf}'s probability
proof). By Cauchy--Schwarz in the operator norm,
\[
  \|\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\cA_t^c\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op
  \;\le\; \E[\|G_t\|_\op^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]^{1/2}\cdot\Pr(\cA_t^c\mid\cH_{t-1})^{1/2}.
\]
For the second-moment term: $\|G_t\|_\op\le|s_t|\,\|u_t\|_2^2$ and
$|s_t|\le y_t^2+\hat\sigma^2\le 2(u_t^\top\theta_t)^2+2\varepsilon_t^2+\hat\sigma^2$.
We compute $\E[s_t^4\mid\cH_{t-1}]$ explicitly. Since $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$
and $\|\theta_t\|\le S_w$, $u_t^\top\theta_t\sim\mathcal N(0,\|\theta_t\|^2)$
with $\|\theta_t\|^2\le S_w^2$; the fourth moment is
$\E[(u_t^\top\theta_t)^4] = 3\|\theta_t\|^4 \le 3 S_w^4$ (constant in $d$).
Similarly $\E[\varepsilon_t^4]\le 3\sigma_\varepsilon^4$ under
sub-Gaussian noise with $\psi_2$-norm $\sigma_\varepsilon$. Expanding
$s_t^4$ via the binomial and using $(a+b+c)^4\le 27(a^4+b^4+c^4)$:
\[
\E[s_t^4\mid\cH_{t-1}]\le 27\bigl(16\E[(u_t^\top\theta_t)^8]+16\E[\varepsilon_t^8]+\hat\sigma^8\bigr)
\le C\bigl(S_w^8+\sigma_\varepsilon^8+\hat\sigma^8\bigr)=:C_s^\prime
\]
(using $\E[(u^\top\theta)^8]=105\|\theta\|^8\le 105 S_w^8$ for Gaussians),
which is a constant in $d$. And $\E[\|u_t\|^8]=d(d+2)(d+4)(d+6)\le C\,d^4$.
By Cauchy--Schwarz,
$\E[\|G_t\|_\op^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]\le\E[s_t^2\|u_t\|^4\mid\cH_{t-1}]
\le\sqrt{\E[s_t^4]\cdot\E[\|u_t\|^8]}\le\sqrt{C_s^\prime\cdot C d^4}=O(d^2)$.
For the probability term,
$\Pr(\cA_t^c\mid\cH_{t-1})\le\Pr(\|u_t\|>L)+\Pr(|\varepsilon_t|>L_\varepsilon)\le 2\delta/T$.
Combining,
\[
  \|\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\cA_t^c\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op
  \;\le\; O(d)\cdot\sqrt{2\delta/T}
  \;=\; O(d\sqrt{\delta/T}).
\]
Averaging over $t\in\cT_k$,
$\|\Theta_k\|_\op\le O(d\sqrt{\delta/T})$, which is $o(1)$ for any
regime of interest ($\delta$ polynomially small in $T$) and in
particular dominated by the Freedman bound
$O(R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k})$ as long as $m_k\le T/d^2$ (the
natural regime for subspace recovery; $m_k\asymp T^{2/3}K^{-2/3}$ in
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}).

Hence the bias offset is absorbed into the $\tilO(\cdot)$ constants
of Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf}.
\end{proof}

The subspace-recovery rate
$\varepsilon_k\le O(1/\sqrt{m_k})+\Delta_\sigma$ therefore holds with
an additional $O(\delta)$ in the failure probability (two contributions
from $\Pr(\cE^c)$ and one from Freedman).

A fully sub-Gaussian argument without truncation is possible
\citep[cf.][]{tropp2015introduction} but yields the same rate.

\section{SPSC-Adaptive: detector and full pseudocode}
\label{app:adaptive_algo}

The main-body Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} (SPSC) assumes oracle segment
boundaries. SPSC-Adaptive (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive} below)
recovers boundaries from probe data via an online change detector;
this appendix specifies the detector form, threshold calibration,
recovery phase, and burn-in, and ties the tuning back to
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}.

\paragraph{Detector.}
At each probe round the algorithm maintains two non-overlapping
rolling-window estimators of the lifted second moment:
$\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}}$ over the last $n$ probes and
$\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}$ over the segment-accumulated probes
preceding that window. The statistic
\begin{equation}
S_t := \bigl\|\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}} - \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}\bigr\|_\op
\label{eq:detector}
\end{equation}
is compared to a threshold $b$; whenever $S_t > b$ the algorithm
declares a change and enters a \textsc{recovery} phase.

\paragraph{Recovery phase.}
Recovery deploys $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$ consecutive probe rounds with
no exploitation. The block-probe design achieves two things: (i) by
Proposition~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf}, $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$
back-to-back probes produce a subspace estimate $\widehat U$ with
projector error
$\|\widehat P - P^\star\|_\op = O(1/\sqrt{m_{\mathrm{relearn}}})$ on a
$1-\delta$ event; (ii) by zeroing out stale buffers it prevents the
windowed ridge estimator from carrying information from the previous
regime into the new one. After $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$ rounds the
algorithm transitions back to \textsc{normal} probe--exploitation
interleaving with probe rate $\mu$.

\paragraph{Threshold calibration and detection delay.}
Under $H_0$ (no change), matrix Freedman applied to the two windows
gives $\Pr(S_t > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}) \le \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}/T$ with
$\eta_{\mathrm{det}} = C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(dT/\delta_{\mathrm{FA}})/n}$;
choosing $b = 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}$ and union-bounding gives at most
$\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$ false alarms across the horizon. Under $H_1$ at
time $\tau_k$, a separation $\Delta_k \ge 2b$ forces a fire within
delay $D_{\max} = 2n/\mu + \tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$ — the $2n/\mu$ term is
the time for the recent window to refill with post-change probes, and
$\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$ is a short arm-in period after recovery that
prevents the detector from firing on its own freshly-reset state.
Since probes arrive as Bernoulli$(\mu)$, the recent window of size
$W_{\mathrm{det}}$ requires $W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu$ \emph{rounds} on
average to refill with post-change probes; hence the delay is
$D_k = O(W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu + \tau_{\mathrm{burn}})$ rounds and the
overall regret overhead is $2R_\cA S_w\sum_k D_k = O(K W_{\mathrm{det}}/\mu)$.
Under the theorem's probe-rate scaling $\mu = \Theta((K/T)^{1/3})$,
this is
$O(K^{2/3}T^{1/3}W_{\mathrm{det}})$, lower-order than the leading
$\tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ whenever $W_{\mathrm{det}} = o((T/K)^{1/3})$.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{\textbf{SPSC-Adaptive}: unknown boundaries via CUSUM-style detection}
\label{alg:spsc_adaptive}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State \textbf{Input:} probe rate $\mu\in(0,1)$, detection window $n$,
       threshold $b$, burn-in $\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$, relearning budget $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$, $W$, $\lambda$.
\State $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{recovery}$;\ $\mathtt{rec}\leftarrow 0$;\ $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}\leftarrow 0$.
\For{$t=1,\dots,T$}
  \If{$\mathtt{phase} = \textsc{recovery}$}\Comment{block-probe to rebuild subspace}
      \State probe: draw $u_t$, play $x_t=u_t$, observe $y_t$; update $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}$; $\mathtt{rec}\mathrel{+}= 1$.
      \If{$\mathtt{rec} = m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$}
          \State $\widehat U\leftarrow \TopEig_r(\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}/m_{\mathrm{relearn}})$;\ reset buffers;\ $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{normal}$.
      \EndIf
  \Else\Comment{interleave probes at rate $\mu$}
      \If{Bernoulli($\mu$) probe round}
          \State update lifted estimator $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}$ and refresh rolling windows.
          \If{$S_t > b$ (Equation~\ref{eq:detector}) and detector is armed}
              \State $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{recovery}$;\ $\mathtt{rec}\leftarrow 0$;\ reset.
          \EndIf
      \Else
          \State windowed projected ridge-UCB step as in Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} (exploit branch).
      \EndIf
  \EndIf
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Tuning used in experiments.}
Across all experiments reported in \S\ref{sec:experiments} and the
appendix we use $\mu=0.1$, $n=W_{\mathrm{det}}=50$,
$\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}=100$, $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}=30$, and
$b$ set from the CUSUM threshold $\mathtt{cusum\_threshold}=3.0$.
These are held fixed across datasets and $(d,r)$ cells; no
per-dataset retuning is done. For the experiments' scale
$T=5{,}000$ and $K=10$, the theorem's asymptotic scaling
$\mu\asymp(K/T)^{1/3}$ predicts $\mu^\star\approx 0.126$, so the
empirical $\mu=0.1$ is within a constant factor of the theoretical
optimum; the experiments therefore realise the $K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$
rate without per-problem tuning. Sensitivity to the probe rate $\mu$
and the detection window $n$ is reported in
\S\ref{app:sensitivity}.

\paragraph{Unknown $K$ via doubling.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive} tunes the probe rate as
$\mu = c_\mu(K/T)^{1/3}$ and therefore requires $K$ (or an
upper bound). For unknown $K$ a standard doubling argument applies:
run SPSC-Adaptive in epochs of doubling length $T_j = 2^j T_0$, at
each epoch using the conservative guess $K_j = $ number of detector
firings observed so far, plus a slack of $1$ for an as-yet-unfired
imminent change. Each epoch's regret is at most
$\tilO(d^{2/3}K_j^{1/3}T_j^{2/3})$ by Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}
(applied to the realised $K_j$); summing the geometric series and
using $K_j\le K$ at every epoch yields a final bound of order
$\tilO(d^{2/3}K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ with the same dependence as the
known-$K$ case, at the cost of a $\log_2(T/T_0)$ overhead in
constants. Detector firings persist across epochs (the subspace
estimate carries over), so the doubling rule only refreshes the
probe-rate schedule.

\section{Experimental setup}
\label{app:setup}

\paragraph{Synthetic phase-transition benchmark.}
$\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ with piecewise-constant orthonormal
$B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$. Per segment, latent state evolves as
$w_t = A_k w_{t-1} + \eta_{t-1}$ with spectral radius $\rho(A_k) = 0.99$
and Gaussian innovation $\eta_{t-1}\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 0.04^2 I_r)$. Reward
noise $\varepsilon_t\sim\mathcal{N}(0,0.09)$; $40$ actions per round drawn
i.i.d.\ uniformly on the unit sphere. $K=10$ segments of equal length
over $T=5{,}000$. Probe distribution $Q$ isotropic Gaussian,
probe cost $c=0.1$, probe period $50$.

\paragraph{UCI datasets (Covertype, Pendigits, Satimage).}
Features are the raw attribute vectors $\phi(x)\in\R^{d_0}$ reduced to
ambient dimension $d$ via Gaussian random projection
$R\in\R^{d\times d_0}$. For Covertype, segments correspond to disjoint
size-$K$ cover-type subsets of the 7 classes; for Pendigits and
Satimage, to disjoint digit/class subsets, $K=10$. Actions at round
$t$ are $|\cA_t|=40$ examples drawn from the active segment's class
pool. Reward is centred target label. $\lambda=0.01$, probe
period $10$, window $W=400$, $\delta=0.05$, $c=0.02$.

\paragraph{Pixelized MNIST / Fashion-MNIST.}
Images flattened, pixel-normalized, then projected to $d$ via random
projection. Segments are class-subset swaps as above. Same
hyperparameters as UCI datasets.

\paragraph{MovieLens-100K with real ratings.}
Standard MovieLens-100K user-item matrix centred by per-item mean.
Features $\phi(\text{user},\text{item})$ are item-side latent
factors from a rank-$d$ SVD of the observed-entry mask; reward is
the raw user rating ($1$--$5$), centred. Segments correspond to
disjoint time-bucketed user cohorts, $K=10$. This is a
\emph{fully real} benchmark, we do \emph{not} generate rewards from
a low-rank surrogate, so the data is only approximately low-rank and
provides a stringent stress test. $T=5{,}000$, same hyperparameters.

\paragraph{Warfarin clinical dosing.}
IWPC cohort statistics (${\sim}5{,}700$ patients); $d=93$ features
(demographics, VKORC1/CYP2C9 genotype, medications, interactions).
Reward is negative absolute deviation from the therapeutic dose,
$K=8$ patient-cohort segments, $T=5{,}000$, $\lambda=1.0$, probe
period $10$, $W=200$, $c=0.1$, 10 seeds.

\paragraph{Baselines and hyperparameters.}
All baselines inherit $\lambda,\delta$ from SPSC; D-LinUCB uses discount
$0.998$; SW-LinUCB uses window $W$; Restart-LinUCB restarts every
$T/K$ rounds (oracle boundaries); Oracle-LinUCB knows $B_k^\star$.
LowOFUL and VOFUL were designed for fixed low-rank representations;
to give them the best chance under our piecewise setting, each runs
online PCA on action-reward outer products $\sum_t(y_tx_t)(y_tx_t)^\top$
with a projector-change-triggered buffer reset (the strongest
nonstationary adaptation we could give them). Experiments use NumPy/SciPy
only and run in 6--20\,h on a single 16-core CPU.

(Figure~\ref{fig:d-scaling}): SPSC's regret is approximately
\emph{constant in $d$} over an order of magnitude in $d$, far below
the theoretical $d^{2/3}\approx d^{0.67}$ rate.  This is direct
empirical evidence that the $d^{2/3}$ in the bound is an artefact of
worst-case truncated-Gaussian Freedman bounding rather than a
fundamental scaling, and that closing the LB/UB gap in $d$ that
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb} leaves open is a concrete avenue for future
analytical work.

\section{Warfarin: random-subspace ablation}
\label{app:warfarin_ablation}

To attribute SPSC's Warfarin gain to its components, we replace
$\widehat U_t$ with a fresh random rank-$r$ projection each segment
and rerun. On $d{=}93$ across $r\in\{1,2,3,5,10\}$ (10 seeds), the
random-subspace variant retains \emph{about half} of the SPSC-vs-LinUCB
gap; the learned subspace captures $2$--$6\times$ more signal variance
than a random rank-$r$ projection. Conclusion: roughly half the gain
is dimensionality reduction, half is the learned subspace itself.

\section{BOSS/Jedra adaptation: experimental setup}
\label{app:boss_jedra_detail}

BOSS~\citep{duong2024boss} and Jedra~\citep{pmlr-v235-jedra24a} are
stationary low-rank methods. To compare fairly with SPSC we restart
each at the \emph{true} segment boundaries (an oracle advantage SPSC
does not get) and otherwise use the authors' recommended defaults.
The benchmark is piecewise LDS, $K{=}10$ segments, $T{=}5{,}000$,
$\sigma_\varepsilon{=}0.3$, 40 actions per round, 10 seeds, probe
cost $c{=}0.1$. We sweep an $8$-cell $(d,r)$ grid:
$d\in\{55,105,200\}\times r\in\{5,10,20\}$ excluding $(d{=}55,r{=}20)$.
Representative cells are reported inline in \S\ref{sec:exp_boss_jedra};
SPSC is best in every cell of the full grid. The relative SPSC advantage is largest at small $d$ ($16$--$20\%$ at $d{=}55$) and
narrows with increasing $d$ ($6$--$8\%$ at $d{=}200$), because in
large-$d$ regimes BOSS/Jedra's oracle-restart-plus-per-segment-PCA
becomes a competitive alternative to SPSC's probe-based online
estimator. SPSC retains a uniform lead because it never requires
oracle boundaries.
