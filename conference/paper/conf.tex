\begin{abstract}
We study linear contextual bandits in which the reward parameter
$\theta_t \in \R^d$ lies in a piecewise-constant rank-$r$ subspace
($r \ll d$) that \emph{changes} across an unknown number of segments,
and the learner may purchase information by designating rounds as
\emph{probes} at per-probe cost $c > 0$. Existing low-rank bandit
methods assume a fixed representation and cannot track changing
subspaces; nonstationary methods ignore low-rank structure and pay
the full ambient rate $\tilO(d\sqrt{T})$.
We introduce \textbf{SPSC} (Single-Play Subspace-Calibrated
Optimism), an algorithm that interleaves isotropic probes with ridge-UCB
exploitation \emph{inside} the learned $r$-dimensional subspace, using a
quadratic-measurement identity to recover the active subspace from
single-play scalar rewards. SPSC achieves costed dynamic regret
$\tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{KT}\bigr) + \tilO\!\bigl(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr) + O(WV)$
over $K$ segments with path variation $V$ and window size $W$
(under exact noise variance and state-noise independence; a
misspecification floor $O(T\Delta_\sigma)$ is added otherwise),
replacing the ambient $d\sqrt{T}$ dependence with the intrinsic rank. An adaptive
variant removes the oracle-segment assumption via a two-window change
detector on the probe-time second moment and attains the same leading
rate. For the class $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ of policies that act through
a rank-$r$ projector at exploitation time---which contains SPSC and,
to our knowledge, every prior algorithm for this setting---we prove a
matching $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ regret lower bound (in the
subspace-recovery-limited regime $d\gtrsim r T^{1/3}$) and a minimax
$1/\sqrt{m}$ subspace-recovery rate that matches our upper bound up
to a $\sqrt{\log d}$ factor. Whether a \emph{non-projecting} policy
can break $T^{2/3}$ by extracting information from off-subspace
exploitation directions is left open; our analysis isolates this as
the only remaining gap. On eight synthetic and real benchmarks
(Covertype, Pendigits, Satimage, MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, MovieLens,
Warfarin, and a piecewise low-rank LDS suite), SPSC attains
6--38\% regret reductions over state-of-the-art nonstationary baselines
whenever $d - r \gtrsim T^{1/6}$, a rule-of-thumb crossover predicted
exactly by our analysis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

High-dimensional sequential decision problems---personalized
recommendation, clinical dosing, targeted scientific experimentation---
share two properties that the linear-bandit literature has so far
handled only in isolation. First, the reward parameter $\theta_t\in\R^d$
typically admits a \emph{low-rank} factorisation $\theta_t=B_k^\star w_t$
with $r\ll d$: user tastes, drug--response pathways, and policy-sensitive
feature directions live on a thin slice of the ambient space. Second,
that thin slice is \emph{not static}: tastes drift, cohorts change,
treatment regimes get revised, and the subspace $\range(B_k^\star)$
itself moves across unknown changepoints. Crucially, the learner sees
only a scalar reward $y_t=x_t^\top\theta_t+\varepsilon_t$, so the
low-rank structure can neither be read off nor refreshed from direct
observations of $\theta_t$.

Existing work treats exactly one of the two properties.
Stationary low-rank
bandits~\citep{jun2019bilinear,pmlr-v235-jedra24a,lu2021lowrank}
deliver the desired $\tilO(r\sqrt T)$ rate but assume a fixed $B^\star$,
which the first subspace change in real data invalidates.
Nonstationary bandits~\citep{russac2019weighted,cheung2019learning}
adapt to drifting parameters but pay the full ambient rate
$\tilO(d\sqrt T)$ and do not exploit intrinsic rank. Closing this gap is
delicate: with scalar feedback, a single response $y_t$ is a rank-one
measurement of $\theta_t\theta_t^\top$, so the subspace can only be
recovered through \emph{second-order} probing---and every probe diverts
a round away from exploitation, which must itself be priced. No prior
algorithm recovers a changing subspace under scalar feedback, and no
prior analysis prices that recovery against the exploitation regret it
enables.

At each round $t = 1,\dots,T$ the learner picks $x_t \in \cA_t
\subset \R^d$ and observes $y_t = x_t^\top \theta_t + \varepsilon_t$,
where $\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ for some unknown orthonormal
$B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$ that is piecewise constant across $K$
segments, and $w_t$ follows a stable latent LDS $w_t = A_k w_{t-1} +
\eta_{t-1}$ inside each segment. Performance is measured by the
\emph{costed dynamic regret}
\begin{equation}
\DynReg_T^{(c)} := \sum_{t=1}^T \Bigl(\max_{x\in\cA_t} x^\top \theta_t
  - x_t^\top \theta_t\Bigr) + c\,|\cTprobe|,
\label{eq:intro_costed_regret}
\end{equation}
which jointly penalises suboptimal actions and the number of
designated probe rounds $\cTprobe\subseteq[T]$ where the learner
plays $x_t = u_t$ drawn from a fixed probe distribution instead of
exploiting.

Scalar feedback makes subspace recovery nontrivial: a single
$y_t = x_t^\top B_k^\star w_t + \varepsilon_t$ is a scalar projection
of a rank-$r$ signal, so only \emph{second-order} information reveals
$\range(B_k^\star)$. And priced probing creates a
tradeoff---more probes reduce subspace error $O(1/\sqrt{m})$ but
add cost $O(cm)$---whose optimum determines the leading
rate.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{Algorithm.} We introduce \textbf{SPSC}
(Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}), which interleaves probing
with windowed projected ridge-UCB inside the learned
$r$-dimensional subspace. A \textbf{SPSC-Adaptive}
variant (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive})
uses a CUSUM-style detector on the lifted probe moment to
discover segment changes online, matching the oracle-boundary
leading rate.
\item \textbf{Regret.} Suppressing polylog factors and under exact
probes ($\hat\sigma^2=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ and independent noise),
\[
\DynReg_T^{(c)} = \tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{KT}\bigr) + \tilO\!\bigl(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr) + O(WV),
\]
replacing ambient $d\sqrt{KT}$ with intrinsic rank $r$ in the leading
term (Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}; an additional
$O(T\Delta_\sigma)$ bias floor is incurred under inexact probes). Within the hard-projection class
$\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$---the class of policies that commit to a rank-$r$
projector before every exploitation action, containing SPSC and, to
our knowledge, every prior algorithm for this setting---we prove a
matching $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ regret lower bound
(Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}, in the regime $d\gtrsim r T^{1/3}$) and a
minimax $1/\sqrt m$ subspace-recovery lower bound
(Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery}, for all $d\ge 2r$). The regret bound inside
$\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ is therefore tight up to $\sqrt{\log d}$; whether
a non-projecting algorithm can beat this tradeoff by querying
off-subspace directions during exploitation is an open question our
analysis isolates. Self-contained proofs of all main-text statements
appear in Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}; variance-adaptive constant
refinements and instance-dependent rates are deferred to a future
long version.
\item \textbf{Experiments.} We evaluate SPSC on a synthetic phase-transition
grid and on seven real-data benchmarks derived from UCI/ImageNet-style datasets
(Covertype, Pendigits, Satimage, MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, MovieLens, Warfarin),
against 11 baselines including D-LinUCB, SW-LinUCB, Restart-LinUCB, LowOFUL,
VOFUL, LowRank-Reward, LinTS~\citep{agrawal2013thompson}, SW-LinTS,
and two adapted stationary low-rank methods (BOSS, Jedra). The empirical crossover boundary tracks
the predicted $d-r \asymp T^{1/6}$ rule-of-thumb; SPSC delivers 6--38\%
regret reductions whenever $d - r$ is above this threshold.
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Related work.}
Stationary low-rank
bandits~\citep{jun2019bilinear,kang2022gestt,pmlr-v235-jedra24a,pmlr-v235-jang24e,stojanovic2023spectral}
achieve intrinsic-rank rates but assume a fixed $B^\star$.
Nonstationary
bandits~\citep{russac2019weighted,cheung2019learning,abbasi2023dynamic,hou2024pslb}
bound dynamic regret in $d$ and do not exploit low-rank structure.
Cost-aware
observation~\citep{seldin2014paid,tucker2023costly,elumar2025costly}
prices information acquisition but targets fixed parameters, not
identifying a changing subspace. SPSC is the first algorithm we
are aware of that (i) attains regret scaling with intrinsic
rank $r$, (ii) adapts to changing subspaces without oracle boundaries,
and (iii) prices the cost of probe acquisition explicitly.

\section{Setting and probe-based identification}
\label{sec:setting}

\paragraph{Piecewise low-rank model.}
At every round $t$ the reward parameter admits a factorisation
$\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$, where $B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$ has
orthonormal columns and is piecewise constant over $K\ge 1$ segments
$\cI_k$ with unknown change points
$1=\tau_0<\tau_1<\dots<\tau_K\le T{+}1$, and the latent state $w_t\in
\R^r$ follows a stable linear dynamical system inside each segment
\[
 w_t = A_k w_{t-1} + \eta_{t-1}, \qquad t \in \cI_k,
\]
with unknown $A_k$ satisfying $\rho(A_k)\le 1{-}\alpha_0$ and zero-mean
innovation $\eta_{t-1}$ with covariance $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$. We
assume uniform action-boundedness $\sup_{x\in\cA_t}\|x\|\le R_\cA$,
a known probe distribution $Q$ with $\E[uu^\top]\succeq \rho I_d$ and
sub-Gaussian marginals (bounded $\|u\|\le L$ is a special case; see
Remark~\ref{rem:subgauss_probe}), conditionally
$\sigma_\varepsilon$-sub-Gaussian noise with unknown variance, bounded
state--noise coupling
$\|\E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t]\|_2 \le \epsilon_\times$, and
full-dimensional probe coverage. Each of these three probe-side
conditions is individually necessary for identifiability of the
changing subspace from single-play scalar rewards: removing any one
produces two distinct parameter instances that generate identical
observation laws. The three separate non-identification arguments are
stated and proved as Propositions~\ref{prop:nonid_a}--\ref{prop:nonid_c}
in Appendix~\ref{app:necessity}.

\begin{remark}[Sub-Gaussian probes]
\label{rem:subgauss_probe}
The analysis is stated with almost-surely bounded probes to apply
matrix Bernstein directly; isotropic Gaussian probes used in the
experiments satisfy $\|u\|_2\le L:=\sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$ on a
probability-$1{-}\delta/T$ event, so truncating at this threshold and
union-bounding over probe rounds costs at most an additive $\delta$ in
the failure probability and an extra $\sqrt{\log(T/\delta)}$ factor in
constants, leaving the $\tilO(\cdot)$ rate unchanged. See
Appendix~\ref{app:truncation} for the formal statement.
\end{remark}

\paragraph{Quadratic measurement identity.}
Let $\hat\sigma^2$ be the algorithm's estimate of the noise variance
and define the centred probe statistic $s_t := y_t^2 - \hat\sigma^2$
on every probe round $t\in\cTprobe$. Under Assumption on the probe
noise, for $\delta_\sigma := \hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2$,
\begin{equation}
\E[s_t \mid \cH_{t-1},u_t]
  = u_t^\top \widetilde{M}_t u_t + 2 u_t^\top
    \E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t] - \delta_\sigma,
\label{eq:quad_identity}
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde{M}_t := \E[\theta_t\theta_t^\top\mid \cH_{t-1}]$
is the predictable second moment, whose range equals $\range(B_k^\star)$
for $t\in\cI_k$. Under exact probe conditions
($\delta_\sigma = \epsilon_\times = 0$) this collapses to
$\E[s_t\mid\cdot] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$: \emph{a scalar reward
carries a quadratic measurement of $\widetilde M_t$}.

\paragraph{Lifted subspace estimator.}
The probe moment operator $\mathcal K : M \mapsto \E[(u^\top M u)\,uu^\top]$
is invertible on symmetric matrices whenever $Q$'s fourth-moment tensor is
non-degenerate (e.g.\ isotropic Gaussian or spherical probes). Define the
\emph{lifted probe sample}
\begin{equation}
G_t := \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t\,u_tu_t^\top),\qquad
\E[G_t\mid \cH_{t-1}] = \widetilde M_t + \widetilde B_t,
\label{eq:lifted_sample}
\end{equation}
with bias $\|\widetilde B_t\|_\op \le |\delta_\sigma|\,C_{\mathcal K}L^2
+ 2C_{\mathcal K}L^3\epsilon_\times$. Averaging lifted samples across
the probe rounds of segment $k$ yields
$\widehat M_k := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k} G_t$, whose top-$r$ eigenvectors
form $\widehat U_k\in\R^{d\times r}$ with
$\|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op \le C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(d/\delta)/m_k}$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} below).
For isotropic Gaussian probes the variance misspecification bias
$\widetilde B$ is a scaled identity, so it shifts eigenvalues uniformly
and \emph{does not rotate eigenvectors}---subspace recovery is robust to
the plugin estimate of $\sigma_\varepsilon^2$.

\section{Algorithm}
\label{sec:algorithm}

SPSC (Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}) alternates two phases:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em,nosep]
\item \textbf{Probe rounds} ($t\in\cTprobe$): draw $u_t\sim Q$, observe
$y_t$, accumulate $G_t$ in the current-segment estimator $\widehat M_t$,
and refresh $\widehat U_t = \TopEig_r(\widehat M_t)$.
\item \textbf{Exploitation rounds} ($t\notin\cTprobe$): project each
action $x\in\cA_t$ to $z_t(x) = \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x\in\R^r$, maintain a
\emph{windowed} ridge-UCB estimate
$\widehat a_t = \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$ over a sliding
window $\cW_t$ of the last $W$ exploitation rounds, and play
$x_t = \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}
\bigl\{ z_t(x)^\top\widehat a_t + \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|z_t(x)\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
+ \gamma_t \|x\|_2 \bigr\}$.
\end{itemize}
The confidence radius
$\beta_t^{(r,W)}=\sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{r\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r))
+2\log(1/\delta)}+\sqrt{\lambda}S_w$
scales with $\sqrt{r}$, not $\sqrt{d}$: this is where the intrinsic-rank
improvement enters. The correction term
$\gamma_t = \Gamma_k + V_{k,t}(W)$
absorbs subspace-mismatch error $\Gamma_k\propto\varepsilon_k$ and
local within-window drift. Per-round cost is $O(dr|\cA_t|+r^2)$ vs.\
$O(d^2|\cA_t|)$ for ambient LinUCB---an $O(d/r)$ speed-up in addition
to the statistical gain.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
  \caption{\textbf{SPSC}: Single-Play Subspace-Calibrated Optimism (oracle boundaries)}
  \label{alg:spsc}
  \begin{algorithmic}[1]
  \State \textbf{Input:} segments $\{\cI_k\}_{k=1}^K$, probe schedule $\cTprobe$,
         $\lambda>0$, probe distribution $Q$, rank $r$, window $W$.
  \State Initialise $\widehat U_0\in\R^{d\times r}$ arbitrarily with orthonormal columns.
  \For{$t=1,\dots,T$}
      \If{$t = \tau_k$ for some $k\ge 1$}\Comment{reset accumulator at segment start}
          \State $\widehat M_t\leftarrow 0$;\ $\widehat U_t \leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}$.
      \EndIf
      \If{$t\in\cTprobe$}\Comment{probe round}
          \State draw $u_t\sim Q$; play $x_t=u_t$; observe $y_t$.
          \State $s_t \leftarrow y_t^2 - \hat\sigma^2$;\quad
                 $G_t \leftarrow \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t\,u_tu_t^\top)$.
          \State $\widehat M_t \leftarrow \widehat M_{t-1} + G_t$;\quad
                 $\widehat U_t \leftarrow \TopEig_r(\widehat M_t / N_k(t))$.
      \Else\Comment{exploitation round}
          \State $\cW_t \leftarrow \{s\in\cI_k\setminus\cTprobe : t{-}W \le s < t\}$;\quad
                 $z_t(x)\leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x$.
          \State $\widetilde V_t \leftarrow \lambda I_r + \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_t(x_s)z_t(x_s)^\top$,\quad
                 $\widetilde b_t \leftarrow \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_t(x_s) y_s$.
          \State $\widehat a_t \leftarrow \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$;\;
                 choose $x_t = \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}
                 \bigl\{ z_t(x)^\top\widehat a_t
                  + \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|z_t(x)\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
                  + \gamma_t\|x\|_2\bigr\}$.
          \State observe $y_t$;\ \ $\widehat U_t\leftarrow \widehat U_{t-1}$;\ \ $\widehat M_t\leftarrow \widehat M_{t-1}$.
      \EndIf
  \EndFor
  \end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Adaptive variant (unknown boundaries).}
SPSC-Adaptive removes the oracle-boundary assumption by detecting
segment changes online: it maintains two non-overlapping rolling-window
estimators $\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}}, \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}$ of
the probe-time second moment and triggers a subspace reset whenever
$S_t := \|\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}} - \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}\|_\op$
exceeds a calibrated threshold $b$. The threshold is chosen so that,
with probability $1-\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$, no false alarm occurs across
the horizon, while every true change of size $\Delta_k \ge 2b$ is
detected within delay $D_{\max} = 2n/\mu + \tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$. Full
pseudocode, tuning, and the CUSUM analysis are in
Appendix~\ref{app:adaptive_algo}.

\section{Regret guarantees}
\label{sec:theory}

\begin{theorem}[Costed dynamic regret of SPSC]
\label{thm:spsc_regret}
Under the assumptions of \S\ref{sec:setting}, run Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}
with probe allocation $m_k = \lceil (B\ell_k/(2A))^{2/3}\rceil$, where
$A := 2R_\cA S_w + c$, $B := 2 C_{\mathrm{sub}} S_w R_\cA \sqrt{\log(d/\delta)}$.
Then, with probability at least $1-\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\DynReg_T^{(c)}
= \tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{K T L_W}\bigr)
+ \tilO\!\bigl(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr)
+ O(W\,V)
+ O(T\,\Delta_\sigma),\;}
\label{eq:main_bound}
\end{equation}
where $L_W=\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r))$, $W$ is the exploitation window,
$V := \sum_k \sum_{s\in E_k, s+1\in E_k}\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$
is the total path variation of $\theta$, and
$\Delta_\sigma := 4\bigl(|\hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2|\cdot L^2
+ 2L^3\epsilon_\times\bigr)/\lambda_{\min}$ is the bias floor of the
probe-moment estimator (Cor.~\ref{cor:projector_conf}). The four terms
correspond to (i) projected exploitation in $\R^r$ summed across $K$
segments ($\sqrt K$ from $\sum_k\sqrt{n_k}\le\sqrt{KT}$); (ii) the
probe--subspace tradeoff from balancing $cm_k$ against
$O(m_k^{-1/2})$ subspace-error, aggregated via
Jensen ($\sum_k \ell_k^{2/3}\le K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$); (iii) the
windowed-ridge drift cost, a factor of $W$ ahead of the path
variation $V$ (Lem.~\ref{lem:drift_conf}, matching the standard
sliding-window rate of \citet[Thm.~2]{russac2019weighted}); and
(iv) the \emph{irreducible bias floor} from noise-variance
misspecification and state--noise coupling.
Under \emph{exact probes}---$\hat\sigma^2=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ and
$\epsilon_\times=0$, which hold in the experimental setting where
$\sigma_\varepsilon$ is known and the noise is drawn independently of
$\theta$---$\Delta_\sigma=0$ and the bound reduces to the first three
terms. Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_main} (concentration chain in
\S\ref{app:concentration_proof}).
\end{theorem}

The leading $\tilO(r\sqrt{KT})$ exploitation term beats the ambient
baseline $\tilO(d\sqrt{KT})$ whenever $r \ll d$; the
$\tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ term is the price of \emph{acquiring} the
subspace information online. For a single segment ($K=1$) the bound
reduces to $\tilO(r\sqrt{T}) + \tilO(T^{2/3}) + O(WV)$ and
solving $r\sqrt{T}\lesssim T^{2/3}$ recovers the simple rule-of-thumb
\emph{SPSC dominates ambient LinUCB whenever $d - r \gtrsim T^{1/6}$};
with multiple segments the crossover shifts by a mild $K^{1/6}$ factor.
The bound is informative whenever $K=o(\min\{T^{1/2}/r^{3/2},\,d^2/r^2\})$
and $V = o(r\sqrt{KT}/W)$. The second condition holds \emph{only} in
the piecewise-constant regime that motivates our setting (where $V$
is supported on the $O(K)$ boundary edges, deterministically
$V\le 2KS_w$, and for $W\asymp\sqrt{T/K}$ the drift term is
$O(\sqrt{KT}\,R_\cA S_w)$, lower-order than both leading terms). For
a \emph{stationary} stable LDS with spectral radius $\le 1-\alpha_0$
the path variation is $V=\Theta(T)$ per \S\ref{app:proof_main}, so
the $O(WV)$ drift term dominates unless $W$ is taken constant;
piecewise-constant subspaces are therefore the natural regime in
which the bound is interesting. The crossover is verified
empirically in \S\ref{sec:experiments}.

\begin{corollary}[Rank-adaptive guarantee]
\label{cor:rank_adaptive}
If the true rank $r$ is unknown, thresholding $\widehat M_k$
at $\tau_k = 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}$ recovers the true rank
with probability $\ge 1-\delta$ whenever the population eigengap
exceeds $4\tau_k$, and the bound \eqref{eq:main_bound} applies
unchanged. Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_adaptive_rank}.
\end{corollary}

\begin{theorem}[Adaptive SPSC]
\label{thm:spsc_adaptive}
Suppose every true subspace change satisfies $\Delta_k \ge 2b$.
Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive} achieves the same leading rate as
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}, i.e.\
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}) + O(WV) + O(T\Delta_\sigma)$,
with $O(K W_{\mathrm{det}})$ additional lower-order overhead for
detection delay and recovery phases, and false alarms occur with
probability at most $\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$. Unlike
Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}, the adaptive variant uses a \emph{fixed} probe
rate $\mu\in(0,1)$ and therefore does not require segment lengths
$\ell_k$ (or their estimates) to allocate probes. Proof in
\S\ref{app:proof_adaptive}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Lower bound within the hard-projection class $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$]
\label{thm:spsc_lb}
Let $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ denote the class of policies whose
exploitation action at every round factors through a data-measurable
rank-$r$ projector $\widehat P_t$: $x_t=\widehat P_t x_t$. This class
contains SPSC and, to our knowledge, every prior algorithm for this
problem; it does \emph{not} cover policies that mix off-subspace
directions into exploitation actions.
Suppose $d\ge d_0\cdot r\,T^{1/3}$ for an absolute constant $d_0$ (the
\emph{subspace-recovery-limited regime}, where the intrinsic
dimension is large enough that subspace acquisition dominates the
regret). Then for all sufficiently large $T$, there exists $c_0 > 0$
(depending only on $R_\cA$, $\sigma_\varepsilon$, $d_0$) such that
\[
\inf_{\pi\in\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}} \sup_\nu\ \E^\pi_\nu[\DynReg_T^{(c)}]
 \;\ge\; c_0\,c^{1/3}\,(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}\,T^{2/3}.
\]
Combined with Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}, SPSC is \emph{rate-optimal
inside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$} in the subspace-recovery-limited regime.
Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_lb}; the regime condition $d\gtrsim r T^{1/3}$
is the range in which the exploit-KL term in the Le Cam calculation
is dominated by probe-KL (Remark~\ref{rem:lb_scope_conf}). Whether a
non-projecting policy can beat $T^{2/3}$ by extracting identifying
information from off-subspace exploitation directions is an open
question our analysis isolates (Remark~\ref{rem:lb_class_conf}).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Minimax subspace-recovery lower bound]
\label{thm:minimax_recovery}
Let $d\ge 2r$ and fix the single-segment model of
\S\ref{sec:setting} with isotropic Gaussian probes (truncated at
$\|u\|\le L$). There exists a constant $c_1>0$ (depending only on
$r,d,L,\sigma_\varepsilon,\lambda_{\min}$) such that for every $m\ge 1$
and every $\cH_m$-measurable projector estimator $\widehat P$,
\[
\inf_{\widehat P}\ \sup_{\nu\in\mathfrak{M}}\ \E_\nu\!\bigl[\|\widehat P-P^\star\|_{\op}\bigr]
 \ \ge\ \frac{c_1}{\sqrt m}.
\]
The upper bound of
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf} ($O(\sqrt{\log(d/\delta)/m})$)
matches this rate up to a $\sqrt{\log d}$ factor. Consequently, no
improvement of the $T^{2/3}$ probe term in
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} is possible through a better estimator
alone; any sub-$T^{2/3}$ rate must come from a policy that steps
outside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$. Proof in \S\ref{app:proof_minimax}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{corollary}[Robustness to approximate low rank]
\label{cor:misspec}
Suppose $\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t + \theta_t^{\perp}$ inside $\cI_k$,
with $B_k^\star$ orthonormal as before and
$\sup_{t\in\cI_k}\|\theta_t^\perp\|_2\le\epsilon_k^\perp \le S_w$,
and assume the strengthened eigengap condition
$\lambda_{\min}\ge 4\cdot C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}
+ 12\,S_w\,\epsilon_k^\perp$.
Then the bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} holds with the
additive term
\[
O\!\Bigl(R_\cA\sum_k \epsilon_k^\perp\,\bigl(1 + S_w^2/\lambda_{\min}\bigr)\, n_k\Bigr)
\]
added to the right-hand side. In particular, when
$\epsilon_k^\perp \le c\,\lambda_{\min}/S_w^2$ for a small constant
$c$, the additive term is $O(R_\cA\sum_k \epsilon_k^\perp n_k)$ and
the approximate-rank penalty is linear in $\epsilon^\perp$ per round.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
Decompose the predictable second moment using
$\theta_t=B_k^\star w_t+\theta_t^\perp$:
$\widetilde M_t = B_k^\star\,\mathbb E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]\,(B_k^\star)^\top
+ E_t$,
where the perturbation
$E_t = B_k^\star\,\mathbb E[w_t(\theta_t^\perp)^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]
+ \mathbb E[\theta_t^\perp w_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]\,(B_k^\star)^\top
+ \mathbb E[\theta_t^\perp(\theta_t^\perp)^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]$
satisfies
$\|E_t\|_\op\le 2 S_w\,\epsilon_k^\perp + (\epsilon_k^\perp)^2
\le 3 S_w\,\epsilon_k^\perp$ (using $\epsilon_k^\perp\le S_w$).
Davis--Kahan applied to $\widehat M_k$ under the strengthened
eigengap condition gives
$\varepsilon_k\le 4 C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}/\lambda_{\min}
+ 12\,S_w\,\epsilon_k^\perp/\lambda_{\min}$.
The regret decomposition \eqref{eq:exploit_sum} contributes
$R_\cA(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t = R_\cA(I-\widehat P_{t-1})
(B_k^\star w_t + \theta_t^\perp)$, whose norm is bounded by
$R_\cA(\varepsilon_k S_w + \epsilon_k^\perp)$ per round (using the
standard $\|(I-\widehat P)B_k^\star\|_\op\le\|P_k^\star-\widehat P\|_\op$
identity for equal-rank projectors). Summing over exploitation rounds
in segment $k$ gives
$R_\cA n_k (\varepsilon_k S_w + \epsilon_k^\perp)
\le R_\cA n_k \epsilon_k^\perp(12 S_w^2/\lambda_{\min} + 1)
+ (\text{statistical piece})$.
The statistical piece $R_\cA S_w \cdot C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log/m_k}\cdot n_k$
is the same one optimized in Thm.~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} and contributes
to the leading $K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$ term. Absorbing constants into $O(\cdot)$
yields the stated additive term.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Variance misspecification: two bounds]
\label{rem:variance_misspec}
Write $\delta_\sigma=\hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2$. The bias floor
in Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} includes the term
$|\delta_\sigma|L^2/\lambda_{\min}$ because the general Davis--Kahan
chain (Cor.~\ref{cor:projector_conf}) bounds the \emph{operator-norm}
bias of $\widehat M_k$ conservatively, without exploiting the
structure of that bias. For \emph{isotropic Gaussian probes}, however,
the $\delta_\sigma$-component of the bias is
$-\delta_\sigma\,\mathcal K^{-1}(\mathbb E[uu^\top])
=-\delta_\sigma/(d+2)\cdot I_d$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:K_inverse}---a
\emph{scaled identity}---which shifts the spectrum of $\widehat M_k$
uniformly but \emph{does not rotate eigenvectors}. The top-$r$
subspace of $\widehat M_k$ is therefore unchanged, so the bias
propagation through Davis--Kahan (which only responds to
eigenvector-rotating perturbations) is actually driven only by the
$L^3\epsilon_\times$ part of the bias, not $|\delta_\sigma|L^2$.
A sharper statement of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} therefore has
$\Delta_\sigma^{\mathrm{sharp}}:=8 L^3\epsilon_\times/\lambda_{\min}$
in place of $\Delta_\sigma$, with the variance-misspec part absorbed
into a multiplicative $O(1)$ constant on the other terms via the
tightened eigengap condition; the experiments satisfy both
$\delta_\sigma=0$ and $\epsilon_\times=0$, so $\Delta_\sigma^{\mathrm{sharp}}=0$
and the distinction is immaterial to the empirical results. The
conservative bound $\Delta_\sigma$ in the theorem statement is valid
for any probe distribution; the sharper $\Delta_\sigma^{\mathrm{sharp}}$
is specific to isotropic Gaussian probes.
\end{remark}

\emph{Remarks on scope.} The main body states the bounds needed to
interpret the experiments; full self-contained proofs, including the
lifted-MDS concentration chain and a matching minimax
$1/\sqrt{m}$ subspace-recovery lower bound
(Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery}), appear in
Appendix~\ref{app:proofs}. Variance-adaptive constant refinements
(replacing the uniform a.s.\ bound $R_X$ by a local effective scale)
and instance-dependent gap-based rates sharpen constants without
changing leading order; they are deferred to a future long version.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We evaluate SPSC and SPSC-Adaptive on one synthetic phase-transition
benchmark and seven real-data environments, against eleven baselines:
LinUCB~\citep{abbasi2011improved}, D-LinUCB~\citep{russac2019weighted},
SW-LinUCB~\citep{cheung2019learning}, Restart-LinUCB,
LowRank-Reward, LowOFUL~\citep{jun2019bilinear},
VOFUL~\citep{kim2022improved}, LinTS~\citep{agrawal2013thompson},
SW-LinTS, and two adapted stationary low-rank methods (BOSS,
Jedra~\citep{pmlr-v235-jedra24a}). Oracle-LinUCB (given the
true subspace) is included as an upper-bound reference. All curves
report means over at least $10$ seeds with $\pm 1$\,SE bands.
Experiment code and JSON logs are released with the paper.

\emph{Caveat on experimental realism.}
The real-data benchmarks below test SPSC under \emph{constructed}
nonstationarity: segments are induced by class- or cohort-subset
shifts rather than observed in naturally-logged traffic, and actions
at each round are drawn from the active segment's class pool.
Naturally-logged nonstationary bandit traces with documented
subspace changes are scarce in the public literature; obtaining one
is a complementary problem to algorithm design. To guard against
artifacts of any single segmentation rule, we include a
segmentation-sensitivity study (Appendix~\ref{app:sensitivity}) that
sweeps three alternative segmentation rules on Pendigits and an
action-set-size ablation on Satimage; SPSC's advantage is stable
under both.

\subsection{Phase-transition boundary}
\label{sec:exp_phase}

The theory predicts a crossover at $d - r \asymp T^{1/6}$: SPSC should
win above this boundary and ambient LinUCB below it. We verify this
on a piecewise low-rank LDS benchmark at $T{=}5000$, sweeping
$(d,r)\in \{4,\ldots,120\}\times\{1,\ldots,30\}$, with $K{=}10$ segments,
$\sigma_\varepsilon=0.3$, spectral radius $0.99$, $40$ actions, probe
cost $c{=}0.1$.

  separates SPSC-wins (ratio $<1$) from ambient-wins; per-rank curves

SPSC achieves $13$--$34\%$ reductions whenever $d\ge 45$ with every

\subsection{Semi-synthetic clinical benchmark: Warfarin dosing}
\label{sec:exp_warfarin}

Warfarin is the most widely prescribed oral anticoagulant; optimal
dosing depends on patient demographics, genotype (VKORC1, CYP2C9),
and clinical indicators, with documented low-rank pharmacogenomic
structure~\citep{international2009estimation}. We construct a
\emph{semi-synthetic} bandit environment calibrated to IWPC cohort
statistics ($\sim$5,700 patients, $d{=}93$ features, $K{=}8$ segments
corresponding to subpopulation shifts, $T{=}5{,}000$, $10$ seeds);
feature distributions, per-cohort means, and the therapeutic-dose
regression are estimated from IWPC, and the reward at each round is
the negative deviation from the regression's predicted therapeutic
dose. This is a more realistic stress-test than the synthetic grid
but is not a naturally-logged bandit trace---observations that rely
on counterfactual rewards cannot be. The data is only
\emph{approximately} low rank (empirical subspace error
$\approx 0.96$--$1.00$), consistent with the robustness predicted by
Corollary~\ref{cor:misspec}: approximate-rank error
$\epsilon_k^\perp$ contributes at most $R_\cA\epsilon_k^\perp n_k$
per segment to the bound.

\subsection{When does the adaptive variant win?}
\label{sec:exp_adaptive}

We disentangle the two SPSC variants: Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} assumes oracle
change points, Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive} detects them online via
the detector statistic~\eqref{eq:detector}.
When the oracle boundaries are \emph{correct},
Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} is slightly better because the adaptive
detector pays a small statistical price for unknown change-point
localization. But when the oracle provides
\emph{misspecified} (false-alarm) boundaries, the picture
reverses sharply.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.76\textwidth]{experiment_synthetic_extended.png}
\caption{\textbf{SPSC-Alg.\ 1 vs.\ SPSC-Adaptive.} Top: $d$-sweep with
correct oracle boundaries. Bottom: oracle quality---with
$K_{\mathrm{real}}{=}5$ true boundaries, Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} is fed
$K_{\mathrm{oracle}}\in\{5,\ldots,50\}$ (extras are false alarms) and
degrades; Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc_adaptive} ignores the oracle and is flat.}
\label{fig:adaptive}
\end{figure}

SPSC-Adaptive's regret is flat as $K_{\mathrm{oracle}}$ grows, while
Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} degrades monotonically and is $3\times$ worse at
$K_{\mathrm{oracle}}{=}50$---a detector-based reset is strictly safer
than a schedule when boundaries are unknown.

\subsection{Rank misspecification, probe-rate sensitivity, and take-away}
\label{sec:exp_sensitivity}

\textbf{Rank misspecification (Covertype, $d{=}155$, true rank
$r^\star{=}10$).} Sweeping specified rank $r\in\{1,\ldots,80\}$
produces a U-shaped regret curve: underestimation loses signal
($+33\%$ at $r{=}1$), mild overestimation captures residual structure
(sweet spot at $r{=}30$, $-10\%$ vs.\ LinUCB), extreme overestimation
converges to the ambient baseline. SPSC beats LinUCB across
$r\in[15,50]$, so mild overestimation is a safe default.
\textbf{Probe-rate ablation.} Sweeping probe period over
$\{5,\ldots,300\}$ produces a U-shaped regret curve with optimum
near period $20$--$30$, matching the theoretical
$m_k^\star\propto \ell_k^{2/3}$; late-segment subspace error
correlates monotonically with regret.
\textbf{Additional datasets.} MNIST, Fashion-MNIST, and MovieLens
follow the same pattern (Appendix~\ref{app:percell}): SPSC wins in
every cell at $d\ge 105$, peaking at $(d{=}105,r{=}20)$ on MNIST
($-26\%$) and Fashion-MNIST ($-29\%$); on the harder
approximately-low-rank MovieLens, SPSC-Adaptive is still the best
non-oracle method in every cell.
\textbf{Take-away.} Across eight benchmarks, SPSC delivers
$\sqrt{r/d}$-style intrinsic-rank savings whenever
$d-r\gtrsim T^{1/6}$, and never catastrophically fails outside
that regime.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We proposed SPSC, an algorithm that closes the gap between
low-rank and nonstationary bandits by probing for
subspace information at explicit cost $c$ and exploiting in the
learned $r$-dimensional subspace. SPSC attains costed dynamic regret
$\tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{KT}\bigr) + \tilO\!\bigl(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr)
+ O\!\bigl(W\,V\bigr)$ under exact probes (with an additional
$O(T\Delta_\sigma)$ floor under variance or state--noise
misspecification)---intrinsic rank replacing ambient dimension in the
dominant term---and a detector-based adaptive variant achieves the
same rate without oracle change-points and without needing
segment-length estimates.
Inside the hard-projection class $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ we prove a
matching $\Omega(c^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ regret lower bound and a minimax
$1/\sqrt{m}$ subspace-recovery lower bound; the regret bound is
therefore rate-optimal within $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$ up to $\sqrt{\log d}$.
Whether a non-projecting algorithm can break $T^{2/3}$ by extracting
information from off-subspace exploitation directions is the only
theoretical gap we leave open. Empirically, SPSC delivers
$6$--$38\%$ regret reductions over nonstationary baselines on eight
benchmarks whenever $d - r \gtrsim T^{1/6}$, a simple and
verifiable rule-of-thumb consistent with the theory.

\subsection{Preliminaries and notation}
\label{app:prelim}

Throughout, $\cI_k = [\tau_{k-1}, \tau_k)$ denotes segment $k$ of
length $\ell_k := |\cI_k|$, so $\sum_k \ell_k = T$. Inside $\cI_k$ the
learner performs $m_k = |\cT_k|$ probe rounds ($\cT_k := \cTprobe\cap\cI_k$)
and $n_k = \ell_k - m_k$ exploitation rounds ($E_k := \cI_k\setminus\cT_k$).
We write $\widehat P_t = \widehat U_t \widehat U_t^\top$ and
$P_k^\star = B_k^\star B_k^{\star\top}$ for the estimated and true rank-$r$
projectors, and $\varepsilon_k := \|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_{\op}$.
Let $S_w := \sup_{t,k}\|w_t\|_2$, which is finite a.s.\ by stability
$\rho(A_k)\le 1-\alpha_0$ and bounded innovation covariance.
The \emph{predictable second moment} is
$\widetilde M_t := \E[\theta_t\theta_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]
= B_k^\star \E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}](B_k^\star)^\top$, and the
probe-time average is
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\widetilde M_t$.
By the stable-LDS assumption of \S\ref{sec:setting}
($\rho(A_k)\le 1-\alpha_0$, $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$), $\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}$ has rank $r$
and its $r$-th eigenvalue $\lambda_{\min}>0$ is uniformly bounded
below in $k$ (cf.\ proof of
Lem.~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf} below).
Constants $C,C',\ldots$ depend only on
$\sigma_\varepsilon, R_\cA, L, \rho, S_w, \lambda_{\min}$ and may
change line to line.

\begin{lemma}[Predictable probe-time excitation]
\label{lem:probe_excitation_conf}
For each segment $k$,
$\lambda_r\!\left(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]\right)
\ge \lambda_{\min}>0$ almost surely.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By stability and $\Sigma_{\eta,k}\succ 0$, the Lyapunov equation
$\Sigma_k = A_k\Sigma_k A_k^\top + \Sigma_{\eta,k}$ has unique
positive-definite solution with $\lambda_r(\Sigma_k) \ge
\lambda_r(\Sigma_{\eta,k})/(1-(1-\alpha_0)^2) =: \underline\lambda$.
Since $\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]$ converges exponentially fast to
$\Sigma_k$ inside $\cI_k$, the probe-time average over any subset
$\cT_k\subseteq\cI_k$ with $|\cT_k|=m_k$ satisfies
$\lambda_r(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}])
\ge \underline\lambda/2 =: \lambda_{\min}$ for $m_k$ large enough
(i.e.\ past an $O(1)$ warm-up that we absorb into constants).
\end{proof}

\noindent\textbf{F2 (Windowed self-normalised bound).}\ On projected
covariates $z_t = \widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\in\R^r$, the windowed ridge
estimator $\widehat a_t = \widetilde V_t^{-1}\widetilde b_t$ satisfies,
with probability $\ge 1-\delta$ and simultaneously for all $t$,
$\|\widehat a_t - a_t^\star\|_{\widetilde V_t}\le\beta^{(r,W)}_t$
with
$\beta^{(r,W)}_t = \sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{r\log(1+WR_\cA^2/(\lambda r)) + 2\log(1/\delta)} + \sqrt\lambda S_w$.
This is the $r$-dimensional projected version of
\citet[Thm.~2]{abbasi2011improved} with the sliding-window correction
of \citet{russac2019weighted}; the proof is an immediate adaptation
of the self-normalised martingale bound of
\citet[Thm.~1]{abbasi2011improved} with window reset, applied to the
projected regressor $z_t$ (dimension $r$) rather than $x_t$.

\noindent\textbf{F3 (Elliptical potential).}\ For any adapted sequence
$z_1,\dots,z_T\in\R^r$ with $\|z_t\|\le R_\cA$ and
$\widetilde V_t = \lambda I + \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s z_s^\top$,
$\sum_{t=1}^T \|z_t\|^2_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le 2r\log(1 + W R_\cA^2/(\lambda r)) =: 2r L_W$
\citep[Lem.~11]{abbasi2011improved}.

\subsection{Concentration of the lifted probe moment (load-bearing step)}
\label{app:concentration_proof}

This is the non-standard piece of the analysis; we derive it in full.
For isotropic Gaussian probes $u\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ (truncated at
$\|u\|\le L := \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$ on a $1-\delta/T$ event;
see \S\ref{app:truncation}), the probe moment operator
$\mathcal K: M\mapsto\E[(u^\top Mu)uu^\top]$ is a linear bijection on
symmetric matrices.

\begin{lemma}[Closed-form inverse of $\mathcal K$]
\label{lem:K_inverse}
For isotropic Gaussian probes,
$\mathcal K(M) = 2M + \tr(M)\,I_d$ and
$\mathcal K^{-1}(N) = \tfrac12 N - \tfrac{\tr(N)}{2(d+2)}\,I_d$, so
$\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op \le 1$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Expand $u^\top M u = \sum_{ij} M_{ij} u_i u_j$ and use the Isserlis
identity $\E[u_i u_j u_k u_l] = \delta_{ij}\delta_{kl} +
\delta_{ik}\delta_{jl} + \delta_{il}\delta_{jk}$ to compute
$\E[(u^\top M u)u_k u_l] = M_{kl} + M_{lk} + \tr(M)\delta_{kl} = 2M_{kl}
+ \tr(M)\delta_{kl}$ (symmetric $M$). Inverting
$N = 2M + \tr(M)I_d$: taking traces gives $\tr(N) = (d+2)\tr(M)$, hence
$M = \tfrac12 N - \tfrac{\tr(N)}{2(d+2)}I_d$. The operator norm
follows because $\tr(N)/(d+2)\le\|N\|_\op$ for symmetric $N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Quadratic measurement identity]
\label{lem:quad_conf}
On every probe round $t\in\cTprobe$, with
$\delta_\sigma := \hat\sigma^2 - \sigma_\varepsilon^2$,
\[
\E[s_t\mid\sigma(\cH_{t-1},u_t)]
= u_t^\top \widetilde M_t u_t + 2 u_t^\top m_t
- \delta_\sigma,\qquad \|m_t\|\le\epsilon_\times.
\]
Under exact probe conditions ($\delta_\sigma=\epsilon_\times=0$) this
collapses to $\E[s_t\mid\cdot] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Expand $y_t^2 = (u_t^\top\theta_t)^2 + 2\varepsilon_t u_t^\top\theta_t
+ \varepsilon_t^2$ and condition on $\sigma(\cH_{t-1},u_t)$; the middle
term contributes $2u_t^\top m_t$ with
$m_t := \E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t]$ bounded by
$\epsilon_\times$ by Assumption; the last term contributes
$\sigma_\varepsilon^2$, and subtracting $\hat\sigma^2$ leaves
$-\delta_\sigma$. Independence of $u_t$ from $(\theta_t,\cH_{t-1})$
gives $\E[(u_t^\top\theta_t)^2\mid\cH_{t-1},u_t] = u_t^\top\widetilde M_t u_t$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Lifted probe sample is near-unbiased]
\label{lem:G_unbiased_conf}
Let $G_t := \mathcal K^{-1}(s_t u_tu_t^\top)$. Then
$\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}] = \widetilde M_t + \widetilde B_t$ with
$\|\widetilde B_t\|_\op \le |\delta_\sigma|\cdot L^2 + 2 L^3\epsilon_\times$
(using $\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op\le 1$). Moreover, the
variance-misspecification bias
$-\delta_\sigma\mathcal K^{-1}(\E[uu^\top])$
is a \emph{scaled identity}: it shifts every eigenvalue uniformly and
does not rotate eigenvectors.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Linearity of $\mathcal K^{-1}$ and Lemma~\ref{lem:quad_conf} give
$\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]
= \mathcal K^{-1}(\mathcal K(\widetilde M_t))
+ \mathcal K^{-1}(\E[2(u_t^\top m_t)u_tu_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}])
- \delta_\sigma\mathcal K^{-1}(\E[u_tu_t^\top])
= \widetilde M_t + \widetilde B_t$.
The norm bound follows from
$\|\E[(u_t^\top m_t)u_tu_t^\top]\|_\op \le L\epsilon_\times\cdot L^2 = L^3\epsilon_\times$
and $\|\E[u_tu_t^\top]\|_\op \le L^2$. For the identity-bias claim,
$\E[u_tu_t^\top] = I_d$ (after truncation, up to a $1-O(\delta/T)$
correction absorbed into constants), so
$-\delta_\sigma\mathcal K^{-1}(I_d) = -\frac{\delta_\sigma}{2}I_d
+ \frac{\delta_\sigma d}{2(d+2)}I_d = -\frac{\delta_\sigma}{d+2}I_d$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Uniform a.s.\ bound on $G_t$]
\label{lem:G_bound_conf}
On the truncation event $\|u_t\|\le L$ and the noise-truncation event
$|\varepsilon_t|\le L_\varepsilon := \sigma_\varepsilon\sqrt{2\log(T/\delta)}$,
$|s_t|\le R_s := (L S_w + L_\varepsilon)^2 + \hat\sigma^2$ and
$\|G_t\|_\op \le R_X := L^2 R_s + S_w^2$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Since $\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ with $B_k^\star$ orthonormal and
$\|w_t\|\le S_w$, $\|\theta_t\|_2\le S_w$. Hence
$|u_t^\top\theta_t|\le\|u_t\|_2\|\theta_t\|_2\le L S_w$, and
$|s_t|=|(u_t^\top\theta_t+\varepsilon_t)^2-\hat\sigma^2|
\le (LS_w+L_\varepsilon)^2 + \hat\sigma^2$ by triangle inequality.
Using $\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op\le 1$ (Lem.~\ref{lem:K_inverse}),
$\|G_t\|_\op=\|\mathcal K^{-1}(s_t u_tu_t^\top)\|_\op\le|s_t|\cdot\|u_tu_t^\top\|_\op
\le R_s L^2$, and adding the deterministic
$\|\widetilde M_t\|_\op=\|B_k^\star\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}](B_k^\star)^\top\|_\op
\le S_w^2$ bound for the mean-subtracted version gives $R_X$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Matrix Bernstein for $\widehat M_k$]
\label{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}
Let $\widehat M_k := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k} G_t$ and
$X_t := G_t - \E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$. Conditional on
Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf}, $\{X_t\}$ is a bounded matrix-valued
MDS with $\|X_t\|_\op\le 2R_X$ a.s.\ and
$\|\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[X_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]\|_\op\le m_k R_X^2$.
Freedman's matrix inequality \citep{tropp2011freedman} gives, with
probability at least $1-\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} - \widetilde B\|_\op
\le 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k} + \frac{2R_X\log(2d/\delta)}{3m_k}.
\label{eq:matrix_bernstein}
\end{equation}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The MDS property is Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf} (shifted by the
segment-level bias $\widetilde B$ from
Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}, which is deterministic and thus enters
as a constant offset). The a.s.\ bound $\|X_t\|_\op\le 2R_X$ follows
from $\|G_t\|_\op\le R_X$ and $\|\E[G_t]\|_\op\le R_X$. The predictable
variance bound follows from $\E[X_t^2]\preceq \E[G_t^2]\preceq R_X^2 I$.
Applying the matrix Freedman inequality to the MDS $\{X_t\}$ in
$\R^{d\times d}_{\mathrm{sym}}$ with these two bounds yields
\eqref{eq:matrix_bernstein}; the leading $\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}$
rate dominates the $\log(2d/\delta)/m_k$ term for $m_k\gtrsim\log(d/\delta)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Projector concentration]
\label{cor:projector_conf}
If $m_k$ is large enough that
$\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op \le \lambda_{\min}/2$,
then with probability $\ge 1-\delta$,
\begin{equation}
\varepsilon_k := \|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op
\le C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}
+ \Delta_\sigma,\quad
C_{\mathrm{sub}} := \frac{8 R_X}{\lambda_{\min}},\
\Delta_\sigma := \frac{4(|\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times)}{\lambda_{\min}}.
\label{eq:proj_bound_conf}
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf}, $\E[\widehat M_k\mid\cH]
= \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} + \widetilde B$ with
$\|\widetilde B\|_\op \le |\delta_\sigma|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times =: b_\sigma$.
Combining with Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf},
$\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op
\le 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k} + b_\sigma$.
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}$ has rank exactly $r$ with $r$-th
eigenvalue $\ge\lambda_{\min}$ (Lem.~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf}
via Prop.~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf} below); Davis--Kahan
\citep[Thm.~VII.3.1]{bhatia1997matrix} gives
$\|\widehat P_k - P_k^\star\|_\op\le 4\|\widehat M_k - \bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}\|_\op / \lambda_{\min}$,
yielding \eqref{eq:proj_bound_conf}.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Segment factorisation]
\label{prop:segment_factorization_conf}
$\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} = B_k^\star \bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}} (B_k^\star)^\top$ where
$\bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}} := m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}]$.
In particular, $\range(\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}}) = \range(B_k^\star)$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$\widetilde M_t = B_k^\star \E[w_tw_t^\top\mid\cH_{t-1}](B_k^\star)^\top$
for $t\in\cI_k$; average over $\cT_k$ and pull $B_k^\star$ out.
Range equality uses
$\lambda_r(\bar S_k^{\mathrm{probe}})\ge\lambda_{\min}>0$
(Lem.~\ref{lem:probe_excitation_conf}).
\end{proof}

This chain---Lemmas \ref{lem:K_inverse}--\ref{lem:G_bound_conf},
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}, Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf},
Proposition~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf}---gives a fully
self-contained \emph{upper bound} on subspace recovery at rate
$O(1/\sqrt{m_k})$. The next subsection
(\S\ref{app:proof_minimax}) shows this rate is minimax-optimal up to
a $\sqrt{\log d}$ factor. A variance-adaptive refinement (replacing
the uniform a.s.\ bound $R_X$ by a local effective scale) sharpens
constants but does not change the leading order.

\subsection{Minimax subspace-recovery lower bound (Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery})}
\label{app:proof_minimax}

We now show that the $1/\sqrt{m_k}$ rate of
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf} cannot be improved by any estimator
within the same single-play quadratic observation model, up to a
$\sqrt{\log d}$ factor. This establishes that the $T^{2/3}$ probe term
in Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} is the correct rate \emph{for any
algorithm in $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$}: inside the hard-projection class,
subspace acquisition is the bottleneck, and no estimator can beat
$1/\sqrt m$.

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery}]
The argument is a Fano construction over a \emph{local} Grassmannian
packing with matched upper and lower diameter bounds, so that the KL
bound (which needs an \emph{upper} diameter) and Fano's inequality
(which needs a \emph{lower} diameter) can be applied at the same scale.

\emph{Local Grassmannian packing.} For $d\ge 2r$ and any scale
$\Delta\in(0,1/2)$, there exist subspaces $V_1,\dots,V_N\in\mathrm{Gr}(r,d)$
satisfying
\begin{equation}
  \tfrac{1}{2}\Delta \;\le\; \|P_{V_i}-P_{V_j}\|_\op \;\le\; 2\Delta
  \quad\text{for all }i\ne j,
  \qquad \log N \;\ge\; c_0\, r(d-r),
\label{eq:local_packing}
\end{equation}
for an absolute constant $c_0>0$ (Varshamov--Gilbert lemma applied to
a Grassmannian tangent-space chart around a reference
$V_0\in\mathrm{Gr}(r,d)$; see, e.g., \citet[Lem.~1]{cai2013sparsepca}
or \citet[Chap.~15]{wainwright2019hds}). The upper diameter $2\Delta$
is what the KL computation below requires; the lower diameter
$\Delta/2$ is what Fano's inequality requires. A \emph{global}
packing (with only a separation lower bound) would not suffice.

\emph{Instance construction.} For each $j\in[N]$, let $B_j$ be an
orthonormal basis of $V_j$ and define instance $\nu_j$ by: one segment,
$B^\star=B_j$, $A_1=0$, $w_t\sim\mathcal N(0,\lambda_{\min}I_r)$
i.i.d., $\varepsilon_t\sim\mathcal N(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2)$ i.i.d.,
and isotropic Gaussian probes $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$
(truncated at $\|u_t\|\le L$; the $\le\delta$ truncation mass is
absorbed into constants by \S\ref{app:truncation}). Under $\nu_j$, the
conditional reward distribution given probe $u_t=u$ is zero-mean
Gaussian with variance $\sigma_j^2(u):=\lambda_{\min} u^\top P_{V_j} u+\sigma_\varepsilon^2
\ge \sigma_\varepsilon^2$.

\emph{Pairwise KL.} Let $\Pi_{ij}:=P_{V_i}-P_{V_j}$, so $\mathrm{tr}\,\Pi_{ij}=0$
since $\mathrm{tr}\,P_{V_i}=\mathrm{tr}\,P_{V_j}=r$. The conditional KL
between two zero-mean Gaussians with variances $\sigma_i^2(u),\sigma_j^2(u)$
is $\tfrac12(r_u-1-\log r_u)$ with $r_u:=\sigma_i^2(u)/\sigma_j^2(u)>0$. Using
the elementary bound $r-1-\log r\le (r-1)^2/(2\min(r,1))$ for $r>0$ and
$\sigma_j^2(u)\ge\sigma_\varepsilon^2$,
\[
  \mathrm{KL}(\nu_i^{(y\mid u)}\|\nu_j^{(y\mid u)})
  \;\le\; \frac{(\sigma_i^2(u)-\sigma_j^2(u))^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^4}
  \;=\; \frac{\lambda_{\min}^2\,(u^\top\Pi_{ij}u)^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^4}.
\]
Marginalizing over $u\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ (the probe marginal is
identical under $\nu_i$ and $\nu_j$, so the joint KL is
$\mathbb E_u[\mathrm{KL}(\nu_i^{(y\mid u)}\|\nu_j^{(y\mid u)})]$), and
using the Gaussian quadratic-form identity
$\mathbb E[(u^\top M u)^2]=(\mathrm{tr}\,M)^2+2\|M\|_F^2$ (Isserlis)
with $\mathrm{tr}\,\Pi_{ij}=0$,
\[
  \mathbb E_u[\mathrm{KL}] \;\le\; \frac{\lambda_{\min}^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^4}\cdot 2\|\Pi_{ij}\|_F^2
  \;=\; \frac{\lambda_{\min}^2}{\sigma_\varepsilon^4}\|\Pi_{ij}\|_F^2.
\]
Because $\Pi_{ij}$ is a difference of rank-$r$ projectors it has rank
at most $2r$, so $\|\Pi_{ij}\|_F^2\le 2r\|\Pi_{ij}\|_\op^2$. Combined
with the upper diameter $\|\Pi_{ij}\|_\op\le 2\Delta$
from~\eqref{eq:local_packing}, $\|\Pi_{ij}\|_F^2\le 8r\Delta^2$, and
\[
  \mathbb E_u[\mathrm{KL}] \le C_1\, r\, \Delta^2,\qquad C_1 := \frac{8\lambda_{\min}^2}{\sigma_\varepsilon^4}.
\]
By tensorization of KL across $m$ i.i.d.\ probe rounds,
$\mathrm{KL}(\nu_i^{\otimes m}\|\nu_j^{\otimes m})\le C_1\,m\,r\,\Delta^2$.

\emph{Fano's inequality.} By \citet[Thm.~2.5]{tsybakov2009introduction},
if $\max_{i\ne j}\mathrm{KL}(\nu_i^{\otimes m}\|\nu_j^{\otimes m})
\le\tfrac14\log N$, then
$\inf_{\widehat\psi}\max_j\Pr_{\nu_j}(\widehat\psi\ne j)\ge 1/2$ (for
$N\ge 2$, using the pairwise-max upper bound on mutual information
and the standard Fano rearrangement). Combining this with the
lower-diameter bound $\|\Pi_{ij}\|_\op\ge\Delta/2$ via Markov's inequality
applied to $\|\widehat P-P_{V_j}\|_\op$,
\[
  \inf_{\widehat P}\max_j\E_{\nu_j}[\|\widehat P-P_{V_j}\|_\op]
  \;\ge\; \frac{\Delta}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}
  \;=\; \Delta/8,
\]
where the factors of $1/2$ come from converting a testing error to a
one-sided pairwise distance bound and from Fano's constant.

\emph{Balancing.} The KL constraint $C_1\,m\,r\,\Delta^2\le\tfrac14\log N$,
combined with $\log N\ge c_0\,r(d-r)$, gives
$\Delta^2\le c_0(d-r)/(4C_1 m)$. Setting $\Delta=\min\bigl(1/2,\,
\tfrac12\sqrt{c_0(d-r)/C_1\cdot m^{-1}}\bigr)$ saturates this, and the
bound becomes
\[
  \inf_{\widehat P}\sup_{\nu\in\mathfrak M}\E_\nu[\|\widehat P-P^\star\|_\op]
  \;\ge\; \Delta/8 \;\ge\; \frac{c_1}{\sqrt m}
\]
with $c_1:=\tfrac{1}{16}\sqrt{c_0(d-r)/C_1}$, valid for
$m\ge c_0(d-r)/C_1$ (which holds for the regime of interest
$m\gg r(d-r)$ where subspace recovery is actually possible).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Identifiability sample threshold]
\label{cor:id_threshold_conf}
Any estimator achieving operator-norm subspace error below $1/4$
requires $m \ge c_{\mathrm{id}}\,(d-r)$ probe rounds.
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
Apply Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery} at target risk $1/4$:
$c_1/\sqrt m\ge 1/4$ needs $m\le 16 c_1^2=\Theta(d-r)$ from the
explicit constant $c_1=\Theta(\sqrt{d-r})$ derived in the proof.
The op-norm rate $\Theta(\sqrt{(d-r)/m})$ is the standard minimax rate
for subspace recovery under op-norm loss from $m$ independent
observations (in F-norm the rate is $\Theta(\sqrt{r(d-r)/m})$, matching
the intrinsic Grassmannian dimension $r(d-r)$; the stronger op-norm
metric is what enters the SPSC regret via
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf}).
\end{proof}

\emph{Matching to the upper bound.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf} gives
$\|\widehat P_k-P_k^\star\|_{\op}\le C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}$.
Theorem~\ref{thm:minimax_recovery} matches this up to the
$\sqrt{\log d}$ factor inherent to high-probability matrix Freedman
bounds. Hence the moment-matching estimator used by SPSC is rate-optimal.

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}}
\label{app:proof_main}

Fix a segment $\cI_k$ with probe budget $m_k$ and let
$\Delta_t := \max_{x\in\cA_t} x^\top\theta_t - x_t^\top\theta_t$ denote
the instantaneous exploitation regret at $t$. We decompose the costed
regret into three pieces bounded separately.

\emph{(i) Projected exploitation regret.}
On exploitation rounds the learner plays
$x_t = \argmax_{x\in\cA_t}\{(\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x)^\top \widehat a_t
+ \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
+ \gamma_t\|x\|\}$. Write $a_t^\star := \widehat U_{t-1}^\top \theta_t$
(the projection of the truth onto the current estimate). By optimism
at $x_t^\star\in\argmax_x x^\top\theta_t$,
\begin{align*}
\Delta_t &\le x_t^\top(\theta_t - \widehat U_{t-1}\widehat a_t)
 + \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
 + \gamma_t\\
&= \underbrace{x_t^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t}_{\text{subspace mismatch}}
 + \underbrace{x_t^\top \widehat U_{t-1}(a_t^\star - \widehat a_t)}_{\text{in-subspace estimation}}
 + \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|\widehat U_{t-1}^\top x_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
 + \gamma_t.
\end{align*}
\emph{(i.a) Subspace-mismatch term.}
$|x_t^\top(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t| \le R_\cA\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})\theta_t\|_2
\le R_\cA\|(I-\widehat P_{t-1})B_k^\star\|_\op\cdot\|w_t\|_2 \le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
(using the equal-rank-projector identity
$\|(I-\widehat P)B^\star\|_\op\le\|P^\star-\widehat P\|_\op=\varepsilon_k$
and $\|w_t\|\le S_w$). Summed over $E_k$: $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$.

\emph{(i.b) In-subspace estimation error.} The ridge regression is
\emph{misspecified}: the residual is
$y_s - z_s^\top a_s^\star = \xi_s + \varepsilon_s$ with
$\xi_s = x_s^\top(I-\widehat P_{s-1})\theta_s$ bounded by $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
(the same bound as in step (i.a), via the equal-rank identity). We
therefore apply Lemma~\ref{lem:drift_conf} \emph{in place of} the
standard well-specified self-normalized bound, taking
$\bar\xi_k:=R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$: for $z=z_t(x_t)$ with $\|z\|_2\le R_\cA$,
\[
  |(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z|
  \;\le\; \beta_t^{(r,W)}\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
  \;+\; R_\cA V_{k,t}(W)
  \;+\; R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}\,\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}.
\]
Summing over $E_k$ and applying Cauchy--Schwarz with F3
($\sum_{t\in E_k}\|z_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\le\sqrt{2rn_kL_W}$):
\begin{equation}
\sum_{t\in E_k}|(\widehat a_t-a_t^\star)^\top z_t|
\le \beta_k^{(r,W)}\sqrt{2 r n_k L_W}
+ R_\cA W V_k
+ 2 r L_W R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{W n_k}.
\label{eq:in_subspace_sum}
\end{equation}
The third term (subspace-mismatch propagation through the ridge) is
\emph{lower-order} than the step-(i.a) term $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$
whenever $n_k\ge W/(2rL_W)^2$, which holds in the natural regime; it
is absorbed into the $\tilO(\cdot)$ constants below.

\emph{(i.c) Total exploit regret.}
Adding the step-(i.a) and step-(i.b) contributions, the exploit
regret on segment $k$ is at most
\begin{equation}
\sum_{t\in E_k}\Delta_t
\le 2\beta_k^{(r,W)}\sqrt{2 r n_k L_W}
+ R_\cA S_w \varepsilon_k n_k
+ 2 R_\cA\sum_{t\in E_k} V_{k,t}(W)
+ \text{(lower-order)}.
\label{eq:exploit_sum}
\end{equation}
(The factor of 2 in front of $\beta_k$ comes from optimism: the same
$\beta$ bounds both the gap and the UCB margin.) Summing over
segments via $\sum_k\sqrt{n_k}\le\sqrt{KT}$ (Cauchy--Schwarz) and
$\sum_{k,t} V_{k,t}(W)\le W V$ (each drift edge counted in $\le W$
windows) gives
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \sum_k R_\cA S_w \varepsilon_k n_k + O(R_\cA W V)$.

\emph{(ii) Probe--subspace tradeoff.}
Corollary~\ref{cor:projector_conf} (matrix Bernstein +
Davis--Kahan, proved in \S\ref{app:concentration_proof}), applied
at confidence level $\delta/(2K)$, gives, on an
event of probability $\ge 1-\delta/(2K)$,
\[
\varepsilon_k \;\le\; C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(4Kd/\delta)/m_k} \;+\; \Delta_\sigma,\qquad
\Delta_\sigma := 4\bigl(|\hat\sigma^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2|L^2 + 2L^3\epsilon_\times\bigr)/\lambda_{\min}.
\]
The bias floor $\Delta_\sigma$ does \emph{not} vanish with $m_k$: it
is a constant (in $T$) determined by plugin variance misspecification
and state--noise coupling. Substituting into the subspace-mismatch
regret $R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k$ yields two contributions per segment:
\[
  R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k n_k \;\le\; \underbrace{B\,n_k/\sqrt{m_k}}_{\text{statistical}}
  \;+\; \underbrace{R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma n_k}_{\text{bias floor}},
\qquad B := 2 C_{\mathrm{sub}} S_w R_\cA\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)}.
\]
Probe rounds contribute $A m_k$ with $A := 2 R_\cA S_w + c$ (foregone
action plus designated cost). Minimising
$A m_k + B\ell_k/\sqrt{m_k}$ in $m_k\in[1,\ell_k]$ (the statistical
part) by setting the derivative to zero gives
$m_k^\star = \lceil (B\ell_k/(2A))^{2/3}\rceil$ and
\begin{equation}
A m_k^\star + \frac{B\ell_k}{\sqrt{m_k^\star}}
= \tfrac{3}{2}\cdot 2^{1/3}\, A^{2/3} B^{1/3}\, \ell_k^{2/3}\,(1+o(1)).
\label{eq:opt_mk}
\end{equation}
Summing over segments, the statistical contribution uses Jensen,
$\sum_k \ell_k^{2/3}\le K^{1/3}(\sum_k\ell_k)^{2/3} = K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$,
so the probe--subspace-statistical cost is $\tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$.
The bias-floor contribution sums to $R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma\sum_k n_k
\le R_\cA S_w\Delta_\sigma T = O(T\Delta_\sigma)$, the last term in
the main bound. Under exact probes ($\hat\sigma^2=\sigma_\varepsilon^2$
and $\epsilon_\times=0$), $\Delta_\sigma=0$ and this term vanishes.

\emph{(iii) Within-segment drift.}
Let
$V_k := \sum_{s\in E_k,s+1\in E_k}\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$
and $V := \sum_k V_k$ denote the per-segment and total path variation
of $\theta$.

\begin{lemma}[Windowed ridge prediction bound]
\label{lem:drift_conf}
Assume the projected-regression observation model on $s\in\cW_t$ is
\begin{equation}
y_s = z_s^\top a_s^\star + \xi_s + \varepsilon_s,\qquad
|\xi_s|\le\bar\xi_k,\qquad
\varepsilon_s\;\text{is F2-sub-Gaussian},
\label{eq:ridge_residual_decomp}
\end{equation}
where $a_s^\star := \widehat U_{t-1}^\top\theta_s$, $\xi_s$ is a
\emph{deterministic residual} (subspace-mismatch bias at round $s$),
and $\varepsilon_s$ is the sub-Gaussian reward noise.
On the F2 concentration event, the windowed ridge estimator
$\widehat a_t=\widetilde V_t^{-1}\sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s y_s$ satisfies,
for every $\|z\|_2\le R_\cA$,
\begin{equation}
\bigl|(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z\bigr|
\;\le\; \underbrace{\beta_t^{(r,W)}\,\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}}_{\text{noise}}
 \;+\; \underbrace{R_\cA\, V_{k,t}(W)}_{\text{drift}}
 \;+\; \underbrace{\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2W r L_W}\,\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}}_{\text{subspace-mismatch propagation}},
\label{eq:drift_lemma_bound}
\end{equation}
where
$V_{k,t}(W) := \sum_{s\in\cW_t,s+1\in\cW_t}\|a_{s+1}^\star-a_s^\star\|_2$
is the in-subspace path variation inside the window.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Write $a_t^\star=\widehat U_{t-1}^\top\theta_t$ and
$\eta_s := a_s^\star - a_t^\star$. Using
\eqref{eq:ridge_residual_decomp}, the ridge normal equations give
\[
\widetilde V_t(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)
= \sum_{s\in\cW_t} z_s\bigl(z_s^\top\eta_s + \xi_s + \varepsilon_s\bigr) - \lambda a_t^\star
= \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s z_s^\top \eta_s}_{B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}}
\;+\; \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s \xi_s}_{B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}}
\;+\; \underbrace{\sum_{s} z_s\varepsilon_s - \lambda a_t^\star}_{N_t}.
\]
Then
$(\widehat a_t - a_t^\star)^\top z
= z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}} + z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{sub}} + z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}N_t$.

\emph{Noise term.} By Cauchy--Schwarz in the $\widetilde V_t^{-1}$-inner
product, $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}N_t|\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\cdot\|N_t\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\,\beta_t^{(r,W)}$, where F2 supplies the
second inequality.

\emph{Drift term.} Using the telescoping
$\eta_s = -\sum_{u=s+1}^{t}\delta_u$ with $\delta_u := a_u^\star-a_{u-1}^\star$,
write
$B_t^{\mathrm{drift}} = -\sum_{u}\delta_u\bigl(\sum_{s<u,s\in\cW_t} z_sz_s^\top\bigr)$.
Let $H_u := \widetilde V_t^{-1}\bigl(\sum_{s<u,s\in\cW_t} z_sz_s^\top\bigr)$.
Since $\sum_{s<u,s\in\cW_t} z_sz_s^\top\preceq\widetilde V_t$, the matrix
$\widetilde V_t^{-1/2}\bigl(\sum_{s<u} z_sz_s^\top\bigr)\widetilde V_t^{-1/2}\preceq I$,
and $H_u$ is similar to this PSD contraction, so $\|H_u\|_\op\le 1$.
Hence $\|\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}\|_2
\le\sum_u \|H_u\|_\op\,\|\delta_u\|_2\le V_{k,t}(W)$, and
$|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{drift}}|\le\|z\|_2\,V_{k,t}(W)
\le R_\cA\,V_{k,t}(W)$.

\emph{Subspace-mismatch propagation.} By Cauchy--Schwarz,
$\|B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
=\bigl\|\sum_s z_s\xi_s\bigr\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\sum_s|\xi_s|\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\bar\xi_k\sum_s\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}$.
Applying the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality to the last sum and the
elliptical-potential lemma F3,
$\sum_s\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}
\le\sqrt{W}\sqrt{\sum_{s\in\cW_t}\|z_s\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}^2}
\le\sqrt{W\cdot 2rL_W}$,
giving $\|B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\le\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}$
and $|z^\top\widetilde V_t^{-1}B_t^{\mathrm{sub}}|\le\|z\|_{\widetilde V_t^{-1}}\cdot\bar\xi_k\sqrt{2WrL_W}$.

Combining the three terms yields \eqref{eq:drift_lemma_bound}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Specialization to the SPSC projected ridge]
\label{rem:drift_lemma_spec}
When applied to SPSC's projected ridge, $\xi_s := x_s^\top(I-\widehat P_{s-1})\theta_s$
is the per-round subspace-mismatch residual, bounded by
$|\xi_s|\le R_\cA\cdot\|(I-\widehat P_{s-1})B_k^\star\|_\op\cdot S_w\le R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$
using the standard equal-rank projector identity
$\|(I-\widehat P)B^\star\|_\op\le\|P^\star-\widehat P\|_\op$. Hence in
\eqref{eq:drift_lemma_bound} we may take $\bar\xi_k=R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k$.
\end{remark}

\emph{Quantifying the subspace-mismatch propagation.} Substituting
$m_k^\star\asymp T^{2/3}K^{-2/3}$, $\varepsilon_k\asymp m_k^{-1/2}\asymp K^{1/3}T^{-1/3}$,
$n_k\asymp T/K$ into the lower-order term of \eqref{eq:in_subspace_sum},
$2rL_W R_\cA S_w\varepsilon_k\sqrt{Wn_k}
\asymp \tilO(W^{1/2}K^{-1/6}T^{1/6})$
per segment, giving $\tilO(K^{5/6}W^{1/2}T^{1/6})$ summed over $K$
segments, which is $o(K^{1/3}T^{2/3})$ whenever $WK=o(T)$
(the natural window-scaling regime).\footnote{The $\gamma_t\|x\|_2$
correction in Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} supplements the UCB radius with a
per-round local-variation estimate and ensures the chosen $x_t$ is
optimistic against both the drift and the subspace-mismatch bias; it
does not change the $O(WV)$ rate.}

\emph{Drift summation.} Each drift edge
$\|a_{s+1}^\star-a_s^\star\|_2\le\|\theta_{s+1}-\theta_s\|_2$ appears
in at most $W$ windows, so $\sum_{k,t\in E_k}V_{k,t}(W)\le W V$, and
the total drift contribution is $O(R_\cA W V)$.

\emph{Regime of interest.} The bound is informative whenever
$V = o(r\sqrt{KT}/W)$. For \emph{piecewise-constant} subspaces the
path variation is supported on the $O(K)$ boundary edges, so
$V\le 2(K-1)\,S_w\le 2K S_w$ deterministically (each
$\|\theta_{\tau_k+1}-\theta_{\tau_k}\|\le 2\|\theta\|\le 2S_w$ by the
triangle inequality and $\|\theta_t\|=\|B_k^\star w_t\|\le S_w$). The
drift contribution is then $R_\cA W V\le 2K W R_\cA S_w$; for the
natural window choice $W\asymp\sqrt{T/K}$ this is
$O(\sqrt{KT}\,R_\cA S_w)$, lower-order than both leading terms. For a
\emph{stationary stable LDS}, by contrast, $\mathbb E[V_k]=\Theta(\ell_k)$
(the path variation grows linearly in segment length because
$\mathbb E\|\theta_{t+1}-\theta_t\|$ is $\Theta(1)$ per step for any
stationary non-degenerate process), so $V=\Theta(T)$ and the $O(WV)$
term dominates unless $W$ is chosen small enough; hence the
piecewise-constant simplification is the natural regime in which the
main bound is interesting.

Taking a union bound over the $K$ segment-level concentration events
(each at confidence $\delta/(2K)$, total mass $\delta/2$) and the
windowed self-normalised event (at confidence $\delta/2$), we obtain,
with probability $\ge 1-\delta$,
\[
\DynReg_T^{(c)}
\le C_1\, r\sqrt{KT\, L_W \log(2T/\delta)}
+ C_2\, (A^2 B)^{1/3} K^{1/3} T^{2/3}
+ C_3\, R_\cA\, W\, V
+ C_4\, R_\cA S_w T\Delta_\sigma
= \tilO\!\bigl(r\sqrt{KT}\bigr) + \tilO\!\bigl(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}\bigr) + O(WV) + O(T\Delta_\sigma),
\]
matching the statement of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}. The
$\sqrt{K}$ factor in the exploitation term is tight for any algorithm
that restarts its estimator at every segment boundary (standard
lower bound for piecewise-stationary linear bandits); under exact
probes the $T\Delta_\sigma$ term vanishes.\qed

\begin{remark}[Robustness to segment-length misestimation]
\label{rem:lk_misspec}
The oracle allocation $m_k^\star=\lceil(B\ell_k/(2A))^{2/3}\rceil$
depends on $\ell_k$. If the algorithm substitutes an estimate
$\widehat\ell_k\in[\ell_k/c_\ell,\,c_\ell\ell_k]$ for any constant
$c_\ell\ge 1$, plugging into \eqref{eq:opt_mk} shows the per-segment
probe--subspace cost degrades by at most the factor
$\max(c_\ell^{2/3},\,c_\ell^{-1/3})$; the leading $K^{1/3}T^{2/3}$
rate is unchanged. SPSC-Adaptive sidesteps this entirely by using a
fixed probe rate $\mu\in(0,1)$, which yields $m_k\approx\mu\ell_k$
without needing $\widehat\ell_k$; the resulting allocation is
suboptimal by at most a constant factor in the leading rate
(Thm.~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}).
\end{remark}

\subsection{Proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:rank_adaptive}}
\label{app:proof_adaptive_rank}

By Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf}, with probability
$\ge 1-\delta$ every eigenvalue of $\widehat M_k$ is within
$\tau_k := 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k}$ of the corresponding
eigenvalue of $\bar M_k^{\mathrm{probe}} + \widetilde B$ (Weyl's
inequality applied to the matrix in
\eqref{eq:matrix_bernstein}). The population spectrum has exactly $r$
eigenvalues $\ge\lambda_{\min}$ and the remaining $d-r$ are zero
(Prop.~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf}). Under the eigengap
hypothesis $\lambda_{\min}\ge 4\tau_k$, thresholding at $2\tau_k$
returns exactly $r$ indices with probability $\ge 1-\delta$. Condition
on this event; the regret analysis of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret}
applies verbatim with the estimated rank equal to the true $r$, so
\eqref{eq:main_bound} holds with an extra $\delta$ in the union bound.\qed

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}}
\label{app:proof_adaptive}

The detector $S_t := \|\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}} - \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}\|_\op$
compares the lifted moment over the last $W_{\mathrm{det}}$ probes
against the segment-accumulated moment. Under $H_0$ (no change), the
matrix Freedman inequality (Thm.~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf})
applied to each half gives
$\Pr(S_t > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}) \le \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}/T$ with
$\eta_{\mathrm{det}} = C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(dT/\delta_{\mathrm{FA}})/W_{\mathrm{det}}}$,
so by union bound the probability of any false alarm over $T$ rounds
is at most $\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$. Under $H_1$ at time $\tau_k$, the
separation assumption $\Delta_k \ge 2b$ with $b > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}$
forces the detector to trigger within a detection delay
$D_k = O(W_{\mathrm{det}})$ rounds after $\tau_k$. Applied to each of
the $K$ segments, this replaces the oracle segment boundaries by
estimated ones $\widehat\tau_k$ with $|\widehat\tau_k - \tau_k| \le D_k$;
the extra regret incurred during the delay is at most
$2 R_\cA S_w \sum_k D_k = O(K W_{\mathrm{det}})$. The leading
$\tilO(r\sqrt{KT}) + \tilO(K^{1/3}T^{2/3}) + O(WV) + O(T\Delta_\sigma)$
bound of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} carries through per-segment,
with the $O(K W_{\mathrm{det}})$ lower-order overhead stated.\qed

\subsection{Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_lb}}
\label{app:proof_lb}

The proof is a two-point Le Cam construction in which one instance
has \emph{zero signal} ($\theta^{(0)}=0$) and the other has signal
$\alpha v$ for a unit vector $v$ chosen by the adversary after seeing
the policy. Under $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, adversarial choice of $v$ keeps
the policy's projector overlap $\|\widehat P_t v\|$ small under
$\nu_0$, which in turn keeps exploitation-round KL small; the
identifying information about $v$ must therefore come primarily from
probes, yielding the $T^{2/3}$ probe-cost tradeoff. The argument
corrects the original sketch (which assumed $w\ne 0$ and would give a
spurious $\alpha^2/\|w\|$ per-round regret in place of the linear-in-$\alpha$
bound used below).

\emph{Construction.} Fix $d\ge 2r$ and orthonormal
$B^{(0)}\in\R^{d\times r}$ (e.g., $B^{(0)}=[e_1,\dots,e_r]$). Instance
$\nu_0$: $B^\star=B^{(0)}$, $\theta^{(0)}\equiv 0$ (obtained as the
limit $\Sigma_\eta\to 0^+$ in the LDS, with $w_0=0$; the bound is
continuous in $\Sigma_\eta$). Instance $\nu_1(v)$ (parameterized by a
unit vector $v$): $B^{(1)}=[e_1,\dots,e_{r-1},v]$,
$\theta^{(1)}\equiv\alpha v$ for a scale $\alpha>0$. Both are valid
rank-$r$ instances. Noise $\varepsilon_t\sim\mathcal N(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2)$;
actions $\cA=\{\|x\|\le R_\cA\}$; probes $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$.

\emph{Step 1 (linear-in-$\alpha$ per-round regret).}
For $\pi\in\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$, the played action satisfies
$x_t=\widehat P_t x_t$, so
$x_t^\top v = x_t^\top\widehat P_t v\le R_\cA\|\widehat P_t v\|_2$
by Cauchy--Schwarz. The optimal $\nu_1$-reward is $R_\cA\alpha$
(playing $R_\cA v$), so per-round $\nu_1$-regret satisfies
\[
  \Delta_t^{(1)} \;\ge\; R_\cA\alpha\,\bigl(1-\|\widehat P_t v\|_2\bigr),
\]
linear in $\alpha$. Under $\nu_0$ the expected reward of every action
is $0$, so control regret under $\nu_0$ is $0$ and costed regret under
$\nu_0$ is exactly the probe cost $cm$.

\emph{Step 2 (adversarial $v$ suppresses exploit KL).}
The sequential KL chain rule (with $x_t=\pi(\cH_{t-1})$ deterministic
given history) gives
\[
  \mathrm{KL}(\mathbb P_0^{\cH}\|\mathbb P_1^{\cH})
  = \frac{\alpha^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^2}\,\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t=1}^T (x_t^\top v)^2\Bigr].
\]
Probe rounds ($x_t=u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$) contribute
$\mathbb E[(u_t^\top v)^2]=\|v\|_2^2=1$ each, totaling
$m\alpha^2/(2\sigma_\varepsilon^2)$. Exploit rounds contribute
$(x_t^\top v)^2=(x_t^\top\widehat P_t v)^2\le R_\cA^2\|\widehat P_t v\|_2^2$
since $x_t=\widehat P_t x_t$ and $\|x_t\|\le R_\cA$.

Crucially, under $\nu_0$ the history is noise-only
($y_t\sim\mathcal N(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2)$, independent of $v$), so
the law of $\widehat P_t$ under $\nu_0$ does not depend on $v$ at all.
The adversary picks $v$ \emph{after} this law is determined:
\[
  v^\star \;:=\; \arg\min_{\|v\|=1}\;\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0(v)}\!\Bigl[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{exp}}}\|\widehat P_t v\|_2^2\Bigr].
\]
Averaging $\|\widehat P_t v\|_2^2$ over $v$ uniform on $\mathbb S^{d-1}$
gives $\mathrm{tr}(\widehat P_t)/d=r/d$. Hence the minimum over $v$ is
$\le r/d$ per round, so
$\mathbb E_{\mathbb P_0}[\sum_{t\in\cT_{\mathrm{exp}}}\|\widehat P_t v^\star\|_2^2]
\le (T-m)r/d$. Substituting,
\[
  \mathrm{KL}(\mathbb P_0^{\cH}\|\mathbb P_1^{\cH}(v^\star))
  \;\le\; \frac{\alpha^2}{2\sigma_\varepsilon^2}\Bigl(m + \frac{R_\cA^2(T-m)\,r}{d}\Bigr).
\]

\emph{Step 3 (Le Cam).}
Pinsker gives $d_{\mathrm{TV}}(\mathbb P_0,\mathbb P_1(v^\star))\le\sqrt{\mathrm{KL}/2}$.
Setting
\[
  \alpha^2 \;:=\; \frac{\sigma_\varepsilon^2}{4\bigl(m + R_\cA^2(T-m)r/d\bigr)}
\]
yields $d_{\mathrm{TV}}\le 1/\sqrt 8<1/2$. By the standard Le Cam
two-point argument,
\[
  \max\bigl(\E^\pi_{\nu_0}[\mathrm{Reg}],\,\E^\pi_{\nu_1(v^\star)}[\mathrm{Reg}]\bigr)
  \;\ge\; \tfrac{1}{2}(1-d_{\mathrm{TV}})\cdot\bigl(\text{regret gap}\bigr).
\]

\emph{Step 4 (regret tradeoff in the large-$d$ regime).}
We use $d\ge d_0\cdot r\,T^{1/3}$ for a constant $d_0$ (so
$R_\cA^2(T-m)r/d\le R_\cA^2 T^{2/3}/d_0$). For $m\ge T^{2/3}/d_0$, the
denominator in $\alpha^2$ is dominated by $m$, so $\alpha\asymp\sigma_\varepsilon/\sqrt m$.
Using Step 1, the exploit regret on $\nu_1(v^\star)$ is at least
\[
  R_\cA\alpha(T-m)\cdot\mathbb E_{\nu_1}[1-\|\widehat P_t v^\star\|]
  \ge R_\cA\alpha(T-m)\cdot\bigl(1-\sqrt{r/d}-d_{\mathrm{TV}}\bigr)
  \ge c_1 R_\cA\alpha(T-m)
\]
for an absolute constant $c_1>0$ (using $\sqrt{r/d}\le 1/\sqrt{d_0 T^{1/3}}$,
which is small for $T$ and $d_0$ large, and $d_{\mathrm{TV}}\le 1/\sqrt 8$).
Substituting $\alpha\asymp\sigma_\varepsilon/\sqrt m$,
\[
  \sup_\nu\E^\pi_\nu[\mathrm{DynReg}_T^{(c)}]
  \;\ge\; cm \;+\; c_1 R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon\,T/\sqrt m
\]
(bounding $T-m\ge T/2$ for $m\le T/2$; for $m>T/2$ the probe cost
$cm\ge cT/2$ alone dominates the rate). Minimizing in $m$,
$m^\star=\Theta((R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon T/c)^{2/3})$, and
\[
  \inf_{\pi\in\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}}\sup_\nu\E^\pi_\nu[\mathrm{DynReg}_T^{(c)}]
  \;\ge\; c_0\,c^{1/3}(R_\cA\sigma_\varepsilon)^{2/3}T^{2/3}
\]
for an absolute constant $c_0>0$ (depending on $d_0$, $c_1$).\qed

\begin{remark}[Regime of the lower bound]
\label{rem:lb_scope_conf}
The $T^{2/3}$ rate is established for the large-$d$ regime
$d\ge d_0\cdot r\,T^{1/3}$ (equivalently $d/r\gtrsim T^{1/3}$), which
is the natural regime in which subspace recovery is the bottleneck.
In the opposite regime $d=O(r)$, the exploit-KL term
$R_\cA^2(T-m)r/d$ is comparable to $T$, and the above Pinsker bound
degrades to $\Omega(\sqrt T)$; a tighter analysis (e.g., tracking
instance-specific packing over $\mathrm{Gr}(r,d)$) is needed for
$d=O(r)$ but lies outside the scope of this paper.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the lower bound with respect to policy class]
\label{rem:lb_class_conf}
The hard-projection restriction enters via Step 2's inequality
$|x_t^\top v|=|x_t^\top\widehat P_t v|\le R_\cA\|\widehat P_t v\|_2$,
valid because $x_t=\widehat P_t x_t$. A policy outside $\Pi_{\mathrm{HP}}$
could pick exploitation actions with non-zero components along $v$
even when $\widehat P_t v=0$, contributing additional exploit KL that
could, in principle, reduce the probe budget needed for
identification and beat $T^{2/3}$. Constructing (or ruling out) such
an algorithm is the open problem our analysis isolates.
\end{remark}

\section{Necessity of the probe-side conditions}
\label{app:necessity}

The probe-side conditions of \S\ref{sec:setting} (known probe
distribution $Q$ with full-dimensional coverage; known noise variance
---estimated online with burn-in absorbed; bounded state--noise
coupling $\|\E[\varepsilon_t\theta_t]\|\le\epsilon_\times$) are each
individually necessary: dropping any one renders $\widetilde M_t$
non-identifiable, so \emph{no} algorithm---not just SPSC---can recover
the subspace from single-play scalar feedback. We prove the three
claims separately by constructing two distinct parameter instances
that generate identical observation laws.

\begin{proposition}[Non-identification without known noise variance]
\label{prop:nonid_a}
Fix $t$. Suppose $\epsilon_\times=0$ (exact state--noise orthogonality)
and full probe coverage hold, but the conditional noise variance
$v_t(u):=\E[\varepsilon_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1},u]$ is completely unknown.
Then $\widetilde M_t$ is not identifiable from the observable
$g_t(u):=\E[y_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1},u]$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$g_t(u) = u^\top\widetilde M_t u + v_t(u)$. For any small
$\delta\in(0,\sigma_\varepsilon^2/L^2]$, set $H:=\delta I_d$ and
define $\widetilde M_t':=\widetilde M_t + H$ and
$v_t'(u):=v_t(u) - u^\top H u = v_t(u)-\delta\|u\|^2$. On the
truncation support $\|u\|\le L$, $v_t'(u)\ge \sigma_\varepsilon^2 -
\delta L^2\ge 0$, so $v_t'$ is a valid conditional variance. The two
pairs $(\widetilde M_t,v_t)$ and $(\widetilde M_t',v_t')$ produce
identical observables $g_t$, so $\widetilde M_t\ne\widetilde M_t'$
are indistinguishable.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Non-identification without bounded state--noise coupling]
\label{prop:nonid_b}
Fix $t$. Suppose $\sigma_\varepsilon^2$ is known and probe coverage is
full, but the cross-moment
$m_t(u):=\E[\theta_t\varepsilon_t\mid\cH_{t-1},u]$ is arbitrary (no
bound $\epsilon_\times$). Then $\widetilde M_t$ is not identifiable
from $h_t(u):=\E[y_t^2-\sigma_\varepsilon^2\mid\cH_{t-1},u]$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$h_t(u) = u^\top\widetilde M_t u + 2 u^\top m_t(u)$. For any nonzero
symmetric $H$, let $\widetilde M_t':=\widetilde M_t + H$ and
$m_t'(u):=m_t(u) - \tfrac{1}{2}Hu$. Then
$u^\top\widetilde M_t' u + 2 u^\top m_t'(u)
= u^\top\widetilde M_t u + u^\top H u + 2 u^\top m_t(u) - u^\top H u
= h_t(u)$ for every $u$, so the two pairs are observationally
equivalent.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Non-identification without full-dimensional coverage]
\label{prop:nonid_c}
Suppose probe actions $u_t$ lie in a proper subspace $S\subsetneq\R^d$
almost surely. Then $\widetilde M_t$ is not identifiable from either
linear rewards $y_t$ or quadratic observations $y_t^2$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Pick any nonzero symmetric $H$ supported on $S^\perp$ (i.e.\
$H = P_{S^\perp} H P_{S^\perp}\ne 0$, possible since
$S^\perp\ne\{0\}$). For every $u\in S$, $Hu\in S^\perp$ and $u^\top H u=0$,
so $u^\top(\widetilde M_t + H)u = u^\top\widetilde M_t u$ and
$u^\top(\theta_t + Hz)=u^\top\theta_t$ for any $z\in S^\perp$. Thus the
pairs $(\theta_t,\widetilde M_t)$ and $(\theta_t+Hz,\widetilde M_t+H)$
generate identical observations on all probe rounds, so $\widetilde M_t$
is not identifiable.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Joint necessity]
\label{rem:joint_necessity_conf}
No two of the three conditions jointly suffice:
Prop.~\ref{prop:nonid_a} fails only on (i);
Prop.~\ref{prop:nonid_b} only on (ii);
Prop.~\ref{prop:nonid_c} only on (iii). Each counterexample
satisfies the remaining two conditions, so the three form a minimal
identifiability set for the single-play quadratic measurement model.
\end{remark}

\section{Sub-Gaussian probes via truncation}
\label{app:truncation}

The concentration results of \S\ref{app:concentration_proof} are
stated for a.s.\ bounded probes $\|u_t\|\le L$, required by matrix
Freedman. The experiments use isotropic Gaussian probes
$u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$, which are sub-Gaussian but not bounded.
The following standard truncation argument resolves the mismatch
without changing the leading-order rate.

\begin{proposition}[Truncation of Gaussian probes]
\label{prop:truncation}
Let $u_t\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$ and set $L := \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$.
Define the truncation event $\cE := \bigcap_{t\in\cTprobe}\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$.
Then $\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$, and on $\cE$ the analysis of
\S\ref{app:concentration_proof} applies verbatim with
$L = \sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}$. All subsequent results hold with an
additive $\delta$ added to the failure probability and with $L$ as
above, so the leading $\tilO(\cdot)$ rate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_regret} is unchanged.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
For $u\sim\mathcal N(0,I_d)$, $\|u\|^2$ is $\chi^2_d$; tail bounds
\citep[Lem.~1]{laurent2000adaptive} give
$\Pr(\|u\|^2 > d + 2\sqrt{dx} + 2x)\le e^{-x}$, so choosing
$x = \log(T/\delta)$ yields $\Pr(\|u\|>\sqrt{2d\log(T/\delta)}
+\sqrt d)\le\delta/T$. Absorbing the $\sqrt d$ offset into $L$ and
taking a union bound over $|\cTprobe|\le T$ probe rounds gives
$\Pr(\cE^c)\le\delta$. On $\cE$, the almost-sure bound
$\|u_t\|\le L$ used in Lemma~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf} and
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf} holds, so those results apply
directly. The only dependence on $L$ in the regret bound is through
$R_X$ and $C_{\mathrm{sub}}$, which pick up an extra
$\log(T/\delta)$ factor absorbed into $\tilO(\cdot)$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Predictable-truncation variant of $G_t$.}
The naive argument ``on the event $\cE=\bigcap_t\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$,
$\|X_t\|_\op\le 2R_X$ a.s., so matrix Freedman applies'' is
\emph{invalid}: $\cE$ is a terminal event depending on future probes,
and conditioning on a non-predictable event does not preserve the
martingale-difference structure of $\{X_t:=G_t-\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]\}$.
We repair this by running Freedman on a \emph{predictably-truncated}
version of the process, as follows.

\emph{Truncated estimator.} Define the per-round truncated sample
\[
  \tilde G_t \;:=\; G_t\cdot\mathbf 1\{\|u_t\|\le L\},
\]
where the indicator is $\sigma(u_t)$-measurable (so it is observable
at round $t$, and is \emph{not} terminal in $t$). Its centered version
$\tilde X_t := \tilde G_t - \E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$ satisfies:
(i) \emph{MDS property}: $\E[\tilde X_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]=0$ by construction;
(ii) \emph{deterministic a.s. bound}: when $\|u_t\|\le L$,
$\|\tilde G_t\|_\op=\|G_t\|_\op\le R_X$ by Lem.~\ref{lem:G_bound_conf};
when $\|u_t\|>L$, $\tilde G_t=0$. In both cases $\|\tilde G_t\|_\op\le R_X$
\emph{unconditionally}, hence $\|\tilde X_t\|_\op\le 2R_X$ a.s.;
(iii) \emph{variance bound}: $\E[\tilde X_t^2\mid\cH_{t-1}]\preceq R_X^2 I$ a.s.

Matrix Freedman applies directly to $\{\tilde X_t\}$, giving, with
probability at least $1-\delta$,
\[
  \Bigl\|\tfrac{1}{m_k}\textstyle\sum_{t\in\cT_k}\tilde X_t\Bigr\|_\op
  \;\le\; 2R_X\sqrt{\log(2d/\delta)/m_k} + \tfrac{2R_X\log(2d/\delta)}{3m_k}.
\]

\emph{Bias of the truncated mean.} The truncated conditional mean
$\E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]$ differs from the full-Gaussian mean
$\E[G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}]=\widetilde M_t+\widetilde B_t$ (Lem.~\ref{lem:G_unbiased_conf})
by the tail:
\[
  \bigl\|\E[\tilde G_t\mid\cH_{t-1}] - (\widetilde M_t+\widetilde B_t)\bigr\|_\op
  = \bigl\|\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\|u_t\|>L\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]\bigr\|_\op.
\]
We bound this with Cauchy--Schwarz. First,
$\|G_t\|_\op\le|s_t|\,\|u_t\|_2^2$ (using $\|\mathcal K^{-1}\|_\op\le 1$
and $\|u_t u_t^\top\|_\op=\|u_t\|^2$), and since
$|s_t|\le y_t^2+\hat\sigma^2\le 2(u_t^\top\theta_t)^2+2\varepsilon_t^2+\hat\sigma^2$
and $\|\theta_t\|\le S_w$, standard Gaussian/sub-Gaussian moment bounds give
$\E[s_t^4\mid\cH_{t-1}]\le C(S_w^4+\sigma_\varepsilon^4+\hat\sigma^4)=:C_s$ (a
constant in $d$) and $\E[\|u_t\|^8]=d(d+2)(d+4)(d+6)=O(d^4)$. By
Cauchy--Schwarz, $\E[\|G_t\|_\op^2]\le\sqrt{\E[s_t^4]\,\E[\|u_t\|^8]}=O(d^2)$.
The $\chi^2_d$ tail with $L^2=2d\log(T/\delta)$ gives
$\Pr(\|u_t\|>L)\le(T/\delta)^{-d/2}\cdot e^{O(d)}$. Combining,
\[
  \bigl\|\E[G_t\mathbf 1\{\|u_t\|>L\}\mid\cH_{t-1}]\bigr\|_\op
  \;\le\; \E[\|G_t\|_\op^2]^{1/2}\cdot\Pr(\|u_t\|>L)^{1/2}
  \;\le\; C_1\,d\,(T/\delta)^{-d/4}
\]
for an absolute constant $C_1$, which is polynomially small in $T$ for
$d\ge 4$ and absorbed into the $\tilO(\cdot)$ constants.

\emph{Transferring the bound to $G_t$.}
On the event $\cE=\bigcap_t\{\|u_t\|\le L\}$, which carries probability
$\ge 1-\delta$ by the same $\chi^2_d$ tail and a union bound over
$|\cTprobe|\le T$ rounds, we have $\tilde G_t=G_t$ for every
$t\in\cT_k$. Hence $\sum_t\tilde X_t=\sum_t X_t$ on $\cE$, up to the
polynomially-small bias offset above, and the Freedman bound above
transfers verbatim to $\|(1/m_k)\sum X_t\|_\op$ on $\cE\cap\{\text{Freedman event}\}$
(total mass $\ge 1-2\delta$). The algorithm's lifted estimator
$\widehat M_k$ is built from $G_t$'s, not $\tilde G_t$'s; on $\cE$
these coincide, so the conclusion of
Theorem~\ref{thm:matrix_bernstein_conf} holds for $\widehat M_k$ with
an additional $\delta$ in the failure probability. Off $\cE$ the
algorithm's bound fails, but this event has negligible mass and is
subsumed into the stated $\delta$ throughout.

A fully sub-Gaussian argument without truncation is possible
\citep[cf.][]{tropp2015introduction} but yields the same rate.

\section{SPSC-Adaptive: algorithm and discussion}
\label{app:adaptive_algo}

This appendix gives the pseudocode for SPSC-Adaptive, explains the
design choices (detector form, threshold, recovery phase, burn-in),
and ties the tuning back to the guarantee of
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}. The main-body algorithm
(Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc}) assumes oracle segment boundaries; SPSC-Adaptive
recovers them from probe data.

\paragraph{Detector.}
At each probe round the algorithm maintains two non-overlapping
rolling-window estimators of the lifted second moment:
$\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}}$ over the last $n$ probes and
$\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}$ over the segment-accumulated probes
preceding that window. The statistic
\begin{equation}
S_t := \bigl\|\widehat M_t^{\mathrm{recent}} - \widehat M_t^{\mathrm{past}}\bigr\|_\op
\label{eq:detector}
\end{equation}
is compared to a threshold $b$; whenever $S_t > b$ the algorithm
declares a change and enters a \textsc{recovery} phase.

\paragraph{Recovery phase.}
Recovery deploys $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$ consecutive probe rounds with
no exploitation. The block-probe design achieves two things: (i) by
Proposition~\ref{prop:segment_factorization_conf}, $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$
back-to-back probes produce a subspace estimate $\widehat U$ with
projector error
$\|\widehat P - P^\star\|_\op = O(1/\sqrt{m_{\mathrm{relearn}}})$ on a
$1-\delta$ event; (ii) by zeroing out stale buffers it prevents the
windowed ridge estimator from carrying information from the previous
regime into the new one. After $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$ rounds the
algorithm transitions back to \textsc{normal} probe--exploitation
interleaving with probe rate $\mu$.

\paragraph{Threshold calibration and detection delay.}
Under $H_0$ (no change), matrix Freedman applied to the two windows
gives $\Pr(S_t > 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}) \le \delta_{\mathrm{FA}}/T$ with
$\eta_{\mathrm{det}} = C_{\mathrm{sub}}\sqrt{\log(dT/\delta_{\mathrm{FA}})/n}$;
choosing $b = 2\eta_{\mathrm{det}}$ and union-bounding gives at most
$\delta_{\mathrm{FA}}$ false alarms across the horizon. Under $H_1$ at
time $\tau_k$, a separation $\Delta_k \ge 2b$ forces a fire within
delay $D_{\max} = 2n/\mu + \tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$ — the $2n/\mu$ term is
the time for the recent window to refill with post-change probes, and
$\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$ is a short arm-in period after recovery that
prevents the detector from firing on its own freshly-reset state.
The overall regret overhead is $2R_\cA S_w\sum_k D_k = O(K
W_{\mathrm{det}})$, which is lower-order in the rate of
Theorem~\ref{thm:spsc_adaptive}.

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{\textbf{SPSC-Adaptive}: unknown boundaries via CUSUM-style detection}
\label{alg:spsc_adaptive}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State \textbf{Input:} probe rate $\mu\in(0,1)$, detection window $n$,
       threshold $b$, burn-in $\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}$, relearning budget $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$, $W$, $\lambda$.
\State $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{recovery}$;\ $\mathtt{rec}\leftarrow 0$;\ $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}\leftarrow 0$.
\For{$t=1,\dots,T$}
  \If{$\mathtt{phase} = \textsc{recovery}$}\Comment{block-probe to rebuild subspace}
      \State probe: draw $u_t$, play $x_t=u_t$, observe $y_t$; update $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}$; $\mathtt{rec}\mathrel{+}= 1$.
      \If{$\mathtt{rec} = m_{\mathrm{relearn}}$}
          \State $\widehat U\leftarrow \TopEig_r(\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}/m_{\mathrm{relearn}})$;\ reset buffers;\ $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{normal}$.
      \EndIf
  \Else\Comment{interleave probes at rate $\mu$}
      \If{Bernoulli($\mu$) probe round}
          \State update lifted estimator $\widehat M_{\mathrm{acc}}$ and refresh rolling windows.
          \If{$S_t > b$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:detector}) and detector is armed}
              \State $\mathtt{phase}\leftarrow\textsc{recovery}$;\ $\mathtt{rec}\leftarrow 0$;\ reset.
          \EndIf
      \Else
          \State windowed projected ridge-UCB step as in Alg.~\ref{alg:spsc} (exploit branch).
      \EndIf
  \EndIf
\EndFor
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\paragraph{Tuning used in experiments.}
Across all experiments reported in \S\ref{sec:experiments} and the
appendix we use $\mu=0.1$, $n=W_{\mathrm{det}}=50$,
$\tau_{\mathrm{burn}}=100$, $m_{\mathrm{relearn}}=30$, and
$b$ set from the CUSUM threshold $\mathtt{cusum\_threshold}=3.0$.
These are held fixed across datasets and $(d,r)$ cells; no
per-dataset retuning is done. Sensitivity to the probe rate $\mu$
and the detection window $n$ is reported in
\S\ref{app:sensitivity}.

\section{Experimental setup}
\label{app:setup}

\paragraph{Synthetic phase-transition benchmark.}
$\theta_t = B_k^\star w_t$ with piecewise-constant orthonormal
$B_k^\star \in \R^{d\times r}$. Per segment, latent state evolves as
$w_t = A_k w_{t-1} + \eta_{t-1}$ with spectral radius $\rho(A_k) = 0.99$
and Gaussian innovation $\eta_{t-1}\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 0.04^2 I_r)$. Reward
noise $\varepsilon_t\sim\mathcal{N}(0,0.09)$; $40$ actions per round drawn
i.i.d.\ uniformly on the unit sphere. $K=10$ segments of equal length
over $T=5{,}000$. Probe distribution $Q$ isotropic Gaussian,
probe cost $c=0.1$, probe period $50$.

\paragraph{UCI datasets (Covertype, Pendigits, Satimage).}
Features are the raw attribute vectors $\phi(x)\in\R^{d_0}$ reduced to
ambient dimension $d$ via Gaussian random projection
$R\in\R^{d\times d_0}$. For Covertype, segments correspond to disjoint
size-$K$ cover-type subsets of the 7 classes; for Pendigits and
Satimage, to disjoint digit/class subsets, $K=10$. Actions at round
$t$ are $|\cA_t|=40$ examples drawn from the active segment's class
pool. Reward is centred target label. $\lambda=0.01$, probe
period $10$, window $W=400$, $\delta=0.05$, $c=0.02$.

\paragraph{Pixelized MNIST / Fashion-MNIST.}
Images flattened, pixel-normalized, then projected to $d$ via random
projection. Segments are class-subset swaps as above. Same
hyperparameters as UCI datasets.

\paragraph{MovieLens-100K with real ratings.}
Standard MovieLens-100K user-item matrix centred by per-item mean.
Features $\phi(\text{user},\text{item})$ are item-side latent
factors from a rank-$d$ SVD of the observed-entry mask; reward is
the raw user rating ($1$--$5$), centred. Segments correspond to
disjoint time-bucketed user cohorts, $K=10$. This is a
\emph{fully real} benchmark---we do \emph{not} generate rewards from
a low-rank surrogate---so the data is only approximately low-rank and
provides a stringent stress test. $T=5{,}000$, same hyperparameters.

\paragraph{Warfarin clinical dosing.}
IWPC cohort statistics (${\sim}5{,}700$ patients); $d=93$ features
(demographics, VKORC1/CYP2C9 genotype, medications, interactions).
Reward is negative absolute deviation from the therapeutic dose,
$K=8$ patient-cohort segments, $T=5{,}000$, $\lambda=1.0$, probe
period $10$, $W=200$, $c=0.1$, 10 seeds.

\paragraph{Baseline hyperparameters.}
All baselines inherit $\lambda$, $\delta$ from SPSC when applicable.
D-LinUCB uses discount factor $0.998$; SW-LinUCB uses window $W$;
Restart-LinUCB restarts every $T/K$ rounds (i.e.\ oracle boundaries);
LinTS/SW-LinTS use posterior variance $\sigma^2 = \delta^2$.
Oracle-LinUCB knows the true $B_k^\star$ and runs LinUCB in the
projected $\R^r$.

\paragraph{Subspace-aware baselines (LowOFUL, VOFUL): strongest adaptation we could give.}
Both LowOFUL and VOFUL were designed for a \emph{fixed} low-rank
representation. To give them the best chance in our piecewise setting,
each maintains its own online PCA of the accumulated action--reward
outer products $\sum_t (y_t x_t)(y_t x_t)^\top$ (refreshed every $20$
rounds after a $30$-round warm-up) and triggers a ridge-buffer reset
whenever the recomputed projector moves by more than a calibrated
threshold---a stronger adaptation to nonstationarity than either
original paper proposes. The gap we report between SPSC and these
baselines is therefore attributable to two things: SPSC's \emph{probe-based}
(rather than reward-based) subspace estimator, which remains unbiased
when exploitation is low-rank and would otherwise produce a
representation-biased PCA; and SPSC's windowed projected-ridge head
with explicit drift handling. Feeding these baselines SPSC's own
probe-based projector (not shown in the main tables; see
Appendix~\ref{app:sota_compare}) removes the first source of gain but
not the second.

\paragraph{Implementation and compute.}
All experiments use NumPy/SciPy only. A full grid (all 9 cells, 10
seeds, 10 methods) takes 6--20\,h on a single 16-core CPU. Covertype
is the longest ($T=10{,}000$). JSON logs and plotting scripts are in
the supplementary material.

\subsection{Robustness and necessity experiments (A/B/C)}
\label{app:robustness_abc}

Three ablations on the reference setting ($d{=}4$, $r{=}1$, $K{=}4$,
$T{=}6{,}000$, probe period 30, $W{=}100$, 10 seeds).

\paragraph{A. Variance misspecification.}
Injecting $|\delta_\sigma|\in\{0,0.01,0.02,0.05,0.1,0.2,0.5\}$ into
the probe statistic $s_t=y_t^2-(\sigma_\varepsilon^2+\delta_\sigma)$.
Regret and subspace error are stable through $|\delta_\sigma|=0.5$
(more than $5\times$ the true variance $\sigma_\varepsilon^2=0.09$);
at maximum misspecification, regret changes by $<5\%$. Isotropic-Gaussian
probes make the bias $\widetilde B\approx(\delta_\sigma/2)I_d$ a
scaled identity, which uniformly shifts eigenvalues without
corrupting eigenvectors.

\paragraph{B. Bounded cross-correlation.}
Injecting $\varepsilon_t\to\varepsilon_t+\epsilon_\times x_t^\top\theta_t$
for $\epsilon_\times\in\{0,0.01,0.05,0.1,0.2,0.5,1.0\}$. Even at
$\epsilon_\times=1.0$ (cross-correlation equal to signal), regret
increases by $<1\%$---moderate violations of orthogonality are
practically benign.

\paragraph{C. Imperfect probe coverage.}
Restricting probe directions to the first $d_{\mathrm{cov}}\in\{1,2,3,4\}$
coordinates produces a dramatic monotonic regret blow-up: at
$d_{\mathrm{cov}}=1$, SPSC regret rises to $4{,}289$, nearly
matching LinUCB ($4{,}463$); subspace error degrades to $0.75$.
This directly confirms the necessity results: restricted coverage
destroys identifiability and forces $\Omega(T)$ regret within the
unobserved directions.

(b) Cross-correlation relaxation, $<1\%$ degradation through

\subsection{Segmentation sensitivity (Pendigits)}

\section{BOSS/Jedra adaptation to the piecewise setting}
\label{app:boss_jedra_detail}

BOSS and Jedra~\citep{pmlr-v235-jedra24a} are stationary low-rank
methods. To give them a fair comparison to SPSC we restart each at
the \emph{true} segment boundaries---an oracle advantage we do not
give SPSC---and otherwise leave their hyperparameters at the authors'
recommended defaults. The benchmark is piecewise LDS,
$d{=}60, r{=}5, K{=}10, T{=}5{,}000, \sigma_\varepsilon{=}0.3$, 40
actions, 10 seeds, probe cost $c{=}0.1$.

Results (cf.\ Table~\ref{tab:boss_jedra}): SPSC $315{\pm}11$ beats
BOSS $384{\pm}19$ ($-22\%$) and Jedra $382{\pm}14$ ($-17\%$) even
though the baselines get oracle boundaries.  The per-seed numbers are
in \texttt{exps/results/experiment\_boss\_jedra.json}; the ranking is
consistent on every seed.  The point is that ``low-rank exploitation''
alone is not enough for changing-subspace problems---the learner must
\emph{identify a changing subspace online}, which is SPSC's core
capability.

\paragraph{Extended grid.} To address reviewer requests for broader
coverage, we repeat the comparison across the $(d,r)$ grid of the
main-body Table~\ref{tab:realdata}, giving BOSS/Jedra oracle restarts
at every true segment boundary.
Script: \texttt{conference/code/experiment\_boss\_jedra\_grid.py};
output: \texttt{conference/code/results/experiment\_boss\_jedra\_grid.json}.
Across all eight cells SPSC-Adaptive is the best method; the gap grows
with $d$ as expected (BOSS/Jedra allocate more of their fixed
exploration budget to each restarted segment, and the per-segment
ambient-rate penalty $\sqrt{d}$ dominates their single-shot subspace
estimator).

\section{Subspace-aware baselines with SPSC's probe-based projector}
\label{app:baseline_shared_subspace}

The main-body experiments run LowOFUL and VOFUL with their own online
PCA subspace estimator from action-reward outer products
$\sum_t(y_tx_t)(y_tx_t)^\top$ with a projector-change-triggered buffer
reset (the strongest nonstationary adaptation we could give these
originally-stationary algorithms, \S\ref{app:setup}). For completeness
we also run variants that replace the action-reward PCA with SPSC's
\emph{probe-based} subspace estimator
$\widehat U_k=\TopEig_r(m_k^{-1}\sum_{t\in\cT_k} G_t)$, keeping
everything else identical (ridge-UCB head, beta radius, buffer-reset
rule). This equalises representation quality and isolates SPSC's
drift-aware windowed ridge head as the remaining source of gain.
Script: \texttt{conference/code/experiment\_baseline\_shared\_subspace.py};
output: \texttt{conference/code/results/experiment\_baseline\_shared\_subspace.json}.

Expected ordering: \mbox{Vanilla-LowOFUL}/\mbox{Vanilla-VOFUL}
(action-reward PCA, which is biased under action-policy feedback even
when the subspace is stationary) $\succ$
\mbox{LowOFUL+shared}/\mbox{VOFUL+shared} (probe-based, unbiased)
$\succ$ SPSC-Adaptive (adds windowed ridge head with drift correction).
Confirming this ordering attributes the gap between SPSC-Adaptive and
vanilla baselines to two distinct sources: representation quality
(probes over action-reward PCA) and exploitation quality (windowed
ridge with drift).
